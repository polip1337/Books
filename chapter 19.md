Chapter 19
Zack sprinted into the tunnel, his heart pounding against his chest as the first spear whistled past his head. It shattered against the wall, spraying shards that nicked his cheek. He didn’t slow down. Behind him, the clamor of footsteps and guttural roars grew louder—some of the Icemen from the street had broken off their fight to pursue. Nyx clung to his shoulder, her claws digging in holding for dear life.

A faint glow shimmered behind them, and a barrier materialized just as another spear flew. It struck Nyx’s shield with a crack, the impact reverberating through the air. Nyx hissed, her tail lashing. “My mana’s not infinite!”

“Bill me later,” Zack grunted, ducking under a low overhang. 

The tunnel sloped upward along the tower’s inner wall, its surface smooth but littered with debris—crushed barricades of ice, their edges jagged where something had smashed through. No Icemen stood guard here, only the remnants of their defenses, abandoned and silent. He vaulted over a toppled slab, feeling the air grow colder, a heavy weight pressing against his chest. The crystal was getting closer—he could sense it, a faint hum pulsing through the walls.

The tunnel twisted, narrowing as it climbed. Zack’s breath fogged, the chill biting deeper into his lungs. Behind, the pursuit thundered closer—heavy feet thumping the ice, punctuated by the occasional crash of a spear against Nyx’s shields. She summoned another, her glow dimming slightly. “They are faster than they look,” another spear fell short of them. “And persistent.”

Zack rounded a bend. The tunnel opened slightly, revealing a shattered watchpost—its ice walls collapsed inward, a lone spear embedded in the floor. He darted past, his legs burning from the incline. The hum grew stronger, vibrating in his bones, urging him onward.

A spear grazed his arm, tearing his sleeve and drawing blood. Nyx reacted instantly, her shield flaring to block the next one. Zack gritted his teeth, pushing harder. The tunnel leveled out ahead, a faint light spilling from an archway—the second floor. He lunged for it, diving through as Nyx’s last shield shattered under a barrage of ice.

They landed hard on the other side. Zack scrambled to his feet, pulling Nyx with him as he turned into a narrow alleyway. As he ran, picking the turns randomly, the sounds of pursuit faded, the attackers staying further and further behind. 


“Guess they’re not fast enough,” Nyx said, as she shook out her wings. “You’re welcome, by the way. Saved your sorry hide again.”

Zack exhaled, wiping blood from his arm. “Yeah, you’re a real hero. Its going to get real old real fast if you keep asking for praise after each fight.”

“I could always let a spear slip by”


Zack just snorted, and fought down the need to pat the little lizard's head. He stepped deeper into the alley, the second floor unfolding around them. The whole place felt different—quieter, like the city itself was holding its breath. The buildings here weren’t shattered ruins but intact, their ice walls still standing, their doorways yawning open like empty mouths. Zack kept his pace steady, his breath fogging in the cold air. No bodies, no weapons left behind, just the thin layer of what looked like frost dust covering everything. There were weird tools scattered around the streets, often next to piles of clothes. Down below, the Icemen had fought and died. Up here, everything simply ... stopped.

After they encountered the first patrol of Icemen they stuck to the alleys, avoiding open spaces where patrols might spot them. The hum of the crystal lingered, a constant pull guiding Zack upward. He paused at a corner, peering into a broad plaza. Rectangular slabs lined the edges, their surfaces sculpted from ice—smooth counters, shelves etched with faint patterns, and basins that might have held goods. No dust coated them, only a thin layer of frost, as if time had paused here.

“Market,” Zack murmured, stepping forward. Zack ran a hand over the counters, feeling grooves of carvings—spirals and jagged lines, like the script he’d seen in the hut. The stalls were arranged neatly, with carved basins that looked like they should be filled with something, but weren’t. “They traded here.”

The Icemen hadn’t just built walls to keep things out. They had built something they thought would last. And now, all that was left of them was a market full of empty stalls and a silence thick enough to feel.

Nyx fluttered to a shelf, sniffing at a shallow bowl. “What, ice cubes?” She poked the bowl with her claw, and it chimed softly, the sound echoing through the plaza. “Huh. Sounds like glass.”
Zack moved to another stall, his eyes catching on a row of small, oval-shaped objects. He picked one up, expecting smooth ice, but the surface was ridged—layered, almost organic. Turning it over in his palm, he realized it wasn’t just a figurine. It was a vertebra. Small, polished, but unmistakably a piece of something’s spine. Tiny carvings spiraled along its surface, intricate patterns cut so precisely that even the grooves felt deliberate. Not decoration. Writing.
Nyx fluttered to his shoulder, peering down at it. “Please tell me that’s not what I think it is.”
Zack exhaled through his nose, weighing the small bone in his palm. It wasn’t brittle, wasn’t crumbling—it had been preserved, hardened in ice. Maybe as a keepsake. Maybe as a warning. He set it back with the others, suddenly aware of how many there were.
 “It's a bone. They lived here for a while, they built a society.” 

Nyx landed beside him, her amber eyes scanning the figurine. “Yeah, till it all went to hell. Didn’t your dream give you a sense of time? Like did they defend for months or years?” She asked.

“Nothing specific. But I saw them building stuff and judging from the pace and the size of this place I lean towards years. Possibly decades”

Nyx’s tone softened as she glanced around. “This place… it’s got a vibe. Like it remembers being alive.” 

Zack turned his attention to the table itself. His reflection warping in the ice, his eyes staring back distorted, unfamiliar. The carvings on the counter deepened under his gaze, their spirals tightening into knots that suggested movement, as if the ice itself remembered the hands that shaped it. Somewhere beyond the plaza, a hollow moan drifted through the streets—he hoped it was the wind.

Zack set the oval down, his gaze drifting across the market. The stalls stretched in orderly rows, their layout planned. Beyond them, a larger structure loomed—its walls taller, its roof a dome of translucent ice. A temple, maybe. At the very least something important. He nodded toward it. “Let’s check that out.”

They crossed the plaza, the silence pressing in. He passed a stall tipped on its side, its basin split open, spilling a trail of frost that glittered like shattered glass. Nyx kept silent as she darted between stalls, exploring.
The temple loomed closer, its dome an eye staring down at them. The streets narrowed as they approached, the buildings crowding in, their ice walls etched with faint scratches—claw marks, perhaps. Zack’s steps moved a patch of ice dust, revealing a darker stain beneath—black and viscous, frozen. It was Jellyfish ichor. 

The temple’s entrance was unguarded, its double doors ajar. Zack pushed one open, the ice creaking under his touch. Inside, the air was thick, colder than the streets.The ice dust on the floor was in constant motion; it traced patterns—faint, spiraling paths that led who knows where.  The interior was vast, its ceiling soaring high, supported by pillars carved with the same spiraling patterns. At the far end, an altar stood—simple, unadorned, but radiating a faint glow.

Nyx fluttered ahead, her wings stirring the dust into faint swirls. “Creepy,” she muttered, landing on a pillar. “Feels like a tomb.”

Zack approached the altar, his steps slow. The glow intensified as he neared, casting shadows that stretched too far, curling around the pillars like tendrils. He stopped short—a figure slumped behind it, its crystalline form flickering weakly. An Iceman, smaller than the ones he’d fought, its body free of the corrupted veins that marred the others. A robe that flowed like silk covered his body. Held together by an oval similar to the one he saw on the market. Its fissures pulsed faintly, a dim blue light struggling to hold on.

The Iceman shifted, its head tilting toward him. A soft tinkling filled the air—like crystals clashing in a breeze—and Zack understood it, the sound forming words in his mind. “Flesh-warm… you trespass… yet hear.”

Zack knelt, keeping his distance but meeting its gaze. “I’m not here to fight. What happened to you?”

The Iceman’s fissures flared briefly, the tinkling sharpening. “Once… we sing through crystal… one mind… one will. Tower rise… shield heart-pulse. Essence… it breathe… it bind us.” Its voice was disjointed, alien, each phrase a shard of thought. “We rule… through hum… no discord… only unity.”

Nyx edged closer, her tail still. “The crystal,” she said softly. “It’s what kept you together?”

The Iceman’s head tilted, the sound slowing. “Yes… small-wing. Heart-pulse… sustain realm… weave us. No single… all whole. Sanctuary… we build… to cradle it.” It paused, its glow dimming. “Then… shadow creep. Soft-things… break rhythm. We fight… we climb… but corruption… seep.”

Zack leaned in, his voice steady. “Corruption? In the crystal?”

The Iceman’s fissures pulsed once, bright and sharp. “Yes… flesh-warm. Heart-pulse… twist. It call them…. We shield it… yet it rot. Beware… what you seek… may unmake.” Its form shuddered, the tinkling fading to a whisper. “I… last… pure. Others… lost.”

Before Zack could respond, the Iceman’s glow faded, a soft chime echoing as it shattered into the same ice dust covering the city. Zack stared at the spot, his chest tight. All the dust around the city... 

Nyx fluttered to his shoulder, her voice low. “That’s a hell of a warning. Still wanna go up?”

Zack stood, his fists clenching. “We don’t have a choice. Rei needs it. But now I know what we’re up against.” He turned to the altar, the hum of the crystal stronger now, pulling him higher. “It’s not just about getting it—it’s about fixing it. Or stopping it.”

Nyx snorted, though her tone lacked its usual bite. “Big hero vibes, huh? Fine, let’s save the world or whatever. But if I get trauma from this, I’m blaming you.”

Zack smirked, stepping toward the temple’s rear exit. “Deal.”
