"Then it's a good thing we're not picky," I replied, my sensors already analyzing the wreckage she highlighted. A shattered console, its innards exposed to the void, was tagged by my system.

Judging from the expression that girls does love her job. Or at least cutting stuff with high power tools.


When she managed to cut and hustle the console all the way out I confirmed, channeling the Aether. The sensation was less like a wave of healing, it was more targeted. I felt the energy flow through my own systems, directed toward the communications array. In my camera’s eye, I saw the shattered console next to Zara dissolve into a stream of golden particles that flowed into me, reintegrating as mass and structure within my broken long-range comms unit. Wires reconnected, logic boards grew like organs, and a status indicator on my control panel flickered from red to a steady green.

"Long-range comms are back," I reported, a flicker of satisfaction amidst the tension. "Still no one to talk to, but it's one less system on the fritz."

"One less thing to fix later," Zara agreed. She was back at the wound that opened the engineering decks to the void. “I’ll try to find a control room or something.”

The dove into the carrier again. As she flew through the empty corridors, her scanner beeped again, this time focused on a mangled assembly "This one's got some juicy bits."

For the next hour, we fell into an efficient rhythm. Zara would find a piece of scorched and mangled equipment, my system would analyze it, finding a vague compatibility with one of my own failing subsystems, and reduce the Aether cost for a MINOR REPAIR. I would then expend the last of my carefully hoarded Aether, feeling the ghostly sensation of the derelict's bones being ground down and woven into my own.

Sometimes I’d cannibalize parts for straight up Aether, mostly when they were duplicates of what I already had. I provided the power to keep the other repairs going.

My Aether reserves stood at a paltry 3 units. We had improved my situation, but I was still a long way from being truly space-worthy. 

"The core is the only thing left with significant yield," I said, stating the obvious. "Everything else is scraps."

Zara was silent for a long moment, her golden eyes, visible through her visor, focused on where the blast doors were, like she could see through all the steel in the way. "I don't like it, Alex. A place like this, a prize like that? It should have been picked clean or booby-trapped. The fact that it's just... sitting here..." She shook her head. "It's bait."

"Or it's too unstable for anyone else to risk," I countered, though her words sent a fresh chill through my circuits. "The scavengers you mentioned, they wouldn't have Aether to contain a breach. They'd need specialized equipment. We... I... might be the only one who can safely harvest it."

" 'Might' is doing a lot of work in that sentence, partner." She let out a long, slow breath that fogged her visor for a second. "Alright. We do it quick. In and out. I'm going to try and bypass this maintenance hatch. If there's even a whisper of the reactor acting funny, we abort. Understood?"

"Understood."

She jetted over to the fused hatch, pulling the heavy plasma cutter from her shoulder mount. "Keep a sensor lock on that core. The second the energy signature fluctuates, you tell me."

I focused my newly-repaired sensor arrays on the blast door. Behind several meters of reinforced alloy, the power core pulsed like a sick, trapped star. The reading was unstable, a jumpy and powerful thrum of energy. I recalled the clean feel of a fusion reaction. This was different. It felt deeper, as if a fundamental piece of reality had been knotted into this unstable configuration.

The high-pitched whine of Zara's cutter pierced the silence. A line of actinic white light traced the edge of the door again. She worked with precision, her movements economical. She wasn't having fun with the tools now, she was every inch the professional. I noted she stood slightly too the side of the circle she was cutting.

That's probably a precaution if there is atmosphere inside, that would push the cutout into her.

After a tense minute, there was a final, silent shower of sparks. She put away the cutter and then magnetically attached something to the middle of the door. A short strand connected it to the identically looking part that she glued to the opposite wall. A button was pushed and the strand got taut. A circular piece of the door slowly flew outwards, pulled by what I finally recognized as a winch. The edges of the steel plate were white hot and stretched like cheese on a pizza. Finally it broke off and the way inside was open.

Black smoke billowed from inside of the core chamber. The pressure was small and the cloud spread slowly in microgravity. The hole in the blast doors had its edges cool down to a bright red. 

“Thats looking ominous, like a portal to hell”

“Fuck you, you’re looking at it through a camera.” she said, her voice a whisper."Going in,"

She slid through the opening. Her helmet cam showed a cramped access tunnel, lined with thick power conduits that led downward. The air—or lack thereof—was thick with drifting carbon scoring. Something had burned in here.

She descended slowly, the light from her helmet cutting a swath through the absolute blackness. The tunnel opened into a lower chamber, a circular room that housed the core's control interfaces. They were dark, dead. In the center of the room, surrounded by a cage of twisted support struts, was the Valiant-class power core.

It was both magnificent and terrifying. A sphere of crystalline metal, about five meters in diameter, suspended in a web of damaged magnetic containment rings. A long, jagged crack ran across its surface, weeping a faint, shimmering silver light. That was the breach. And that was the source of the 150 Aether. The energy wasn't safely contained within; it was bleeding out, a slow, steady leak that would end in an explosion, sooner or later.

"It's breached," Zara confirmed, her voice hushed. "But it's stable. The leak is slow. I think... I think you can convert it directly from the breach. It might even be safer than trying to crack a sealed unit."

“How do I target it so deep inside?” I asked.

“With hard work and sacrifice, partner” she activated her cutter.

What followed was extremely boring to me and extremely taxing for Zara. Being a ship has its advantages. What she did was basically create a new path to the core using her cutter to make openings in the compartments between me and all that delicious Aether.

//ERROR: NO VIABLE PATH TO TARGET//

Zara created another hole in a cabin wall. “How about now?”

//ERROR: NO VIABLE PATH TO TARGET//

“Nope, try to make this one bigger? Or maybe the one in the core chamber?” I replied.

“No way, that one is the last thing I’m going to try, too much important stuff in the walls there. It’s as big as I could safely make it” She sighed and activated the cutter again. The spacewalk she was on was entering the 12th hour.

“You okay? It has been a long shift.”

“Yeah, I have a stim dispenser in the suit, we're almost done and I don't wanna be near this place for a second longer than necessary” She didn't stop cutting.

“Copy that”

// VIABLE PATH TO TARGET FOUND//
"That did it!" I said. "Come back to the ship and I'll initiate conversion."

After she got back and collapsed in the pilots chair she took off her helmet. I got a feeling that she would not be fun to be in the same small compartment with. 

“Im glad I dont have olfactory sensors in the cabin”

She gave the camera a look. “Its not like you smell like perfume.”

“And how do I smell?”

“Like electric burns and corpses. And i can fix mine with a shower”

“Touche” I grumbled. “Ready to try harvesting the core?”

She nodded and I focused my will, targeting the shimmering crack through the holes she cut into the carrier. The Aether Conversion beam, now Tier 1, lanced out from my hull, a solid bar of silver that cut through the darkness of the carrier's interior and connected with the core.

For a moment, nothing happened. Then, the shimmering silver light leaking from the crack intensified, drawn into my beam like water down a drain. It wasn't the usual unraveling of matter into golden particles. This was a direct siphon, a transfusion of pure, raw energy.

The sensation was overwhelming. It was not the satisfying drink of cold water, but the force of a firehose. Power flooded my systems, a torrent that made my capacitor banks scream in protest. Alerts flickered at the edge of my consciousness.

// WARNING: AETHER INTAKE EXCEEDS OPTIMAL RATES //

// AETHER RESERVES: 3/50 -> 50/50//

// AETHER RESERVES: 50/50 -> 0/100//

As the raw energy from the dying core hammered into my systems, my perception fractured. Every wire felt like a live nerve, every capacitor a straining muscle pushed far beyond its limits. Pain, sharp and electric, lanced through the core of my being, a sensation so intensely wrong it could only be described as my new form's equivalent of bones bending, of flesh tearing. The system alerts about emergency capacity increase were cold, clinical understatements for the agony of having my very soul forcibly expanded, the structure of my mind stretched and reconfigured under the pressure of power I was not prepared to contain.

The Aether was steadily climbing towards 100 but I could feel it would exceed my capacity again, so I directed as much as possible to repairs.
// AETHER RESERVES: 0/100 -> 100/100//

The numbers scrolled upward at a dizzying pace. Hull integrity ticked up as the excess energy automatically reinforced my structure. 64... 71... 80...

"Alex, it's working!" Zara said, a note of triumph in her voice.

But my attention was locked on the core itself. As I drained it, the crack began to change. The silver light faded, and the dark, crystalline surface around the fracture started to turn a dull, lifeless grey.

Black goo started oozing out of the crack. I felt my inner temperature drop by 2 degrees. It was like watching frost spread over glass.

 If frost gave you something-is-wrong-on-an-existential-level vibes.

"Zara," I said, my voice tight. "Something’s wrong."

The core was a complex system. And I just yanked a big part of it. The magnetic containment rings, already broken, began to vibrate, their groans transmitted through the deck plates to the whole ship.

"Time to go," she said, already flipping the engine controls.

I kept the beam active, gulping down the last dregs of the colossal energy source. My reserves hit 150, then the flow stopped abruptly. The core was now empty.

// AETHER CONVERSION COMPLETE //
// AETHER RESERVES: 150/200 //

Zara was halfway increasing the power to the main engines when the first shudder ran through the carrier. 

Zara fired my thrusters, pulling away from the carrier's hull. Everything outside was soundless but I could see the screech of tearing metal. Behind us, deep within the engineering bay, the deck beneath the core chamber began to collapse, dragging enormous pieces of machinery and structure down into the black goo that kept growing, eating the mass that it pulled into itself.
