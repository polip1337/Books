Chapter 14
Zack couldn’t see far through the rain and what he saw was not filling him with optimism. The landscape was rugged and desolate, sometimes he thought he could see distant peaks. The crystal was probably on one of them. A harsh wind howled around him, but like the rain, it seemed frozen in time, its sound distorted.
Zack picked up his backpack, ensuring the egg within was secure. He had no idea which direction to go, but standing still wasn’t an option.
Navigating the terrain was grueling. The ground was uneven, with loose rocks and cracks that threatened to twist his ankles with every step. The rain turned from beautiful to unnerving in his mind. Each droplet seemed like a thousand unblinking eyes, watching his every move.
After what felt like hours, Zack spotted the dark mouth of a cave nestled against a craggy hillside. Relief washed over him as he approached. The cave was shallow but dry, its entrance wide enough to offer some protection from the elements—not that the frozen rain seemed to be falling.
He set down his backpack carefully, pulling out the egg. Its fiery veins pulsed faintly, it was fine. Maybe they are a bit wider? Zack thought to himself. Zack placed it on a bed of moss near the wall, then sat down with a heavy sigh.
“Alright, let’s see how I’m doing,” he said, summoning his status screen. The familiar translucent interface appeared, casting a soft glow in the dim cave.

Level: 7
Attributes:

He lingered on the description of Inferno Step:
Inferno Step (Level 1):
Dash forward with explosive speed, leaving a fiery trail that damages enemies and ignites flammable surfaces. The distance and intensity scale with Dexterity and Intelligence.
“A mobility skill,” he mused. “That’ll be useful.”
Satisfied for now, Zack leaned back against the cave wall. The exhaustion of all the recent fighting and travel caught up with him, and despite the unease gnawing at his mind, he drifted into a fitful sleep.
Zack woke with a start. The cave was silent, but something felt different. He stepped outside, squinting against the dim light. The air seemed less dense somehow.
Zack packed up and set off, hopefully in the same direction as before. He paid attention to his footing mostly so it took him a while to notice the rain was thinning out, the droplets spaced farther apart.
Curious He thought but he didn’t think about it any more.

As he reached the top of another rocky peak, Zack’s eyes caught something in the distance. The land seemed to… end. The wasteland gave way to a black void, an expanse of nothingness that swallowed the horizon. The ground at the edge crumbled slowly, but noticeably, pieces of rock and earth breaking off and vanishing into the darkness.
The Void wasn’t just empty; it was oppressive. Looking into it felt like staring into the heart of something ancient and malevolent. The edges of the crumbling land seemed to writhe, as though in pain. 
A low, resonating hum emanated from the pieces, vibrating in Zack’s chest. It wasn’t a sound he heard so much as a sensation that gripped him, a warning. The closer he moved, the thinner the air became, pressing against his lungs and making it harder to breathe. He had to fight the instinct to flee, the sheer wrongness of the place was hard to bear.
The darkness beyond the void wasn’t just black; it was an absence, a gaping wound in reality. Zack’s eyes struggled to focus, the edges of his vision blurring as if the void were actively resisting his gaze. Shadows danced at its perimeter, faint whispers tickling the edge of his hearing. He wasn’t sure if the voices were real or a trick of his mind, but their presence chilled him to the core.
“It’s an island surrounded by Void, just like Aiko said,” Zack whispered, a chill running down his spine. He watched as the Void consumed the landscape. It wasn’t just a nothingness; it was a force, erasing everything it touched.
Realization hit him like a punch to the gut. “I’ve been going the wrong way.”
The realm wasn’t just a strange, static environment—it was collapsing, and he was running out of time. Aiko warned him about it but hearing it and seeing the blackness of the Void was something else.
The thinner rain was the clue. The closer he got to the center the denser the rain might become. At least that was the current theory. 
“Can’t stop now,” he muttered through gritted teeth. The egg in his backpack pulsed faintly, as if urging him onward. The stakes were clear: escape, or be swallowed by the void.
Hours later, Zack stumbled back into the cave, his chest heaving with exertion. Every muscle in his body ached, his limbs felt like lead, and his breath came in ragged gasps. He needed another break.
He slumped against the wall, his legs giving out beneath him. The cave, dimly lit by faint bioluminescent moss, felt like a haven compared to the crumbling wasteland outside. Zack dropped his pack, pulling the egg free. Its fiery veins pulsed in a slow, steady rhythm, matching the beat of his own heart. It radiated warmth, as if reassuring him.
“Guess it’s just you and me,” he murmured, running a hand over the egg’s warm surface. Talking to the egg felt ridiculous, but in the silence of the cave, it was comforting. “I don’t know if you can hear me in there, but… you’re gonna have to help me out when you hatch, alright?”
As if in response, the egg pulsed brighter. Zack blinked, sitting up straighter. The glow intensified, shifting to a radiant orange-red, and cracks began to spiderweb across its obsidian-black surface.
“Whoa, whoa—wait, you’re hatching? Now?” Zack scrambled back slightly, both excited and nervous. He watched, transfixed, as the egg began to break apart.
The cracks widened, and pieces of the shell flaked off, revealing glimpses of smooth, dark scales beneath. The glow pulsed faster, and with a sharp crack, the top of the egg shattered completely.
Tiny claws emerged first, gripping the edges of the shell. The creature inside let out a soft, high-pitched chirp as it wriggled free. A small, sleek head poked out next, its eyes gleaming with a sharp intelligence far beyond its size. The creature shook itself, scattering fragments of the egg as it unfurled its wings.
Zack’s breath caught. The dragon was no larger than a crow, its body covered in smooth, black scales that shimmered like polished obsidian. Its wings were delicate and translucent, edged with faint streaks of red that glowed faintly in the dim light. Two tiny horns curved gracefully back from its head, and a ridge of spines ran down its neck to a long, whip-like tail.
“Hey there, little one,” Zack whispered, his voice soft with awe.
The dragon tilted its head, fixing him with piercing amber eyes. It chirped again, the sound somewhere between a bird’s call and a cat’s meow. Then it stretched its wings fully, flapping them experimentally before settling them against its back.
Zack extended a hand cautiously. The dragon regarded him for a moment, then stepped forward, its movements graceful and deliberate. Its tiny claws pricked his skin as it climbed onto his hand, but Zack barely noticed. The dragon’s warmth was comforting, its scales smooth.
“You’re amazing,” Zack said, grinning as the dragon climbed up his arm to perch on his shoulder. He took in the shape of its snout, it seemed delicate. “And you’re a girl, huh?”
The dragon trilled again, but this time it was followed by a distinct voice in Zack’s mind, smooth and laced with playful sarcasm. “Of course, I’m a girl. Didn’t the horns give it away, genius?”
Zack blinked, startled. “Wait—you can talk?!”
The dragon snorted, a puff of smoke curling from her tiny nostrils. “Obviously. I’m a dragon. How else are we supposed to keep humans from hurting themselves.”
Zack laughed, shaking his head in disbelief. “Alright, sassy. I’m Zack. Guess you’re smarter than I expected.”
“Smarter than you, for sure,” she shot back, her amber eyes gleaming. “Now, let’s get to the important part—naming me. You better make it good.”
Zack leaned back, crossing his arms. “Alright, let’s see… How about Ember?”
The dragon made a sound that could only be described as a groan. “Ugh, too obvious. Try again.”
“Okay, fine. Shadow?”
“Seriously? What am I, a bad poem?” She swished her tail, clearly unimpressed. “Put some effort into it!”
Zack rolled his eyes, grinning despite himself. “Alright, alright. What about Nyx?”
The dragon paused, tilting her head as if considering. Finally, she gave a satisfied trill. “Nyx. I like it. Short, mysterious, and fitting for someone as brilliant as me.”
Zack chuckled, reaching up to scratch under her chin. “Nyx it is. Welcome to the team.”
Nyx purred, her tail curling tighter around his neck. “So Zack, where the hell are we?”
Zack’s hand fell down. “Now you see, that's going to be a bit harder to explain.”

Zack sighed, leaning back against the cave wall. The glow from the bioluminescent moss cast faint shadows across Nyx’s sleek, obsidian-black scales. Her amber eyes were fixed on him, expectant and sharp, her tail flicking impatiently.
“This place,” Zack began, gesturing to the jagged rocks and sparse vegetation surrounding them, “We’re stuck in the Void”
Nyx cocked her head. “You’re gonna need to dumb that down a bit.”

Zack explained how the Void works, he also mentioned they are here for the Essence Crystal that's in the middle of the island. Nyx was silent for a moment, her tail curling around Zack’s arm as she processed his words. 

“Do you have a death wish?”

“It’s not for me,” Zack said, standing and stretching. “It’s for a friend, Rei. She’s in danger. Without that crystal, she doesn’t stand a chance.”
Nyx blinked, her sharp retort stalling mid-thought. “Rei,” she repeated, her voice softer now. “This friend of yours... she’s why you’re dragging us into this deathtrap?”

Zack nodded, his expression earnest. 
Nyx’s usual sass was absent as she leaned closer, her warmth pressing against his neck. “You’re risking all this for someone else? I can respect that,” she said, her voice uncharacteristically gentle. “Guess I better make sure we don’t screw it up, then.”
Zack chuckled, scratching under her chin. “Thanks, Nyx. I appreciate the vote of confidence.”
“Don’t get used to it,” she replied, the playfulness returning to her tone. “Now, let’s go risk our necks for your friend. But if we die out there, I’m haunting you.”
“Noted,” Zack said with a faint smile.
Zack and Nyx stepped out of the dim cave. The landscape was just as unforgiving as Zack remembered it.
Zack stretched his arms and winced slightly, instinctively touching his side where a cut from the fight with the jellyfish had been. He stopped in his tracks. "Wait a second..." he muttered, pulling up his shirt to examine the wound. "It’s gone."
Nyx turned to him with a faint smile, her eyes gleaming. “Oh, you’re welcome,” she said.
"You're saying you did this?" Zack asked, turning to face her fully. He rolled his shoulder, noticing how much lighter he felt. "I mean, I’m not complaining, but... how?"
Nyx gave a slight shrug, obviously proud, “It’s a passive healing ability of mine. Only you benefit from it, though it’s not immediate. Takes a bit of time, but it’ll mend most light injuries and stave off fatigue.”
Zack stared at her, half in awe and half skeptical. "So you’re like... a walking health potion?"
Nyx paused for a bit. “I’m going to take my revenge for that quip.” She bit his ear lightly.
Zack’s hand went to his head as he laughed. “I surrender! You win!”
“As it should be.” She puffed out her chest. But that’s not all I can do.” 
Nyx horns lighted up with a slight shimmer, and a glowing shield the size of a dinner plate appeared, floating just in front of Zack. It disappeared after about half a second. “I can summon this. It’s not big, and it doesn’t last long, but it can take a beating.”
Zack leaned closer, inspecting the intricate runes that danced along the edge of the shield. "That’s... incredible. It would be a gamechanger in a fight."
“You have no idea, now get those legs moving, the Void waits for noone” She stretched and then wrapped around his neck, clearly comfortable in her perch.
