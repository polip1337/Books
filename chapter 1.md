Chapter 1

Zack Gottjaeger strolled along the crowded streets of Shibuya, but the cacophony of Tokyo's bustling heart was merely a backdrop to the voice of his audiobook. He was medium height and lean, with a shock of unruly brown hair that constantly fell into his hazel eyes. Zack had been in Japan for nearly a year now, an exchange student from the States, and he still found himself soaking in the sights of the city.

The audiobook he was listening to, "The Art of War," was a nod to his latest obsession: strategy games. Zack had discovered a thriving community of gamers in Tokyo, and he spent many of his evenings battling opponents from around the world.

The strategies and tactics from Sun Tzu's treatise had found their way into his gameplay, giving him an edge that he enjoyed. He was getting the most fun out of outthinking his opponents, anticipating their moves, and executing plans, the winning was less important.

"Know thyself, know thy enemy," the narrator intoned, and Zack smirked at the irony. What enemies did a university student have, other than impending make-believe battles and a dwindling bank account? His parents had balked at his decision to study abroad, citing distance and expense, but Zack had persisted.

Japan had always fascinated him, ever since he was a child devouring manga and anime, dreaming of the day he could walk the streets of Tokyo and experience it firsthand. The reality had been even more exciting than the dream. The food, the people, the language—it was all even better than Zack could have imagined.

Not to mention the culture! Zack's mind wandered to the lecture he was about to attend. Professor Nakamura was known for his deep knowledge of Japanese mythology, a subject that Zack found fascinating. Today’s lecture was on yōkai, the supernatural creatures of Japanese folklore. Zack had been looking forward to it all week. The stories of mischievous spirits, vengeful ghosts, and powerful demons had always captivated him.

The pedestrian light turned green and Zack stepped off the curb, his thoughts still immersed in the world of yōkai. He moved with confidence, navigating the crowded streets with ease. He had come a long way from the shy, uncertain boy who had arrived in Tokyo nearly a year ago. The city, and living alone, had changed him, shaping him into someone more self-assured. As he crossed the street he caught movement out of the corner of his eye.

He turned, his eyes widening at the front lights of the truck barreling down in his direction. His feet felt glued to the pavement, muscles locked in place as the vehicle rushed towards him. He dodged to the side but knew it was too little, too late. The sound of screeching tires echoed in his ears, but all he could do was watch as the vehicle hurtled towards him.

Suddenly a shape crashed into the truck, throwing the whole thing into a wall of the shops on the opposite side of the street. Pieces of the truck were strewn along the path that the vehicle flew along. It ended in a hole in the shopfronts.

Zack was still lying on the ground as a guttural roar shattered the air, and a nightmare beast emerged from the darkness.

Standing on four legs, about the size of a car. Its skin was chitinous and gnarled, with texture like an old stump. It shimmered with an oily sheen under the city lights and seemed to be constantly flowing, making it look ethereal. Each movement it made was accompanied by a sickening, wet sound, as if its very flesh was straining against something. Its limbs were disproportionately long and ended in wickedly sharp claws that scraped the pavement.

Zack watched frozen in shock as the monster shook rubble from its back. Its eyes glowed an unnatural, endless grey. Rows of jagged teeth protruded from its massive jaws, which dripped with a viscous, dark fluid. The creature's breath was visible, a miasma that made Zack gag from the stench, even from across the street.

Before Zack managed to scramble to his feet, another figure darted into view—a woman, with what looked like floating shards of ice around her. She attacked from a distance, hurling icicles at the beast. The air around her crackled as she conjured ice from nothing.

The woman was so beautiful it was hard not to notice, even under the circumstances. She fought the monster like something out of a legend, silver hair streaming behind her. She wore a form-fitting bodysuit that, impossibly, looked to be made from opaque ice. She wielded a crystal staff, its tip glowing with an inner light.

The monster was unfazed by the ice smashing against its head and lurched at her, swiping with its clawed hand. She danced around the monster letting it sail in the air right beside her. She conjured another icy spike under the monster and with a gesture thrust it upward. The icicle struck the creature in its apparently softer underbelly.

This time it managed to pierce the gnarly skin causing the beast to roar in pain and rage as it crashed to the ground.

Zack instincts screamed at him to run, so that's exactly what he did. The beast noticed him at that moment. It let out another maddened roar and charged. Zack's body finally responded, but not in time. He stumbled backward, knowing with sickening certainty that he wouldn't escape.

Just as the monster's claws were about to tear him apart, the woman leapt between them, her ice magic forming a barrier. However it turned out not to be enough. The monster stabbed with its claw, piercing the ice wall and the woman’s side. Red blood stained the frozen surface of her armor.

The impact sent her sprawling, and Zack's stomach dropped as he watched her hit the ground hard. She managed to get up, holding her side and tried to take a step but stumbled. The monster's claws were stuck in the ice.

Without thinking Zack was beside her, holding her up. Her eyes—sharp and determined—met Zack’s.

She opened her eyes wider. "I’m sorry," she said, her voice steady but thin, like she was holding something back. Before Zack could react, she closed her eyes and a warm, tingling sensation spread through his arms, coursing through his veins like liquid fire. She stood on her own and pushed him away.

The world around him blurred, and Zack felt a rush of power unlike anything he had ever known. His vision sharpened, and the colors around him grew more vivid. He could see the monster in startling clarity—the intricate patterns of its skin, the cold emptiness in its eyes. The energy that surged within him begged to be unleashed

"Fight," the woman gasped. "Use your power."

Zack didn't understand what she meant, but he didn't have time to question it. The monster finally managed to free its clawed hand and leapt at him again. Zack raised his hands instinctively. To his shock, flames erupted from his fingertips, roaring to life and engulfing his hands and forearms.

The beast’s claws struck against the flame as if it was a physical thing. The monster recoiled, a hideous screech escaping its maw as the flames licked at its skin, charring it black.

The creature fell back and thrashed wildly, its sharp limbs swinging in a desperate, blind rage. Zack felt the power surging, threatening to consume him, but he couldn't stop. He wouldn't stop. The flames grew hotter, fiercer, until his whole arms were covered with fire.

In a desperate rage the monster lunged again, its claws aiming for Zack's throat. He reacted instinctively, thrusting his hands forward and releasing more power. His fist met the monster's claw with a sound of a hammer striking the anvil. The flames stuck to the creature's claw, igniting it in an instant. The stench of burning flesh filled the air as the beast howled in agony, retreating a few steps.

Thanks to his sharpened vision, he could see every detail of the creature's suffering. He saw the skin crack and peel away, revealing raw, burnt flesh beneath. The fiery glow reflected in the monster's gray eyes, there was anger in them. It was far from over.

Zack could feel the energy building inside him, a roaring inferno waiting to be unleashed. He took a step forward, his eyes fixed on the beast. He could feel the heat radiating from his own body, an aura that made him feel secure and powerful. The air shimmered with heat, distorting the world around him.

“Move swift as the Wind and closely-formed as the Wood. Attack like the Fire and be still as the Mountain.” The audiobooks narrator said into Zack's ears.

Zack rushed the monster.

The flames roared higher, fed by Zack’s growing confidence. He pushed harder, feeling the fire respond to his will, becoming an extension of his very being. The monster tried to swipe at Zack again, but it was slow. He sidestepped the attack and as he moved, he struck out with flaming fists, punching the monster's side. It landed with a resounding crack, the fire searing into the beast’s flesh. The creature's shrieks grew louder, its pain resounding through the street.

The monster tried to lunge one last time, but its strength was gone. It collapsed to the ground, writhing in its death. Zack stood over it, his hands still blazing with fire. He watched as the flames devoured the last remnants of the creature, reducing it to ash and smoke.

Zack exhaled hard, his chest loosening as the tension drained from his body. His hands returned to normal, the flames extinguished but the fire stayed within him. It was now a smoldering ember, waiting to ignite once more.

The silence that followed was almost deafening. The monstrous roars and the crackling of flames had ceased, leaving only the faint sound of the city in the background. Zack took a step back, his legs weak and unsteady.

The street around him was scorched, the pavement cracked and blackened from the intense heat. The monster was gone, reduced to nothing. Zack stared at the spot the monster was, the reality of what he had just done slowly sinking in. His body trembled, caught between wanting to collapse and wanting to laugh. The adrenaline was draining out of him fast.

Zack turned to see a crowd gathering on the edge of the scene. People were pointing and whispering, their faces bewildered, some covering their mouths. But as he looked closer, he realized they weren’t reacting to the battle that had just taken place. Instead, their focus was on the wreckage scattered across the street.

A mangled truck lay overturned, its metal frame twisted and smoking. Broken glass littered the pavement, reflecting the flashing lights of approaching emergency vehicles. It was as if only he saw the monster. The woman who had spoken to him was gone.

A police officer approached Zack with wide eyes. “Sir, are you alright?” he asked., his tone professional but concerned.

Zack blinked, trying to reconcile the two realities. “I... I think so,” he replied, his voice hoarse. “There was a...”

The officer nodded sympathetically. “It looks like you were in a bad accident. Can you tell me what happened?”

Zack hesitated, glancing back at the spot where the monster had fallen. There was no trace of the creature, no scorch marks. Just the aftermath of what appeared to be a severe car crash. His throat was dry. “I’m not sure,” he said finally. “Everything happened so fast.”

The officer gave him a once over and a reassuring pat on the shoulder. “It’s alright. It can be hard to remember details after a traumatic event. Just try to stay calm. Wait over there, the paramedics will check you out.”

Zack nodded numbly. Has it all been real? The flames, the monster, the battle—it had felt real, more real than anything he’d ever experienced. But now, faced with the mundane chaos of an accident scene, he doubted his own senses. He closed his eyes and could still feel the ember of power inside him. Was it real or was he imagining it? Did it feel smaller than before?

As he moved to the side, allowing the emergency personnel to do their work, he overheard fragments of conversation from the onlookers.

“Did you see that car flip? It came out of nowhere!”

“I didn’t even hear a crash. Just turned around, and there it was.”

“Must’ve been going too fast. Lucky no one was seriously hurt.”

The snippets of dialogue only deepened Zack’s confusion. It was as if everyone around him had seen a completely different event. His thoughts drifted back to the silver-haired woman. She had to have something to do with this strange perception shift.

A paramedic approached, offering a warm smile. “Hey there, I’m just going to check you out, okay? Make sure you’re not injured.”

Zack nodded, allowing the paramedic to examine him. As she checked his vitals, he noticed the faint smell of smoke clinging to his clothes. A reminder that something had indeed happened, even if no one else could see it.

“Your vitals are good,” the paramedic said, pulling back. “Do you feel any pain or dizziness?”

“No, I’m okay,” Zack replied, though his mind was far from settled.

“Alright, if you’re sure. But if you start feeling off, make sure to get checked out at a hospital.”

“Thanks,” Zack said, offering a weak smile.

The paramedic moved on, and Zack found himself standing alone amidst the chaos.

The woman. He needed to find the woman. She had answers—about the fire, the monster, all of it.

He scanned the crowd, but she was gone. Of course she was.

Zack turned his back on the wreckage. His legs were shaking and his head was pounding, but the ember in his chest was still there, faint and stubborn. He had questions. A lot of them.

He took a step forward and felt the flickering flame inside him go out. He collapsed to the pavement, the world dimming around him.
