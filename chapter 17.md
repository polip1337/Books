
Chapter 17
Zack sat up, his breath coming in sharp gasps. The cold still clung to him, the echoes of the dream refusing to fade. His hands trembled as he pressed them against his face, trying to ground himself in the present. The room was dim, the flickering light casting long shadows on the stone walls. Across from him, Nyx watched, her dark eyes worried.
“Nightmare?"
Zack hesitated. The dream still pulsed within him, too vivid to be just imagination. He rubbed his arms, the sensation of ice still lingering on his skin. "I saw the Icemen. Before. Before everything went wrong."
Nyx's expression darkened. "Explain."
Zack took a moment, piecing the dream together. "They built this place around a crystal—an enormous one. They moved it higher and higher, floor by floor, always retreating upwards. Those Jellyfish we fought outside. They attacked in waves, getting smarter, getting stronger. No matter what the Icemen did, they kept coming."
"They made their last stand on the highest floor, around the crystal. The barriers failed. The creatures broke through. The light from the crystal pulsed one last time… and then nothing. Silence."
Nyx was quiet for a long time. When she finally spoke, her voice was low. "That doesn't match what we know."
Zack frowned. "How so?"
Nyx gestured to the walls around them. "If they lost, why are the Icemen still here? Why are they still fighting on the lower floors?”
Zack swallowed, his mind racing. The dream had felt absolute, a history carved in ice. But Nyx was right. The tower stood, its defenders still locked in endless battle. One more thing bothered him, the veins he saw in the present had not been present in his vision.
"Something changed," he said finally. "Something the dream didn’t show me."
Nyx nodded slowly. "Or something it didn’t want you to see."
A heavy silence fell between them. The flickering light cast jagged shapes against the walls, mirroring the uncertainty in Zack's mind. If the past he had seen was true, then the present was a contradiction. And contradictions meant missing pieces.
He clenched his fists. "We need to find out what happened."
Nyx fluttered her wings. "Then we start at the source. We go to the crystal."
Zack eyed the alcove. "Give me a moment. I have one more idea before we go."
He entered the alcove. He saw that the Icemen used it for meditation, maybe there could be a way to learn something if he tried this too.
He closed his eyes and focused on his breathing, like Aiko taught him. His consciousness felt like it was slipping ever deeper into himself. All the noises of the outside were at first muffled, then quiet. Only the hum of the alcove and the beat of his heart remained.
 When he opened his eyes, he realized that he was in the caldera where he got Nyx’s egg. That felt like it happened so long ago. The passage of time was visible within the landscape too. The once-stable lava flows had become more pronounced, the light they gave off enough to see everything clearly. The sparse flames were no longer sparse— the patches of burning ground were numerous enough he had to circle around them. The heat wrapped around him like a living thing, pressing against his skin and feeling like a warm blanket.
Zack took a slow step forward, feeling the earth beneath him shift as if alive. Embers floated lazily through the air, twisting before vanishing. The egg he had once found Nyx in was long gone, but the pedestal remained. The blackened stone had changed, it was more reflective and sharper now, like obsidian. He reached out a hand, feeling the heat intensify the closer he got. The moment his fingers brushed the stone, a deep tremor rumbled through the caldera, sending waves rippling across the molten pools.
The caldera was changing. And so was he.

Zack clenched his fists, feeling the power humming through him. The caldera pulsed in response, as if recognizing his growth. He felt a tug at the edges of his consciousness, the sense that something deeper lay ahead, waiting for him to claim it.
Not yet, he thought. One step at a time.
He opened his eyes in the waking world, the fire still burning within.
"Alright," he said. "Let's go."
Zack and Nyx stepped out of the house, he felt a weight settle in his chest. He had been here before, but it felt different now. The knowledge of his dream made him look at the streety with surprising sadness. He saw them as they were meant to be, full of strange and alien life. Their current state made him want to punish the beings guilty of this destruction.
Nyx perched on his shoulder, her claws gripping lightly. “You’re different,” she said. “It’s like you’re taking it personally.”
Zack nodded, rolling his shoulders. "It’s like you’re reading my thoughts."
“Very funny”
The tower seemed to answer his challenge. From the shadows, a glow pulsed, illuminating the ice formations scattered across the street. Zack’s head throbbed as he watched the now familiar way the jellyfish moved. It froze when it spotted them, letting out a low, menacing hum that vibrated through the ground.
Zack activated his skills. This time, he was ready.
The situation was eerily familiar, yet different. It had changed. Or maybe Zack had. The last time they fought one of these things, he had barely scraped by. Now, fire burned beneath his skin, and his strength had grown. He wouldn’t be caught off guard again.
The creature lunged, its tendrils slashing through the air. Zack sidestepped, Inferno Step activating with a burst of heat, sending him sliding away in a blur of flame. The cobblestone beneath him glowed briefly where he had stood before fading. Zack slammed shoulder first into the wall. 
“Faster than I thought,” he hissed, clutching his shoulder.

Nyx spun away from a swipe of the creature’s claws, giving Zack a second to recover. “Or you’re just worse at steering,” she shot back. A tendril lashed toward her; she blocked it with a shield. “Maybe ease off before you turn yourself into a wall mural.”
The creature twisted toward Zack, its movements off, as if it slightly out of sync with time. Zack didn’t hesitate—he raised his hands, channeling Armor of Flame. Fire surged from his fists forming punching daggers. He ducked under a tendril and struck, driving the blades into the creature’s side. Heat coursed through his arms, the dark veins in the creature writhing against the flames.
Unlike before, his magic wasn’t dampened by the rain, and his recent growth gave him an edge.  So he managed to punch through its skin. He knew he was not doing as much damage as he should. 

The monster recoiled, shrieking as its body flickered. Zack twisted his grip, opening the wound and slammed a knee into its core. The impact sent a shockwave rippling outward, cracks forming beneath their feet. The creature thrashed, but Zack didn’t let up.
Attack I will shield you! “Nyx’s voice rang in his mind, sharp and urgent.
Zack didn’t need to be told twice. He gritted his teeth, and punched with his other hand. Two daggers pierced the monster's chest as it prepared its counter-attack. The Voidstalker lashed out, but the blow glanced off a shimmering shield Nyx summoned, the barrier holding firm for a crucial moment.
Now! Nyx shouted.
Zack dispelled his armor, freeing his fists.Zack pressed the attack. He surged forward, Inferno Step igniting as he delivered a flying knee straight to the creature's head. Its neck stretched way too far, right until it started to tear like plastic.
The jellyfish let out one last wail, its head falling onto its back as it collapsed to the ground, dead.
Inferno Step has reached level 2.
Zack landed hard, his knees bending to absorb the impact, but he managed to stay upright. He stood still for a moment, catching his breath. His heart pounded, but not from fear. From adrenaline. From power. He was stronger now. 
Nyx landed beside him, looking at the almost torn off head. Well… shit That went different than before.
Zack wiped a streak of soot from his cheek, his grin wide and wild "Yeah. It was fucking awesome."
Can’t deny that. Let’s find another, she said, with a predatory grin that would look scary if it was not on a cat sized body. You need to be stronger. 
Zack cracked his knuckles, nodding. "Then let’s keep going."
They stalked the streets of the city, the blue glow from the ice formations reflected off shattered buildings and narrow alleyways. They moved cautiously, pausing at intersections to scan for movement. Zack wanted to fight, but he wanted to control the terms. He had faced these creatures before; now, he sought mastery over them.
There’s one ahead, Nyx whispered, pointing to a flickering glow beyond a ruined archway. Don’t kill it right away. Use it to train.
Zack grinned. "Good idea."
He stepped forward, deliberately making a noise to draw the creature’s attention. It turned, its tendrils snapping to life, glowing with unnatural energy. The moment it lunged, Zack activated Inferno Step, shifting forwards, under its blow. He struck in passing and finished his move a couple of yards behind the monster. He moved in and out of range, letting the fight stretch, trying out different combinations. Every attempt felt smoother, his body adapting to the heightened speed.
Inferno Step has reached level 3.
After they were done with the first, they hunted more. At first Nyx worked as an additional safety cushion. If Zack mistimed a dodge she was there to intercept the blow with a barrier, before flicking her wings to reposition. With time Zack needed her help less and less.

He even experimented, he tried to use Scorching Aura as area denial, pushing the enemy back with waves of heat. He was met with limited success as the Jellyfish were highly resistant to magic, but the effort still honed his control.
Scorching Aura has reached level 8.
With each new fight Zack felt something shift within him. His Armor of Flame responded more instinctively, absorbing impacts with greater efficiency. His aura burned brighter, more intense. And Inferno Step—his footwork visibly improved, the bursts of speed almost always leaving him where he wanted. He was pushing himself, and his skills were responding.
Armor of Flame has reached level 8.
Incandescent Form has reached level 7.
Devouring Fire has reached level 8.
Zack delivered the finishing blow to yet another Jellyfish. His fist crashing into the creature’s core, obliterating it. He exhaled, wiping sweat from his brow. A familiar sensation rushed through him. He had leveled up. Even his passives Incandescent Form and Devouring Fire had grown stronger. And he could feel his martial skill refining itself as well, he should soon get an upgrade to that too..
Nyx smirked. That was better. You run into any walls this time.
 Zack flexed his fingers, feeling the lingering heat in his skin. The exhaustion was there, but so was exhilaration. “Yeah. Let’s hunt more.”
