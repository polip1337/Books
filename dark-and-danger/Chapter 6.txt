Chapter 6




Mia woke with a start, her heart pounding in her chest as she scanned the darkened room. The faint glow of the weakening light rune barely bright for her to make out the sleeping figures of her companions. Her eyes fell upon Hector, slumped against the wall with his mouth hanging open, snoring softly. Panic surged through her; he was supposed to be keeping watch.


"Damn it, Hector," Mia muttered under her breath, her wavy auburn hair falling into her deep green eyes as she pushed herself up from the cold stone floor. She nudged Diana awake, who stirred and looked around with alarm etched on her face.


"Wha...what's going on?" Diana asked groggily, her piercing blue eyes narrowed in confusion.


"Look." Mia pointed at Hector, her voice tense and angry. "The idiot is asleep."


Diana's pretty face hardened as she assessed the situation. The air grew heavy, suffused with the weight of their collective anger. Hector had jeopardized their safety, and they all knew it.


"Hey!" Mia hissed, kicking Hector's leg none too gently. He jolted awake, eyes wide with surprise and disorientation.


"Wh-what happened?" he stammered, rubbing his eyes and trying to sit up straighter.


"Nice job on the watch, Hector," Mia said, her voice dripping with sarcasm. "You were supposed to be our first line of defense, and you just left us exposed."


"Come on, Mia," Hector protested, running a hand through his short, dark hair. "I was just resting my eyes for a moment. I didn't think—"


"Exactly. You didn't think," Mia interrupted, her anger flaring. "We're in a dangerous place, Hector! We can't afford to let our guard down, not even for a second."


"Alright, alright," Hector said defensively, trying to diffuse the situation. "I made a mistake. I'm sorry. I'll make it up to you guys, I promise."


"Promises aren't going to keep us alive," Mia snapped, her fear fueling her agitation. She knew they were all vulnerable in this strange world, and the thought of losing any of her friends was unbearable.


"Enough!" Frank interrupted before this could spiral out of control. "Hector fucked up, but were fine, we got lucky. Move on."


Hector's brown eyes burned with frustration, but he held his tongue. He understood Mia's concerns, but being chastised so publicly stung. The silence that followed was filled with tension and unspoken accusations.


Mia took a deep breath, attempting to push her anger aside. They couldn't afford to be at each other's throats; they needed each other if they wanted to survive and get back home.


"Boy, this place must be pretty safe if even our fearless protector can catch some Z's," Frank quipped, his messy brown hair falling into his gray eyes as he tried to lighten the mood with a grin. "Maybe we're not in such a bad spot after all."


"Are you for real?" Mia snapped, her green eyes narrowing as she clenched her fists. Her anger redirected to Frank "This is serious."


"Alright, alright," Frank said, holding up his hands in surrender. "I was just trying to help. No need to bite my head off."


"Can we all just take a step back and breathe?" Diana interjected, her blue eyes scanning the group. "Arguing isn't going to solve anything. We need to focus on what we can do to keep ourselves safe."


"Thank you, Diana," Mia said, taking a deep breath. Trying to control her inner rage "We need to come up with a better system for keeping watch so this doesn't happen again."


"Fine," Hector grumbled, still feeling unfairly attacked. "I'll take double shifts tonight if it'll make everyone happy."


"Nobody wants you to take double shifts," Diana replied, her voice calm and cold. "We just want to find a solution that works for everyone."


"Seems your trust stock is currently quite low buddy" Frank whispered to Hector who gave him an angry glance.


"Maybe we should set up a rule against sitting?" Frank suggested, scratching his chin thoughtfully. "No way to fall asleep that way"


"Sounds dumb but workable," Diana agreed, nodding hesitantly.


“Its not dumb if it works, also there is a reason why its called standing watch, Diana” Frank said.


"Enough," Mia snapped, her green eyes blazing with determination. "We're wasting precious time arguing when we should be finding food and water." She scanned the desolate surroundings of the room they found themselves in, taking charge of the situation. "Without them, we won't have the strength to continue or defend ourselves against whatever fucked up monstrosity we encounter next."


Hector and Diana exchanged wary glances but didn't object. They knew she was right, and their empty stomachs were a constant reminder that they needed food sooner rather than later.


"Alright," Hector conceded, rubbing his hands together. "Let's get to work." 


The group unlocked the entrance and then got to searching the immediate surroundings. Room after room, they found nothing but desolation. Their search felt increasingly futile, and the realization of their dire circumstances weighed heavily on them. Frank, Mia, Hector, and Diana shared a silent moment of disappointment, their stomachs rumbling in protest, a constant reminder of their pressing need for sustenance in this unforgiving city.


They continued their search, rummaging through piles of rubble and rotten wood, the air thick with dust. The place seemed to have been abandoned for centuries, making it difficult to find anything fresh or remotely edible.


"Anything over there?" Mia called to Hector, who was inspecting the remains of what looked like a large wooden table.


"Nothing ," he replied, tossing the squishy remains of wood aside with a look of disgust. "This is hopeless."

"Wait!" Diana said suddenly, her eyes widening as she spotted something among the debris.
She bent down, her fingers gingerly brushing aside a layer of rubble. Suddenly she recoiled and fell down on her behind. Her heart raced as she watched a grotesque sight buried beneath the wreckage—a lifeless, decaying monster, its twisted form crushed by the rubble that had buried it. It was clear that the wall nearby collapsed on it.
The group recoiled in shock and horror as the truth of their surroundings sank in. Frank's earlier optimism faded, replaced by a realization of the new danger. Like the monsters weren't enough, they needed to watch for collapsing walls too.
Mia's voice finally broke the silence, though it was tinged with unease. "We need to be even more cautious. We don't need another run in with ... those things."
________________


Mia's eyes scanned another similar room, her determination unwavering as they searched through the ruins. The sound of shifting debris echoed in the empty space around them. Just as she was about to give up hope, a glimmer caught her attention.


"Over there," Mia called out, pointing through a partially collapsed wall. In the room beyond a beam of light pierced through a hole in the ceiling, revealing a small pool of water that had formed in a sunken corner of the room. The shimmering liquid seemed to call out to them, beckoning from its resting place amidst the dirt and debris.


"Water," Hector breathed, relief washing over his face. He rushed toward the pond but Mia stopped him in his tracks.


"What if it's not safe?" she hesitated aloud, her gaze locked on the glistening surface of the water as if searching for reassurance that wasn't there. Deep within, a gnawing fear gripped her, making her question every potential consequence, especially the prospect of falling ill in this unforgiving place.


"Only one way to find out," Frank said with a shrug. "We draw lots. Whoever gets the short straw takes a sip first."


Mia held her tongue for a couple of heartbeats hoping for Hector to step forward. When that didn't happen she said: "Frank’s right," her deep green eyes never leaving the surface of the water. One in a four chance, not so bad."It's fair that way."


Each of them hesitated, their fingers hovering over the makeshift straws Frank had fashioned from broken wood. Their gazes were filled with uncertainty and fear, knowing that any one of them could be chosen to test the potentially dangerous water.


"Alright," Mia said finally, exhaling sharply as she drew a straw from Frank's closed fist. She held it up for the others to see, her heart pounding in her chest. "Here goes nothing."


One by one, they revealed their straws, each pair of eyes flicking nervously between the sticks in their hands and the pond before them.


"Looks like we have our winner," Frank murmured, holding up his notably shorter straw. His usual grin was nowhere to be found, replaced with a tight-lipped grimace. "What a great idea you had, Frank."


"Be careful," Diana whispered, her gaze filled with concern.


"Hey, I'm always careful, and I'm going to drink this extra careful" he replied, attempting to inject some humor into the situation. But his voice wavered, betraying his apprehension.


"Alright, let's get this over with," Mia said, her eyes locked on Frank as he knelt by the edge of the water. She wanted to get on with this and go back to searching for a way home. The tension in the room was palpable, the silence broken only by their shallow breaths and pounding hearts.


Frank's fingers trembled slightly as he reached out towards the pond, his eyes darting between the water and his friends' expectant faces. He could feel their collective breaths hitch in their throats, the air thick with anxiety and desperation.


"Here goes nothing," he murmured, giving Mia, Diana, and Hector one last glance before scooping up a minuscule amount of water with cupped hands.


"Always am," Frank mumbled to himself, though with a quiver in his voice.


He brought his hands to his lips, pausing for a beat as he hesitated Then, with a steadying exhale, he took a cautious sip.


The group watched with bated breath, their eyes glued to Frank's face for any sign of distress or discomfort. His gray eyes flickered with uncertainty, a bead of sweat forming at his temple as he swallowed the water.


"Frank?" Diana asked hesitantly, her voice barely above a whisper. "Are you okay?"


"I... I think so, but honestly I'm amazed at myself that I did not pretend to choke and die." he said, his gaze still unfocused as he tried to discern any abnormal sensations within his body. "I don't feel any different. Yet."


"Alright," Mia declared, her tone firm but laced with concern. "We'll wait here for a couple of hours. If nothing bad happens to Frank by then, we can assume that the water is safe enough to drink. Not like we have much choice"


As they settled down to pass the time, it was impossible not to notice the gnawing hunger that clawed at their insides, a constant reminder of their precarious situation. The minutes dragged on, each second feeling like an eternity as they kept a watchful eye on Frank.


After an hour of sitting around Mia was starting to doze off.


"Hey, look at this," Hector called out, crouching down near the water's edge. His fingers traced over a set of indentations in the thin layer of dirt around the water – tracks that bore an eerie resemblance to the clawed feet of the monsters they had encountered before.


"Damn it," Mia muttered under her breath, her knuckles turning white as she clenched her fists. "They must have found this place too."


"Right now, we need to focus on Frank," Diana reminded them, her gaze never leaving the man in question. "We'll deal with the monsters if they come. They didn't seem concerned about stealth before."


"Agreed," Mia said, nodding sharply. "Let's just hope that the water isn't tainted with whatever twisted those creatures."


As the hours trickled by, relief began to settle over the group. Frank showed no signs of illness or poisoning, and it seemed that they had stumbled upon a much-needed lifeline in their quest for survival.


"Frank, how're you feeling?" Diana asked, her voice soft yet laced with anticipation.


"Surprisingly okay," he replied, a tentative smile forming on his lips. "I guess we can consider this water safe... for now."


With Frank's approval, the group took turns drinking from the water reservoir, each swallowing the liquid with a combination of hesitation and desperate thirst. Mia watched as Hector drank, her eyes narrowing in anger, still not forgetting him falling asleep. They needed to focus on finding food, on surviving, she admonished herself. No time to be angry.


"Alright," Mia said, wiping droplets of water from her chin. "We've bought ourselves some time. Let's go back to searching for food."


"Agreed," Diana chimed in, her tone heavy with exhaustion. "The sooner we find some, the better off we'll be."


"Hey, maybe we'll find a hidden stash of candy bars!" Frank quipped, trying to lift the mood, but his words sounded hollow even to his own ears.


“I know we’re all hungry, ” He made a placating gesture do Diana, ”but we have days before we start getting physically weak from lack of food, maybe longer because we have water now”

The group set into a routine, resting in the grand hall and looking for any food source during the day. They checked their marked building, and a couple of nearest ones. At first they stuck to the ground level but after they cleared that they decided to check the first and second floors without success. Any time they heard anything suspicious they ran towards the safety of the grand hall.

Hunger gnawing at their stomachs, they delved deeper into another building, searching through every corner, every pile of rubble, every crevice that might hold something edible. The light filtering through collapsed walls cast eerie shadows over the once-grand halls, making them feel like intruders in a forgotten world.


"Anything?" Diana asked hopefully, her voice echoing through the desolate space.


"Nothing, not even fucking mold or shrooms." Mia replied, frustration boiling within her. "How can there be nothing? We're not going to last much longer without food."


"Keep looking," Hector urged, his voice strained. "There has to be something here."


"Maybe if someone hadn't fallen asleep during their watch, we wouldn't be in this mess," Mia snapped, unable to hold back her anger any longer.


"Enough, Mia," Diana interjected, her brow furrowed. "You're not even making sense. We need to focus on the task at hand."


"Fine," Mia muttered, biting back a retort. She clenched her fists and forced herself to keep searching, trying not to let her emotions cloud her judgment.


Hours passed, and the group's energy waned, their hunger growing more urgent with every fruitless search. It happened a bit sooner every day, even with access to water they were growing weaker. Desperation seeped into Mia's thoughts as she rummaged through a pile of debris, willing herself to find something, anything.


"Damn it!" she exclaimed, slamming her hand against a crumbling wall. "There has to be something!"


"Let's regroup," Diana suggested, her voice weary. "Maybe we need to change our approach."


"Or maybe we just need to face reality," Mia said bitterly. "We're stuck in this godforsaken place, and there's nothing left for us here."


"Then we'll just have to find a way out," Hector declared, determination shining in his eyes. Mia was jealous of his optimism.


The team returned to their makeshift base, shoulders slumped, and their faces etched with dejection. Their footsteps, once filled with purpose, now echoed with a heavy sense of disappointment. The room, which had previously held a glimmer of hope, felt suffocating and empty. Frank's usual jokes had faded into a somber silence, and Mia's bright spirit had dimmed. They gathered in the dimly lit chamber, their tired expressions conveying the silent story of a group grappling with the harsh reality.


"Guys, there's only one option left," Diana said, breaking the heavy silence. She took a deep breath, bracing herself for the reaction she knew was coming. "We need to hunt one of the monsters."


"Are you insane?" Mia asked incredulously, rubbing her temples. "We barely made it out alive last time!"


"Look around, Mia! There's no other choice!" Diana insisted, her voice cracking with desperation. She continued, "We're weak, we're hungry, and if we don't do something now, we won't have the strength to do it later."


"Can we really do this?" Mia asked hesitantly, her eyes wide with fear.


"Maybe not," Diana admitted, her own heart pounding in her chest. "But I'd rather die trying than starve slowly in this place." She expected more resistance, but as she looked around at her friends' gaunt faces, she saw only resignation and exhaustion.


"We might still have some time before we starve, but we're getting weaker already," Hector muttered reluctantly. "Let's hunt."


Mia nodded, taking charge as the group began their preparations. They checked their makeshift weapons, each one was crude and untested, but they clung to them like they could save their lives. Maybe they could.


"Lets go, no time to waste" Mia instructed as they formed a hunting party. Once the decision was made there was no reason to delay. 


“Wait, I should enchant your weapons before we go hunting,” Diane said.


“You said each one will take four hours, this will take forever. Can I get my dagger enchanted first?” Frank said.


Hector shrugged and agreed, the rest of the team settled in for the wait.


Meanwhile, Diana carefully cradled the scrap dagger in her hands, her fingers tracing the rough edges of the metal. In the dim light of their hideout, she began the enchantment ritual, her movements deliberate and precise. A soft incantation escaped her lips, whispered words carrying the weight of her concentration. As she channeled her energy into the blade, a faint, ethereal glow began to envelop it, casting a gentle radiance in the otherwise gloomy room. Diana's face bore a look of intense focus, her piercing blue eyes locked onto the dagger as the enchantment took hold, infusing the weapon with newfound strength.


“It's done!” Diana announced four hours later. The rest of the team gathered to watch the dagger.

As Diana handed the newly enchanted scrap dagger to Frank, he examined it with an impressed grin. "Wow, Diana, this dagger looks almost as sharp as my wit!"


Mia rolled her eyes, while Hector chuckled, "Let's hope it's sharper, Frank, or we're in real trouble."


“Shit, I have bad news,” Diana said. It seems the enchantment is fuelled by my mana. I got a ten point decrease for my max. If I enchant all of your weapons I'm down to half my mana. 


The team huddled together, their faces etched with concern, as Diana revealed the unexpected downside of her enchantments.


Mia bit her lip, deep in thought. "We can't keep enchanting everything, then. We need to be selective. Can you dispel the enchant?"

“Yeah, at least my mana is not gone forever, I just have to want it dispelled.” Diana said.


Frank chimed in with a smirk, trying to lighten the mood. "So, how about we only enchant the weapons that have the best punchline potential?"


Diana managed a weak smile, appreciating the attempt at humor. "As much as I'd love to do that, we have to be practical. Let's prioritize the weapons we use the most, the ones that will give us the greatest advantage in tough situations."


They knew that every enchantment came at a cost, and they couldn't afford to deplete her mana reserves completely. The team's struggle with this dilemma highlighted the harsh reality of their situation - in this unforgiving city, every choice came with consequences, even the ones meant to protect them. In the end they enchanted Frank’s second dagger and Hector's mace, because of the skills they had with the weapons.
