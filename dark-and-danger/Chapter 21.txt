Chapter 21: Picking up the pieces
Diana's healing spells had done their work, and Hector felt strong enough to stand up. The image of Mia's frightened face as the centipedes noticed her haunted him, and he knew that they had to go check if she was alive.


“Let’s go.” With a determined glint in his eye, Hector knew they had to keep moving, to press on, he didn’t want to slow down and think. 


They moved slowly, with Hector leaning on Diana to walk. They left behind the scene of the explosion as they made their way toward the last known location of Mia. Hector wanted to rush forward but didn’t have the strength.


As they went out to the street , they were met with a horrific sight. Mia lay in the middle of the street, her legs broken, pain clouding her eyes. Frank knelt beside her, offering her comfort. They were surrounded by dead abominations, Hector counted at least six.


Hector felt an overwhelming desire to protect Mia. He turned to Diana, who was casting her spells as soon as some mana regenerated. Her staff emitted a faint glow as she focused on Mia's injuries, channeling her power into mending the broken bones.


Hector glanced at the lifeless bodies of the centipedes that littered the ground, the remains of the fierce battle they'd fought. The monsters were a gruesome sight, especially the one propped up by the spear coming out of the back of its skull. There was way more blood than usual, the monsters had multiple wounds, as if someone wanted to hurt them as much as possible. Frank was bloodied and battered, and Hector looked at his friend with new appreciation. But none of that mattered now. Mia's well-being was their priority.


"Hey, Mia, you're gonna be okay," Hector said with a weary yet reassuring smile, his voice trembling from his own pain and exhaustion.


Mia nodded, her voice barely above a whisper, her face contorted with pain. 


“Hector, no reason for you to keep standing, buddy, you're injured too” Frank said. Hector's initial refusal gave way to Frank's insistence, and he sank down against a nearby wall, his body weary.


"I'm fine, Frank," Hector assured, his voice strained, a weak smile on his lips. "Just a scratch. But let's make sure Mia's okay." He waved his hand in dismissal. His missing left hand.


Frank’s eyes opened wide “Oh my god, what happened to you?” he asked in shock.


Hector looked down at his missing hand, the reality of it setting in. The initial adrenaline had masked the shock, but now the gravity of the situation hit him like a tidal wave. He hadn't realized the extent of the damage. He swallowed hard, his eyes locked on the stump where his hand used to be. He felt a wave of bitterness wash over him.


"It's just a reminder of what this place can do to us. Nothing more." He avoided looking at his stump, his gaze instead focused on Mia and Diana, who were just a few feet away. 


Diana's healing magic enveloped Mia, her wounds slowly mending. Mia's face was contorted with pain, but there was a glimmer of relief in her eyes. She knew she was in good hands.


Hector watched the process, his own emotions swirling. The loss of his hand was a constant bitterness he couldn't deal with, on the other hand the overwhelming relief that Mia was going to be okay was confusing him. He didn't know how to feel.


With Mia finally receiving the care she needed, Hector cast a somber look in Frank's direction. "Thank you, Frank. Your quick thinking might have saved her life."


"You're welcome, man. I'm here to save lives, crack jokes, and I'm all out of jokes." Frank said with a small smile.


Hector managed a faint chuckle, the sound hoarse and strained. The weight of their recent ordeal pressed down on him. They had faced so much darkness, it all just felt like too much.. 


He steeled himself as he clasped Frank's shoulder in a silent acknowledgment and then looked back to Mia. As Diana finished her healing magic, Hector watched with relief and anxiety. Mia's broken legs were whole again, her pain gone, but she looked fragile, her strength sapped. The toll that this had taken on her was evident, and Hector felt a pang of worry.


"Thank you, Diana," Mia whispered, her voice frail, as she attempted to sit up. It was clear she was determined to show her strength. She managed to prop herself up with her arms, but her legs wobbled, unable to bear her weight just yet. Mia's eyes met Hector's, and there was a sense of vulnerability he hadn't seen before.


"You'll be back on my feet in no time." Hector said, automatically. He wasn’t sure if it was true.
It was as if this nightmarish place thrived on breaking them apart, testing the limits of their endurance.


The group had faced countless horrors, but in that moment, their exhaustion was undeniable. Hector, Frank, Diana, and Mia shared an unspoken understanding – they had to lean on each other, there was no room for squabbling. 


“Check your status, maybe it explains why you are still weakened,” Diana suggested to Mia.

Mia watched as Diana closed the doors behind them with the small stone key. Her heart was heavy with guilt. As she approached the hideout, the anxiety of the previous encounter weighed on her, and the image of Hector's missing hand haunted her thoughts. She dreaded the conversation to come.


The familiar space greeted her, her friends were scattered around, recovering from their most recent ordeal. Hector sat off to one side, his face unreadable, but the vacant space where his left hand should have been spoke volumes. 


Mia couldn't shake the feeling that Hector's injury was her fault. She ran off  and got herself  trapped, helpless as the centipedes closed in, and it was everyone else who had to risk their life to save her. The result of that rescue was Hector's dismembered hand.


She sat down next to the fire, Mia stared at the flame with unfocused eyes, her hands fidgeting in her lap. She could feel the tension building within the group, but no one dared address the elephant in the room – her sudden disappearance earlier. It was as if they were afraid that talking about it would somehow make it more real. Mia clenched her fists, a knot forming in her stomach.


Tears welled in her eyes, she knew that no words could mend the damage done. She had been too scared, too weak, and it had cost her friend dearly. But she had to try.


"Guys... I need to say something," Mia began hesitantly, breaking the silence that had settled between them. "I'm scared. I don't know how we ended up here or why we're trapped in this world, but it terrifies me." She paused for a moment, gathering her thoughts, her expression a mix of anger and fear.


"Sometimes I can't help but feel paranoid, like there's something lurking just beyond my sight, waiting to strike when I'm at my weakest," she continued. Her voice grew more desperate, the words tumbling out uncontrollably. "I hate feeling so powerless, and I worry that we might never find our way back home."


As Mia's pent-up emotions spilled forth, her friends just listened. 


“I need to get home. I know we all want that but I don't think any of you feel it to the same degree as I do.” She shook her head “I was going insane, our lack of progress was making me furious. I even thought you wanted to stay here and that's why you were talking about caution and slowing everything down.”

Mia laughed a bitter laugh “So i decided to take things into my own hands, i wanted to get to the pyramid and go home and bring some help for you guys. I have been deluding myself. I didn't even get halfway there before you had to save me.” 


“Look Mia, i get it i really do” Frank hesitated before speaking up, his eyes downcast as he traced patterns in the dirt with his finger. "I'm terrified," he admitted softly. "Of dying here, of never finding a way back, of pain... But at the same time, I have hope. We've made it this far because we've worked together. We can't let anything tear us apart now."


As if to emphasize his point, Frank reached for a nearby stick and poked at the fire, sending a shower of sparks into the air. Mia, and Diane slowly nodded in agreement, but Mia noticed Hector sat there unmoving.


"Remember when we first started out?" Frank reminisced. "We were so clueless back then. But look how far we've come. We’ve survived and gotten stronger."


"Here's to us," Frank raised his hand in a toast. "To our strength and determination... and to trust."


"Cheers to that," Mia agreed, trying to be positive. Hector and Diane hesitated for a moment before joining in, their hands raised in a symbol of unity despite the undercurrent of unease. Mia knew that healing her relationship with Hector, might not be possible. She was happy that Frank at least seemed to have forgiven her.


She noticed Frank was engaged in a hushed conversation with Diana, and she decided to take the first step. She stood up and walked over to Hector.


Hector looked up as she neared, and his gaze met hers. Mia's voice was soft, filled with sorrow and self-blame. "I'm so sorry, Hector. None of this should have happened." Her voice quivered as the weight of her guilt bore down on her.


Hector's response was measured, his tone devoid of feeling. "Doesn’t matter, what’s done is done. No way to turn back time now.”
