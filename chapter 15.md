Chapter 15
Zack trudged through the wasteland with Nyx on his shoulder, her tail occasionally flicking to keep her balance. The rain grew denser, each suspended droplet glistening like crystal. Zack pushed through them, leaving a corridor of empty air. Since there was no sign of danger for hours now they started to talk and get to know each other.
“So you’re telling me,” Nyx said, her voice laced with mockery, “that you thought punching him really hard was a brilliant tactical decision?”
Zack huffed, dodging an outcrop of jagged stone. “It was all I could think of, it was…” He trailed off, gesturing vaguely.
"A hard fight, yeah you mentioned” Nyx finished, snorting. “Genius-level problem solving right there. Remind me again why I hatched for you?”
He chuckled, shaking his head. “I’m starting to wonder that myself. Maybe I should’ve left you in the egg a bit longer.”
Nyx’s claws lightly gripped his shoulder as she leaned closer. “Admit it—you’d be lost without me. And I’m the best thing to happen to you in… well, probably ever.”
Zack opened his mouth to retort, but Nyx suddenly straightened, her amber eyes narrowing. Her wings flared slightly, and Zack felt the heat of her body increase.
"Stop,” she said sharply.
Without hesitation, Zack froze mid-step. His hand curled into a fist but he didn’t activate his skills. The rain—so dense it was hard to see—distorted his view of the landscape, but nothing seemed immediately amiss.
“What is it?” he asked quietly.
Nyx didn’t answer immediately. Instead, she leapt from his shoulder and hovered a few feet above the ground, her translucent wings beating in a soft rhythm. She turned her head slowly, sniffing the air.
"The ground,” she said, her tone cautious. “It’s not right. Look closer.”
Zack knelt, studying the rocky terrain. At first glance, it seemed no different from the endless wasteland they’d been navigating. But he noticed something strange. The surface wasn’t just rock and dirt—it was textured, uneven in a way that didn’t match the rest of the terrain. He reached out to brush away some of the loose soil, revealing a smooth, pale surface beneath.
His stomach twisted with recognition. “No way…”
Nyx landed beside him, her claws scraping lightly against the surface. "Does it look like those jellyfish you mentioned?”
Zack’s breath quickened. The surface matched the flesh-like texture exactly. He looked around and realized with mounting dread that the ground for several yards in every direction had the same organic texture.
“They are everywhere,” he said, keeping his voice low. “A whole field of them.”
Nyx nodded, her eyes scanning the horizon. “Half-buried, dormant maybe. But I wouldn’t count on them staying that way if you step on them.”
Zack’s instincts screamed at him to move, but the danger of stepping wrong made him hesitate. The waking one would probably wake all of them, and he doubted they’d survive that. He looked to Nyx.
“What’s the plan?”
Nyx’s tail lashed, her mind clearly working quickly. “First off, don’t panic. Second, follow my lead. We’ll move slow and quiet. Minimal vibration, no sudden movements.”
Zack nodded, rising to his feet. Nyx took the lead, gliding low over the ground and signaling him to follow. Her wings barely stirred the air, and Zack did his best to step lightly and deliberately.
Every step felt like a gamble. There could be a tendril sneaking just below the layer of dirt.The ground beneath him seemed to shift ever so slightly, and he imagined the popping out of the ground. The thought sent a shiver down his spine.
They moved in tense silence for what felt like an eternity. Zack kept his focus on Nyx, trusting her. 
The rain became so dense that Zack could barely see more than a couple of meters ahead. The resistance of the rain required strength to push through which made silent movement even harder. The distorted reflections of the droplets made the ground hard to discern .By this point Nyx was walking on the raindrops instead of flying between them. 
Finally, the texture of the ground began to change. The pulsing organic surface gave way to solid, rocky terrain. Zack let out a shaky breath of relief as he stepped onto firmer ground. 
Zack stumbled forward, as the rain stopped suddenly. He looked up, blinking against the sudden clarity. They had emerged into an open area, a wall of droplets all around it.

“We made it,” Zack muttered, his voice tinged with disbelief. “And got something extra for the effort!”
Nyx landed on his shoulder. “Told you I’d get you through.” Her tone was smug, but Zack could hear the relief beneath it.
As Zack caught his breath, his eyes were drawn to the center of the area. Rising out of the wasteland was a massive tower, its surface smooth and white, reflecting the faint light of the storm like a mirror. It was massive at least two hundred meters wide. The air was eerily still, the ground dry and undisturbed.
“What is that?” Zack whispered, his eyes fixed on the structure.
Nyx followed his gaze, her amber eyes narrowing. “Looks important. And dangerous. Probably where the crystal is.”
Zack nodded. “We’ve come this far. Let’s finish it.”
With renewed determination, the two of them began making their way toward the tower, the storm swirling ominously around them.
The approach to the tower was unlike anything Zack had experienced before. The silence was suffocating, a presence that pressed in on him more acutely with every step. Without the constant resistance of the rain, his body felt unnervingly light, his movements exaggerated. Each step he took seemed louder than it had any right to be, the crunch of dry dirt beneath his boots echoing in the stillness.
“It’s… too quiet,” Zack murmured, the sound still too loud for the dead air around them.
Nyx perched on his shoulder, her claws gripping tightly as her wings twitched. Her gaze flitted across the landscape. “It’s wrong,” she murmured. “Like we’re not supposed to be here.”
Zack nodded. He couldn’t quite describe it, but there was a tangible weight to the atmosphere around the tower. The ground was rocky with large stones jutting out of the landscape.
As they drew closer, Zack’s stomach turned at the sight ahead. The ground around the base of the tower was littered with corpses. Some were old, their forms desiccated and twisted, while others were fresh, still glistening with viscous ichor that pooled in dark puddles. The bodies were unmistakable—the grotesque, jellyfish-like creatures he had encountered before. Many lay split open, their heads cracked as if by immense force, and their tendrils sprawled lifelessly across the ground.
“What the hell happened here?” Zack muttered, his voice catching in his throat.
Nyx fluttered off his shoulder, her wings beating softly as she hovered closer to one of the corpses. “Something’s been killing them,” she said, her tone guarded. “And it’s recent.”
Zack crouched by one of the fresher bodies, inspecting the deep holes in its flesh. The wounds were clean and went right through, as if pieced by something fast. He frowned. “Don’t know what made the holes, something like a gun would be messier on the other side and I don’t know what a spear wound looks like. Also why don’t the bodies disappear?”
Nyx landed lightly beside him, her eyes scanning the carnage. “It’s a warning,” she said softly. “Whatever’s in that tower doesn’t want these things getting close. Maybe it's also preventing them from dissolving?”
Zack’s gaze shifted to the tower itself. It loomed impossibly tall, its polished surface gleaming faintly in the dim light. The lack of seams or visible entrances made it seem alien, unwelcoming. Yet there was an undeniable pull, a magnetic force that drew him forward despite the pit growing in his stomach.
“If it doesn’t want them near, what does that mean for us? ” Zack asked, rising to his feet.
Nyx flicked her tail, and a low hum emanated from the tower. “Maybe we’re about to find out the hard way.”
The corpses nearest the tower shifted slightly, as if responding to the sound. Zack instinctively activated his aura, the faint flames licking at his arms, weaker than he would have liked but still a comforting presence. His eyes narrowed as he studied the structure before him.
A portion of the tower’s surface shimmered faintly, like a mirage. A part of the wall changed. Massive cracks spiderwebbed across the surface, and gigantic gates lay mangled and caved in, their heavy metal frames warped inward as if something had broken in. Pale, bluish light spilled through the shattered gates.
Nyx’s claws gripped his shoulder tightly. “Either way, we’re going in, right?” 
Before Zack could respond, a low rumble reverberated through the air. A deep, rhythmic pounding followed, like a colossal heartbeat echoing across the landscape. The ground beneath his feet began to vibrate, sending small stones skittering across the dirt.
“That’s not good,” Zack muttered, dropping into a fighting stance.
Nyx hissed, her tail lashing as she perched on his shoulder. “Whatever did that to the gates might still be inside. Hope you’re ready.”
“I’m always ready,” Zack said, though his voice betrayed a hint of doubt. He took a steadying breath and moved toward the ruined entrance, his steps slow and deliberate. The gates loomed before them, twisted and sharp. Zack paused just outside, his eyes darting to Nyx. “Stay alert.”
Nyx snorted. “I’m always alert. You’re the one who gets distracted.”
Zack didn’t rise to the bait. Instead, he stepped cautiously over the crumpled remains of the gates and into the tower’s shadowy interior. The bluish light emanated from glowing veins that snaked along the walls. The air was cold, each breath fogging faintly before him.
When they finally got through the wrecked gates another field of corpses greeted them. Hundreds of Jellyfish corpses strew around in various states of wholeness. Beyond them there stretched walls with jagged spikes made entirely of ice. The walls formed a half circle, closing the area off.
“This is a kill box,” Zack realized.
Nyx gave him a look that said to elaborate. Wonderful how broad an expression range her lizard-like face had.
“Someone is defending this tower, the outside gates got breached but they held here and built these walls inside later. The walls are uneven and just look rushed.”
From a smaller gate on the other side of the corpse-strewn field, three towering figures emerged, their massive forms barely making it through the entrance. Each stood nearly three meters tall, their bodies appearing to be made from jagged, crystalline ice. Their faces were featureless masks of frost, but cracks along their bodies glowed with an eerie, pale blue light that pulsed in rhythm with their thundering steps.
Nyx hissed, her tail lashing. “Look at their hands.”
Zack’s eyes flicked to the creatures’ massive, clawed hands. Shards of ice formed and reformed in their palms, growing into spears. One of the creatures raised an arm, and without warning, it hurled the shard toward Zack. He barely had time to dive to the side as the spear shattered against the ground, sending a flurry of razor-sharp fragments flying in all directions.
Another creature raised both arms, and a swirling cloud of ice shards materialized around it. With a wave, the shards hurtled forward in a deadly barrage, forcing Zack tu duck behind a nearby boulder, the sharp, staccato impacts shaking loose bits of stone as he huddled for cover.
Each impact gouged deep, jagged marks into the boulder, and Zack could feel the icy cold radiating through the rock, numbing his exposed skin. When he peeked out, the creature’s glowing cracks pulsed again, gathering energy for another attack.
Zack gritted his teeth and rolled to the side as a hail of ice shards shattered the boulder he had been hiding behind. The air around him was frigid, each breath a painful gasp that burned his lungs. 
One of the Ice Men advanced slowly, its massive frame leaving deep tracks in the ground. A flicker of blue light coursed through the cracks in its body, and Zack’s eyes locked onto them. He darted forward, almost sidestepping another storm of icy projectiles. The shards nicked his arms and legs, leaving shallow cuts that stung in the freezing air.
 Nyx hissed as she swooped low, unleashing a fiery blast that forced one of the creatures to halt its next spell. The flames licked at its crystalline surface but barely left a mark. 
Zack launched himself at the nearest Ice Man, his body blazing with the heat of his Scorching Aura. He drove his fist-blades into the glowing fissure on the creature’s chest. The impact sent a searing wave of heat into the Ice Man, and it shuddered violently, a deep groan reverberating through its form. Cracks spread outward, and the glow dimmed slightly.
The creature retaliated with a swing of its arm. Zack barely ducked in time, the icy limb grazing his shoulder and sending him sprawling to the ground. Pain shot through him, but he rolled to his feet, his fists igniting once more.
Another Ice Man moved in, raising both arms as a swirling vortex of ice shards materialized around it. The shards erupted outward, forcing Zack to dive onto the ground. His back got peppered with the shards from the edge of the cloud. The cold bit into his skin, and he gasped as frost formed on his clothes. Nyx darted above him, her amber eyes sharp with focus.
"Don’t stay down, Zack!” she snapped, launching another burst of fire at the advancing creature. The flames distracted it just long enough for Zack to scramble to his feet.
He clenched his fists, the fire enveloping them roaring brighter.
Charging forward, Zack leapt onto the Ice Man, his fiery blades slamming repeatedly into the glowing fissure along its chest. Each strike sent shards of ice flying, and the creature’s groans grew louder as its movements became sluggish. Finally, with a resounding crack, the Ice Man collapsed, its body shattering into a fine mist that dissolved into the air.
Zack stumbled back, breathing heavily. His arms ached, and his knuckles were raw from the repeated blows. He turned to face the remaining two Ice Men, who had begun to flank him. Their glowing cracks pulsed ominously, and the air grew colder as they prepared another onslaught.
Zack forced himself to his feet, but the fire around his fists sputtered and dimmed. He grimaced, realizing his mana reserves were nearly depleted, he used too much to pummel the Ice Man down. “Nyx,” he called, his voice tight, “I’m tapped out!”
Nyx swooped down, her wings flaring wide. "They blocked the exit! Run deeper into the tower! Now!” she said sharply.
Zack realized Nyx was correct, and started towards the smaller gate. The Ice Men wasted no time. One hurled a stream of shards, but Nyx summoned a shield that hovered behind Zack, keeping him safe. Zack sprinted with everything he had.
“Move faster, Zack!” Nyx snapped, the strain of summoning multiple shields evident in her voice.
The glowing fissures of the Ice Men pulsed brighter, their movements growing more frantic as Zack and Nyx neared the tower. Another wave of shards was sent out and another shield summoned. The projectiles struck the shield, cracks spiderwebbing through its surface. Nyx gritted her teeth, pouring the last of her strength into holding it together.
As Zack reached the gate he dove inside, Nyx following close behind. 
Inside, the air was even colder. Zack ran into an alleyway between buildings.
