

Chapter 11

“S…She’s not d…dead” Kaito said with certainty.
“How do you know?” Zack asked. His inability to save her hit him like a punch to the gut, and the guilt hit him like a punch to the gut.
“I…t could have just slashed her t..throat” Kaito explained. “I…it didn't need the whole ritual”
“Then we need to find her again! Aiko,” Zack said, his voice trembling with urgency. “There has to be a way. We can’t just let that thing take her!”
Aiko’s mind was already working at a frantic pace. The warding stone had been destroyed, which should have lifted the interference. But even now, her locator wasn’t picking up anything beyond the faint traces of the Voidstalker’s lingering presence. She glanced at Zack, who was still clutching the coin that the tanuki rolled at them at the hot spring.
“Z…Zack,” Kaito said suddenly, his eyes narrowing in thought. “That coin… there were t..two, right? Rei has the other one?”
Zack nodded, hope flickering in his eyes. “Yeah, we each have one. What about it?”
“From what I understand about the locator it track magic signatures? Like specific radio wavelengths?” Kaito looked to Aiko for confirmation and the woman nodded ”Then, if the coins are connected, like a set… then maybe, just maybe, we can use one to find the other.”
Aiko took a deep breath, trying to calm her nerves. “That could work. If the coins have a similar signature we can use the one we have to configure the locator to search for that specifically. It might lead us to Rei.”
Zack didn’t hesitate. He gave the coin to Aiko, the metal warm from being held so tightly. “Do it, Aiko. We don’t have time to waste.”
Aiko nodded, her hands already moving to adjust the settings on the locator. She worked quickly, her fingers flying over the device as she recalibrated it to tune into the coin’s unique energy signature. Kaito leaned over her shoulder, and helped her as much as he could.
The locator beeped softly, the screen flickering as it tried to pick up the faint trace of the matching coin.
Zack tried not to hover, his breath shallow as he watched them work. He could feel his heart pounding in his chest, each beat a reminder of the seconds ticking by—seconds that Rei didn’t have.
The locator beeped again, louder this time, the screen showing a faint, pulsing dot. Kaito’s straightened up, staring at the readout. “I’ve got something. It’s weak, but it’s there.”
“Is it Rei?” Zack asked, his voice tight.
Kaito didn’t look up, his focus entirely on the locator. “It’s her coin. The signal is faint, but it’s getting stronger the more I fine-tune it. She’s close.”
Zack’s jaw tightened as he glanced around the darkening forest. “How close?”
Kaito adjusted the locator again, the beeping becoming more rapid. “A mile, maybe less. But the signal’s unstable. We need to move fast before we lose it.”
“No we this time Kaito, you need to get back to the inn, if we get into a fight with that Voidstalker we won’t be able to protect you” Aiko said.
“B…but” Kaito stumbled out.
“Aiko is right, sorry but you have to get to safety, friend.” Zack put a hand on Kaito’s shoulder. “Sorry to say but you're no help in a direct fight. Prepare some first aid supplies for when we get back, ok? You already helped a lot with the locator.”
Kaito nodded with resolve in his eyes. “G…good luck” He turned and ran towards the inn.
Zack took off in the direction the locator indicated, his body full of adrenaline. Aiko was right beside him, their feet pounding against the forest floor. 
The locator’s beeping grew louder again, more insistent, guiding them through the dense underbrush and over the rough terrain. Zack’s breath came in ragged gasps, but he didn’t slow down. 
Suddenly, the beeping slowed, then faltered, as if struggling to maintain the signal. Aiko’s heart skipped a beat as she adjusted the locator again, her fingers shaking. “The signal’s getting weaker—something’s blocking it!”
“We can’t lose her!” Zack shouted, panic creeping into his voice. He pushed himself harder, refusing to let the weakness to take over.

They rounded a bend, the trees thinning out to reveal a rocky outcrop that overlooked a steep drop into a narrow ravine. The air was cooler here, the wind whipping through the trees, carrying with it a faint, sickly-sweet scent that Zack immediately recognized.
“There!” Aiko pointed to a small, narrow cave entrance partially hidden by the rocky terrain. The locator’s beeping was rapid now, the signal strongest here.
Zack didn’t hesitate. He scrambled down the slope, rocks shifting under his feet as he made his way to the cave. The darkness inside was oppressive, but he could feel it—Rei was close.
Zack realized it could be a trap, yet again, but he was beyond caution. He edged into the cave, the darkness swallowing him whole. His heart pounded in his ears, the sound almost drowning out the rapid beeping of the locator. He felt the coin in his pocket grow warm again. Zack stopped at the entrance of the cave.
The fox demon emerged from the shadows, its eyes gleaming with malevolent glee. It stood just beyond the reach of the faint light filtering into the cave, its grin wider than ever.
“Well, well,” the demon purred, “What do we have here? The brave little human coming to rescue his damsel in distress? How quaint.” The demon’s voice was full of sarcasm, each word drawn out as it relished every syllable.
Zack’s muscles tensed as he kept his gaze locked on the demon. He summoned his Armor of Flame, Scorching Aura, Flame Talon he lit up the whole cave, casting dancing shadows onto its walls. “Let her go,” he demanded, trying to keep his voice steady. “This isn’t a game.”
The demon’s grin widened impossibly further, its sharp teeth gleaming in the light. “Oh, but it is a game, my dear human. Life, death, the chase… it’s all one big, delightful game! You’re playing it right now, and you’re not winning.” The demon began to pace back and forth, its tail flicking behind it as it spoke. “ You see, I’ve been watching you and your little friends, watching as you stumbled around. And now, here you are. How exciting!”
Aiko moved to flank Zack. Aiko was wearing her ice armor and had a halo of icicles floating around her, ready to strike at any moment. “You’ve made a mistake taking one of ours,”
The demon let out a high-pitched, cackling laugh that echoed through the cave, the sound bouncing off the walls in a disorienting way. “Mistake? Oh no, my dear, this is all part of the fun! Humans are so serious, always worrying about right and wrong, life and death. You never stop to enjoy the mischief, the chaos!” Its voice dropped to a low, conspiratorial whisper. “But me? I live for it.”
Zack felt his pulse quicken, and his cheeks flushed red. The demon’s casual disregard for Rei’s life, fueled a fire in him. He took a step forward, his fists clenched. “I’m done playing,” he growled, his voice low. “We’re taking Rei back, and if you try to stop us, you die.”
The Voidstalker paused, tilting its head as if considering Zack’s words. Then, with a flick of its wrist, it conjured a small, glowing orb of light in its palm. “Those are fighting words, friend” it said, its voice full of amusement. “And I do love a good fight.”
With a sudden, sharp movement, the demon hurled the orb toward Zack. He barely had time to react, dodging to the side as the orb exploded against the cave wall, sending shards of rock flying. 
When Zack looked up everything had changed. They stood on cracked, barren earth under a blood-red sky. The air shimmered with heat, and the horizon was warped as if by a desert mirage. Zack stumbled, disoriented, and even Aiko paused.
With the corner of his eye Zack saw a claw striking from the side. He moved to dodge only to run face first into rock. He heard the Voidstalker cackle again.
“It’s an illusion,” Aiko muttered, raising her staff. Ice crackled as she took a slow breath. The air around them grew frigid, and the edges of the mirage nearest to them blurred, revealing the cave underneath. The monster did not move from its spot and Zack and Aiko were still standing at the entrance. Zack just dodged straight into a wall.
The voidstalker had a look of surprise on its face but it was gone quickly. It dispelled the illusion of an open plain and darted forward, faster than Zack could track. Aiko reacted instantly, slamming her staff into the ground. A jagged wall of ice erupted in its path, forcing it to leap to the side. Nine tails fanned out from under its kimono, and suddenly, there were three foxes, identical and snarling.
“Zack!” Aiko snapped. “Cover me!”
Zack nodded and stepped forward, his hands ablaze. He swung wildly at the closest fox, but his fists hit nothing but air. One of the illusions vanished, but the other two darted around him, their movements synchronized.
“Damn it!” he growled, frustrated. He turned to see the two foxes lunging at Aiko. She spun, her staff glowing, and unleashed a flurry of ice shards. Each shard homed in on one of the foxes. When they hit the illusions they disappeared, but within seconds new ones appeared.
“How do we find the real one?!” Zack yelled, dodging another clawed swipe without knowing if it was actually there.
“Focus!” Aiko shouted. “Use your senses! Feel the heat it gives off.”
Zack steadied his breathing, centering himself as he had during their training. He let the warmth of his core expand, using it to feel the air around him. Aiko was right! The illusions were cold! He tuned out the illusions and honed in on the faint heat signature moving through the air.
When two foxes lunged again, Zack sidestepped, ignoring one of the attacks. It passed through him, harmlessly. He retaliated on the other fox with a fiery uppercut. His flames grazed its flank. The creature hissed in pain, and leapt back.
“That’s it!” Aiko called, her voice sharp. She raised her staff high, conjuring a massive cloud of ice spears above her head. The frost glittered ominously, and the air around them dropped several degrees. “Keep it distracted, Zack!”
Zack closed the distance, forcing the fox to focus on the melee and abandon the full size illusions. His fists blazed with fire as he swung, aiming for the creature’s face. The fox ducked under his strike, its tails whipping out like lashing serpents. One struck Zack square in the chest, knocking him back a step.
He barely regained his footing before the fox lunged again. Zack managed to block with his flaming arms, but the impact was jarring. Pain shot up his arms and his muscles screamed in protest as he tried to counter with a fiery punch, but the fox moved faster, circling and raking its claws across his side.
The ground in front of him seemed to ripple, but Zack didn’t notice until it was too late. He stepped forward, and his foot plunged into what he thought was solid ground. A hole, disguised by the fox’s illusion, sent him sprawling. The Voidstalker struck from behind, its claws leaving bloody gashes across his back.
“Damn it!” Zack snarled, wrenching his foot free and rolling away.
Pain seared through Zack’s body, and his flames flickered unsteadily. He staggered, trying to stay upright. The fox darted in and out, its movements a blur, landing blows while Zack struggled to land even a single hit.
Each time Zack threw a punch, the fox would blur momentarily, as if phasing through his attack. His fist grazed it, but the timing felt wrong—as if he'd punched too late. He couldn’t follow the heat signature and fight at the same time. The fox's laughter reverberated in his mind as it lashed out again, knocking him back. 
Finally Zack managed to figure out the trick. The fox was overlaying its form with a slightly slower, mirrored image hiding its true body at the same time. Zack’s punches landed just behind the real fox, missing by fractions of a second. His frustration boiled over as he swung wildly, his flames flaring uselessly.
The fox danced around him, its illusions throwing off his sense of timing. His strikes became more desperate as exhaustion set in, each missed attack costing him precious energy. Its claws nicked his legs and arms, and he could feel blood soaking into his clothes, mixing with sweat.
Just as Zack thought he’d adjusted to the trick, the fox used another one. The ground directly in front of him seemed to rise like a solid wall. Instinctively, he blocked, but the fake barrier passed through him. The fox was right behind the illusion and slammed into him, sending him sprawling.
For the first time in the fight, doubt crept into Zack’s mind. He wasn’t strong, fast or experienced enough to match this creature. He was way over his head. Each second he survived felt like borrowed time.
He glanced at Aiko, who was still gathering power for her ice spears. The fox used the opening mercilessly. It let out an unearthly howl, and a wave of energy burst from its maw. Zack’s instincts screamed at him to move, but the wave expanded too quickly. It caught him mid-leap, and he was flung backward, landing hard on the ground.
The world around him was muffled, his ears ringing painfully. He touched his face, realizing blood was trickling from his ears. His vision blurred, but he could see the fox lunging toward him, its maw open grotesquely wide.
“No!” Aiko’s voice cut through the haze, sharp and furious. She stepped between Zack and the advancing fox, slamming her staff into the ground. A protective dome of ice rose around them, but it cracked almost instantly under the fox’s assault.
“I can’t keep up with him.” Zack grimaced at the pain and the realization that Aiko had to abandon her spell to save him.
Aiko shook her head, her expression concerned. “Stay down. I’ll finish this.”
“No,” Zack said, his voice firm. He pushed himself to his feet, wobbling but determined. “I’ve got an idea.”
The slowly circled the dome, its tails swaying hypnotically. He lazily threw the exploding orb he opened the fight with. Aiko’s barrier was weakening, the cracks spreading like spiderwebs.
“Come on out and play some more” the fox teased.
“Aiko,” Zack whispered, hoping the monster won’t hear him “Freeze the ground around us, it doesn’t have to do anything, but make it flashy.”
Aiko hesitated for a split second, then nodded, trusting him. The moment the dome crumbled she raised her staff, and frost spread rapidly across the battlefield. The fox leaped to avoid the encroaching ice, but that was exactly what Zack was waiting for. The moment its paws left the ground again, he surged forward..
“RAAAAH!” Zack yelled.
With the fox unable to alter its path mid-air Zack knew exactly where it would be. The voidstalker tried the same tactic with an overlaying illusion but when not in direct combat Zack managed to track the real body’s heat.
Zack pushed the Devouring Fire and Armor of Flame to their very limit. He dumped all his remaining mana into them. Flames roared around his fists. He struck the fox just as it landed. The flames wrapped around the creature, searing its fur and forcing it to the ground. The creature screeched in pain and anger.
Aiko seized the opening. With a shout, she hurled a spear of ice. It flew straight and true, piercing the fox’s chest with a sickening crunch. The creature let out one final, echoing screech as its body dissolved into a shower of glowing embers.
Zack rushed beside Rei, his breath still coming in ragged gasps from the fight. Her normally vibrant features were pale, her blue hair matted against her pale forehead. She looked serene, almost peaceful, but Zack knew better. Her chest rose and fell steadily, but there was no reaction to his voice or touch. He shook her shoulder. “Rei… come on, wake up. ” 
He pressed his fingers to the side of her neck, finding a faint but steady pulse. Relief flooded him, but it was short-lived. Her skin was icy to the touch, far colder than even Aiko’s frost aura. 
“Aiko, what’s happening to her? She’s freezing.”
Aiko stepped closer, her expression grim as she knelt on the opposite side of Rei. She extended her hand over Rei’s body, a faint blue glow emanating from her palm. “It’s not the cold you’re feeling,” Aiko murmured. “Her spirit energy is trapped, locked in some kind of magical stasis. It’s keeping her unconscious.” Her voice was tense.
Zack clenched his fists. He glanced down at Rei’s face, willing her to wake. “So how do we bring her back?” he demanded, his voice cracking. 
Aiko’s gaze hardened as she met his eyes. “I don’t know.”
