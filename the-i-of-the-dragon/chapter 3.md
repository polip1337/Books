Chapter 3

A couple of days of rest later, Zack was riding behind Aiko on her bike, his arms wrapped around the pretty girl. She was taking him to a part of the forest that she dubbed her training area. Zack's stomach knotted, his grip on Aiko tightening. The surreal events in Shibuya, the awakening of his powers, and meeting Aiko had all led him to this point.

They apparently arrived at the spot and it was time to train.

"Are you ready?" Aiko asked, already bouncing on her heels. Zack noted the way sunlight caught her silver hair, making it shimmer.

Zack rolled his shoulders and nodded. "As ready as I’ll ever be."

The area was quiet and isolated, perfect for the training Zack needed. Various equipment was scattered around: wooden dummies, targets, and what looked like an obstacle course.

"We’ll start with the basics," Aiko said, walking to the center of the field. "Before you can control your powers, you need to understand them."

Zack followed her. "How do we do that?"

Aiko turned to face him, the cute girl gone, replaced with a serious drill instructor. "With focus and control. The first thing you need to learn is how to channel your spirit energy. It’s the foundation of everything else."

She demonstrated a simple breathing exercise, standing with her feet shoulder-width apart, eyes closed, hands by her sides. "Watch closely," she instructed.

Zack observed as Aiko took slow, deliberate breaths. Gradually, a soft blue light began to emanate from her hands, growing brighter with each breath. He had a flashback to when he saw her at the hospital and thought her an angel. The air felt colder as her aura expanded. After a few moments, she opened her eyes and extinguished the light.

"Now you try, feel the power inside you and try to bring it to the surface." she said, stepping back to give him space.

Zack positioned himself as she had, feeling a bit self-conscious. He closed his eyes and tried to mimic her breathing pattern. At first, nothing happened. He opened one eye, glancing at Aiko, who nodded encouragingly.

"Relax and focus on your breath. Let the energy flow through you, work with it, don’t try to force it" she advised.

Zack closed his eyes again, concentrating. He inhaled deeply, feeling a warmth build in his chest. As he exhaled, he imagined that warmth traveling down his arms and into his hands. To his surprise, he felt a tingling sensation in his palms. Opening his eyes, he saw the air shimmered with heat around his right hand.

This would not do. It was not even a flickering candle compared to the inferno he’d felt at Shibuya.

Zack refocused on the warmth coursing through his body, trying to increase the flow. He took another deep breath, imagining he was feeding the flames, making them grow hotter and more powerful. He tried to replicate the feeling of all encompassing flame he had during his last fight. As he exhaled, the tingling in his hands intensified. He opened his eyes and saw faint, flickering flame dancing around his right hand.

Aiko leaned forward, mouth falling open. "That's it, Zack! You're doing it! This usually takes a lot longer. Now try to do both hands."

Zack nodded, a grin spreading across his face. He closed his eyes again, focusing on his core, but this time directing the energy equally to both hands. He imagined a steady flow of power moving through his chest, down his arms, and into his palms. The heat built up, growing more intense with each breath. When he opened his eyes, flames were flickering around both hands, brighter and more substantial than before.

"Excellent, Zack," Aiko said, her voice steady and warm. "Now, try to sustain it. Keep the flames steady and controlled."

He could feel the power within him, a raw, untamed energy that needed to be harnessed. He concentrated on steadying the flames, keeping them from flaring out of control. The flow was unpredictable, it flared with intensity just to decrease to a trickle in the next moment. It took all of Zack’s concentration to keep it more or less even.

Aiko watched closely, her gaze locked on his flames. "You're doing great. Remember, control is key. The flames are a part of you, an extension of your will. Guide them, don’t let them guide you."

Hours passed in deep concentration. As he practiced, Zack felt a growing sense of control. He no longer struggled with managing the flow. He could make them grow larger or smaller with just a thought. Already, he felt more confident and in tune with his power.

Aiko clapped her hands together. "You're a natural, Zack. Keep practicing, and you'll only get better. Now, let's try something different, focus on creating flames not on your body but next to it, like how I create ice for ranged attacks."

She created a small icicle and with a flick of her wrist sent it into the closest target’s bullseye.

Zack nodded, eager to expand his abilities. He extended his right hand, concentrating on generating flames in the air beside him. He envisioned the heat moving outward from his body, forming a ball of fire in mid-air. He strained, feeling the energy surging through him, but nothing happened. The flames stubbornly clung to his hand, refusing to move away from his body.

He tried again, straining his control, but the result was the same. But it was like trying to move a muscle that just wasn’t there. The flames remained attached to his skin, flickering brightly but failing to separate. Frustrated, he glanced at Aiko.

She approached him, a sympathetic look on her face. "It's okay, Zack. Not everyone can create ranged attacks. Your power is more suited for getting up close, and personal."

Zack frowned, more than a bit disappointed. "So, no fireballs for me?"

Aiko laughed gently. "Not exactly. You might have some limited ranged options with practice, but you're what we call a melee type. Your true strength lies in enhancing your physical abilities. The flames can be used to empower your body, making you faster, stronger, and tougher."

Zack raised his flaming fist, and closed it slowly “Punching that voidstalker did feel… right.”

Aiko laughed again, patting his shoulder. "Your power is unique to you. It is an expression of your soul so it should fit you really well."

Aiko then shifted their training to martial arts. "Now, Zack, let's see how you can integrate your flames with some basic martial arts. Follow my lead."

She demonstrated a series of movements, combining strikes and blocks with quick footwork. Zack’s jaw went slack before he attempted to mimic her. His movements were clumsy, lacking the precision and grace of Aiko's.

Aiko noticed his struggle and offered encouragement. "Don't worry, Zack. Martial arts takes time and practice. Focus on the basics first."

Zack nodded, trying to follow her instructions. He felt awkward, his limbs not quite moving the way he wanted them to. His strikes were off-target, and his blocks were too slow. The flames flickered erratically around his hands, reflecting his lack of control. A couple of times they went out completely. It was hard to focus on both things at once.

Aiko observed Zack carefully, her eyes sharp. She stepped closer, adjusting his stance with gentle nudges. "Keep your feet shoulder-width apart. Balance is key," she instructed.

Zack tried to follow her guidance, planting his feet more firmly, feeling the ground beneath him.

Aiko continued demonstrating the basic movements, breaking them down into smaller, manageable parts. "Watch my hands," she said, executing a slow, deliberate punch. "And remember to breathe. Your breath guides your energy."

Zack mimicked her movements, focusing on the rhythm of his breathing. He felt a bit more stable, but his punches still lacked power and precision. Aiko smiled encouragingly. "Better. Now, let's work on your blocks."

She showed him a basic block, moving her arm fluidly to intercept an imaginary attack. Zack followed suit, his movements stiff but improving. "Good. Relax your shoulders. Let the movement flow naturally," Aiko advised, demonstrating again.

They continued practicing, Aiko patiently correcting Zack's form and providing constant feedback. "Don't rush. Speed will come with time. Right now, focus on getting it right."

Zack nodded, repeating the movements over and over. Gradually, he started to feel a bit more comfortable. His strikes became more accurate, and his blocks quicker.

After a while, Aiko called for a break. "Let's take five," she said, offering Zack a water bottle. "You're doing well, Zack."

Zack accepted the water, panting slightly. "Thanks, Aiko. It's harder than it looks."

She chuckled softly. "It always is. Just keep practicing, and don't get discouraged."

"Thanks," Zack replied, taking a sip. "But I still have a long way to go."

"True," Aiko agreed. "But you’re making progress. That’s what matters."

Zack looked at her, tilting his head. "Aiko, how did you get involved with all of this? The Dragon Guard, the powers... everything."

Aiko’s gaze drifted to the treeline, and she was quiet for a moment before speaking. “My parents were both members of the Dragon Guard. They died protecting Tokyo from a powerful Voidstalker when I was a child. I was raised by the organization, and trained to take up their mantle. It’s been my life for as long as I can remember.”

Zack felt a pang of empathy. “I’m sorry. That must have been hard.”

“It was,” she admitted, her voice barely above a whisper. “But it also gave me a purpose. I know the risks, but I also know what’s at stake. Protecting people, keeping them safe from these threats, it’s worth it.”

Zack had a new appreciation for the woman, not only was she beautiful she had a strength to match.

Aiko stood up. "We’ve rested enough. Let's have a short spar. It'll help you integrate what we've been practicing."

They moved back to the center of the training area, assuming their stances. "Remember, Zack, focus on the basics. Don't overthink it," she advised.

Zack nodded, steadying himself. Aiko initiated the spar with a series of quick jabs and low kicks, each one measured to test his reactions. Zack managed to block some of her strikes, though his counters were slow and imprecise. Aiko offered continuous feedback, her voice guiding him through each movement.

"Don't just block, Zack. Look for openings," she said, dodging one of his punches and tapping his shoulder lightly. "Keep your guard up."

Zack adjusted, trying to anticipate her movements. As the spar continued, he settled into a rhythm, his blocks and strikes becoming more synchronized.

Aiko increased the intensity slightly, pushing Zack to his limits. Suddenly, heat flared through Zack’s chest, locking his joints.

You’ve reached level 1.

Zack stopped, confused, he looked at Aiko with wide eyes.

"What just happened?" he asked, breathless and bewildered.

Aiko approached him, her expression proud. "You leveled up, Zack. I hoped it might happen today, you probably got a lot of experience killing that voidstalker and grounded it with training. Got a surprise for you. Focus on your core again."

Zack did just that, feeling the ember of power nestled within. He felt a shift, as if he was being pulled inward, deeper into himself.

When he opened his eyes, Zack found himself standing inside of a vast caldera, the heart of a dormant volcano. The air was thick with the scent of sulfur, and the ground beneath his feet was warm, pulsating with latent energy. Molten lava bubbled and hissed in the distance, illuminating the space with a reddish glow.

“What the hell?” Zack muttered.

Even though the place was clearly dangerous Zack felt safe. Like the place belonged to him. He decided to explore a bit. As he ventured further into the caldera, he marveled at the rugged beauty surrounding him. Jagged rocks and crystalline formations jutted from the earth, glowing with an inner fire.

Streams of lava carved paths through the rocky terrain, he was able to feel their heat even from a distance. There was an undeniable sense of power here, an energy that resonated with his very soul.

Exploring deeper, Zack noticed a peculiar formation in the center of the caldera. A pedestal of blackened stone stood there, and atop it rested a large, black egg. The egg was covered in red cracks, like its surface was made of lava. It called to him, beckoning him closer.

Zack approached the pedestal cautiously, his heart pounding with anticipation. He reached out, feeling the warmth radiating from the egg, and gently lifted it from its resting place. The moment his fingers closed around the egg, warmth flooded through him, and for a moment the egg’s pulse matched his own heartbeat.

The world around him began to blur and fade, and Zack felt himself being pulled back to reality. He opened his eyes and found himself back in the real world, still holding the egg. It was warm to the touch, its surface had the same fiery patterns he had seen in the caldera.

He stared at the egg in wonder. He raised his eyes to Aiko

“What is this?” Zack asked, breathless.

"That," Aiko said, nodding towards the egg, "is your boon."

"My what?" Zack asked, still trying to process everything that had happened.

"Every person who unlocks their spiritual energy and reaches level one receives a boon," Aiko explained. "It's a gift from the universe, a manifestation of your inner strength. For some, it's a piece of armor or a weapon, like my staff. In rare cases, it's a familiar."

"A familiar?" Zack echoed dumbly, glancing down at the egg in his hands.

"Yes," Aiko confirmed. "A familiar is a powerful ally, a creature that bonds with you.. They are rare and incredibly valuable."

Zack’s hands tightened around the egg. "So, this egg... it’ll hatch into a familiar?"

Aiko nodded. "It's likely. You’ll need to take care of it. The bond between a summoner and their familiar is deep and profound. It will take time and effort to fully realize its potential."

Zack looked at the egg, turning it slowly in his hands. "Why do you think I got a familiar instead of a weapon or armor?"

Aiko's smile grew. "It means you have great potential, Zack. The familiar will be a reflection of your spirit, your strengths, and your potential."

“What will it be like?” Zack asked.

This time Aiko straight up laughed. “Why do you think we're called the Dragon Guard?”
