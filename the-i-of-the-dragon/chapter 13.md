
Chapter 13
The room was as silent as a tomb. Aiko, Rei, Kaito, and Zack remained frozen. Every muscle in Zack’s body screamed at him to react, to move, to do anything but it was as if Shiro’s gaze pinned him down.
Then Shiro burst out laughing.
Loud and unabashed, his laughter echoed through the room like an explosion, shattering the stillness. He doubled over, clutching his sides, while everyone else stared at him in bewilderment.
“You should have seen your faces!” Shiro wheezed, barely able to catch his breath. “Priceless! Absolutely priceless! Disappearing? Come on, do you think I’m that dramatic?”
Aiko’s eyebrow twitched dangerously. “You were messing with us?” Her voice rose, sharp with incredulity. “You can’t just drop cryptic threats like that and then laugh it off!”
“I can and I did,” Shiro replied smugly, straightening up. “A little levity never hurt anyone, Aiko. Though I guess in your case, that's a first.”
“You’re insufferable!” Aiko snapped, crossing her arms. “You think this is funny?”
Aiko took a calming breath. “What did he actually mean, Shiro?”
Shiro’s grin faded slightly, though the twinkle in his eyes remained. “Fine, fine. I’ll get to the point. You’re not disappearing permanently. You three—Aiko, Rei, and Kaito—are coming with me to Dragon Guard HQ.”
“What?” Zack cut in. “Why only them?”
“Aiko needs to debrief; the higher-ups are going to want her perspective on some recent... Tokyo … events.” His eyes flicked toward Zack meaningfully but didn’t elaborate. “Rei, well, it’s kind of obvious, isn’t it? We can take better care of her there.”
“And Kaito?” Aiko asked.
Shiro’s expression turned serious, all traces of humor vanishing. ”He’s insurance.”
“Insurance for what?” Zack demanded.
“For your return” Shiro looked at Zack with that golden eye again.
“Speak clearly!” Zack’s fist clenched. “What do you mean?”
Shiro flashed a wide grin. “You, my friend, get the honor of retrieving the Essence Crystal. It’s a shiny little trinket with a lot of power, hidden in a place called the Void.”
“The Void?” Zack repeated, his heart sinking. “That doesn’t exactly sound like a vacation spot.”
“It’s not,” Shiro confirmed cheerfully. “It’s a... challenging environment, to say the least. But don’t worry! If the amount of power I sense is correct you have a shot.”
“Wait, hold on,” Aiko interrupted. “You’re splitting us up and sending Zack on a solo mission to the Void? Have you completely lost it?”
Shiro tilted his head, a picture of calm. “Relax, Aiko. There is literally no other choice. We will have our hands full of explaining those two. But someone with Zack’s raw power is not gonna get a pass.”
Aiko’s lips pressed into a thin line. “This is stupid.”
“It’s calculated,” Shiro corrected smoothly. “And elegant. We avoid scrutiny and get what we need to heal your friend. Zack can handle it. You sense the same energy level as I do.”
Zack, still processing the sudden shift in plans, glanced at Aiko. Her frustration was palpable, but beneath it was a flicker of worry that mirrored his own. He gave her a small, reassuring nod.
“I’ll be fine, Rei needs the crystal” he said, more to convince himself than anyone else. “You’ve got your part to play, and I’ve got mine.”
Aiko let out a resigned sigh, shaking her head. “Just don’t get yourself killed, okay?”
Shiro clapped his hands together, grinning once more. “Great! Now that that’s settled, let’s get moving. Time’s ticking, and the Void won’t wait forever.”
As the group began to prepare, Shiroshot one last glare at Aiko. “You owe me for this, Aiko.”
Aiko nodded with a sigh.
“You need to grab anything Zack? There is no time to waste, HQ will soon wonder why I'm gone so long” Shiro asked.
“Yeah, gimme a sec.” Zack rushed to his own room where he left the egg.

He picked up the backpack and tightened the straps, the egg safely nestled inside a padded compartment he had thrown together with a few old towels. The weight of it felt oddly reassuring against his back. He adjusted the bag, testing its security, and glanced around his room one last time. 
When he returned to the main room, Shiro’s eyes immediately zeroed in on the backpack. His gaze lingered for just a moment too long, his expression unreadable, but he said nothing. Zack resisted the urge to fidget under the scrutiny.
"Ready?" Shiro asked, his usual smirk in place.
"Ready as I'll ever be," Zack replied, trying to sound more confident than he felt.
Shiro clapped him on the shoulder. "That’s the spirit. Let me just open the portal." He grabbed a small cube from the inside of his pocket and threw it on the ground. It glowed with runes and started unfolding. Zack watched as the sides of the cube just kept replicating and growing, slowly taking a shape of a door.
Aiko approached Zack, her expression a mixture of worry and determination. “It will take a moment to open. Before you go, there’s something you need to understand about the Void.”
Zack nodded, his full attention on her.
“The Void,” she began, “isn’t an alternate dimension. It’s where Voidstalkers drag parts of a world when they destabilize reality enough. Picture an archipelago where each island is torn from a different place. That’s the Void.”
“So... they just hang there?” Zack asked, his stomach twisting at the thought.
“Sort of,” Aiko confirmed. “They are also constantly crumbling, getting consumed by the void that surrounds them.”
“Great,” Zack muttered. “Sounds like the perfect place for a relaxing stroll.”
Aiko gave him a sharp look. “This isn’t a joke, Zack. The Void is dangerous. You’ll need to stay focused and trust your instincts. Each island should have its own Essence Crystal, usually in a central location.”
Zack swallowed hard. “Location? That's vague.”
“It should be clear,” Aiko said, her voice soft. “It will be a mountain, a tower, a shrine. You should have no trouble finding the place at least.”
“Any idea what the crystal looks like?” Zack asked.
“It’s hard to miss,” Aiko replied. “It glows, like the embers of a dying fire, and it’s shaped like a double hexagonal pyramid.”

“A what?” Zack asked in confusion.

“T-think a d12 dice.” Kaito helpfully added.
“Oooh” Zack said, getting it now. Who knew those board games would come in useful.
Shiro stepped between them, cutting off Aiko’s lecture. “It will be fine. Glowy, hovering crystal. Finding or recognizing it is not the issue anyway.”
Aiko’s lips thinned. “It will be protected.”
“You’ll head out as soon as the portal stabilizes,” Shiro said to Zack. “In the meantime, get your head straight. The Void isn’t kind to hesitation.”
Zack nodded, processing fast. He glanced at Aiko one last time, and she placed a reassuring hand on his shoulder.
“You’ve got this,” she said firmly. “And if you don’t, I’ll kill you myself for dying.”
Shiro laughed, though there was a flicker of unease in his eyes. “I’ll hold you to that, Aiko.”
Zack felt a small smile tug at his lips despite himself. With one final deep breath, he followed Shiro out of the room, the weight of the egg in his backpack grounding him as he prepared to step into the unknown.
“No turning back,” he muttered, his voice barely audible over the hum emanating from the gate. 
With a final glance over his shoulder, he stepped forward. The moment his hand touched the gate, it rippled like water. The world around him twisted, colors blending and swirling in a vortex of light. He felt a pull, as if something was drawing him into an endless expanse.
Then, silence.
Zack stumbled forward, the ground beneath his boots soft and uneven. He looked up and his breath caught in his chest as he took in the world around him. A torrential downpour was frozen in place—millions of raindrops suspended mid-air, glinting faintly in the dim, bluish light that seemed to emanate from everywhere and nowhere all at once.
The rain wasn’t the only thing suspended. Streams of mist hung in mid-motion, twisting like snakes made of vapor. A thick fog clung to the horizon, its edges constantly shifting and curling, as if it were alive. Above, an ocean of stormclouds churned in unmoving chaos, lightning arcs between them stopped in time. 
He reached out, touching one of the suspended raindrops. It was cool and solid, like glass. It provided resistance and when he pushed a bit, the drop shattered, splintering into fragments before dissolving into mist.
“This is...” he whispered, his voice trailing off as he turned in a slow circle. ”Beautiful.”
Zack adjusted the straps of his backpack, where the black-and-red egg rested securely. “Okay, time to move. Need to save Rei.”
The ground beneath him was firm enough to hold his weight. It was slippery, as if covered in a thin sheet of ice. The chill in the air bit at his skin, and he instinctively activated his Armor of Flame, fire flickering to life around his body. The effect was immediate—and alarming.
The fire sputtered, dimming almost instantly, as if the atmosphere itself was actively smothering it. Zack grimaced, clenching his fists and trying to force the flames to flare, but they remained weak, struggling against the cold.
"Not good," Zack muttered, glancing warily around. If his fire was this feeble, he’d need to rely on strategy more than brute force to survive.
A haunting, hollow wail rose and fell in the distance. It felt unrelenting, digging into Zack’s bones and making the air feel heavier. The sound seemed to echo all around him, growing louder with each passing moment.
Then the rain began to move.
Not all of it—just small patches. Some of the suspended droplets began to ripple slightly, vibrating in place before moving up, as though they were rewinding. Zack’s heart raced as he noticed the patterns forming in the rain—a circular ripple here, a flowing spiral there.
The Voidstalker stepped into view, its presence warping the rain around it like a gravitational pull. It was a tall, gangly creature with a translucent, jellyfish-like body. Its torso shimmered with hues of blue and gray, veins of black pulsing just beneath the surface. Its limbs were impossibly long and thin, tapering into claw-like fingers that cut through the frozen rain like blades.
The creature moved fluidly through the rain. Where it stepped, the droplets turned to mist, swirled around its limbs before snapping back into place.
Zack’s stomach churned. The Voidstalker’s movements weren’t just wrong—they were off. Time itself seemed to stutter and flicker around the creature, and Zack could feel it like a dull ache in his head.
"Fantastic," Zack muttered, activating Scorching Aura in desperation. The heat radiated from his body, faint tendrils of steam rising as the frozen environment resisted his fire. His aura was weaker than he’d ever felt it, but it was enough to keep him focused.
The Voidstalker cocked its head, as if studying Zack. Then it moved.
It was a blur—one moment standing still, the next streaking toward him, its long limbs slicing through the air like scythes. Zack barely had time to react, diving to the side as one of the creature’s claws slammed into the ground where he had been standing. The impact sent ripples through the frozen rain, droplets shattering into icy shards.
Zack scrambled to his feet, flames flickering weakly around the blades extending from his fists. He lashed out, punching toward the creature with all his strength. His flames connected, cutting the Voidstalker’s translucent body, but the heat dissipated almost immediately, as though the creature’s very presence drained the energy from his attack.
The Voidstalker recoiled slightly, but the shallow cut wasn’t enough to deter it. It lunged again, its claws swiping in a wide arc. Zack ducked under the attack, the claws slicing through a nearby patch of rain, the droplets providing no resistance.
Then the creature jumped back and stopped.
Zack froze, confused as the Voidstalker stood motionless. The wailing sound returned, louder and more chaotic this time. Zack’s pulse raced as he realized the rain around him was moving again; It was rewinding and so was Zack.
The suspended droplets shifted, pushing Zack, erasing his recent steps and leaving him stumbling to regain his footing. With a flick of its claws, the Voidstalker manipulated the reversing rain, pulling it into a tightening spiral that swirled around Zack like a draining whirlpool, dragging him toward the creature.
Panic flared in Zack’s chest as he struggled against the pull. He slammed his fists together, forcing his Armor of Flame to reignite, the weak fire barely holding as he broke free from the spiral’s grip.
"Shit," Zack hissed through clenched teeth. "Of course you can control the rain."
The Voidstalker let out a low, resonant growl. The howling in the background grew louder, drowning out Zack’s thoughts.
The Voidstalker’s translucent body shimmered faintly as it tilted its head. The frozen rain around them glinted in the dim light, hanging like a million tiny blades poised to strike. The frigid air gnawed at the heat, sapping Zack’s strength. He squared his shoulders, his fists clenching as he prepared for the next attack. 
The Voidstalker didn’t wait. It spun in place, its tendrils lashed out in an arc, cutting through the frozen droplets. He dove to the ground, the nearest tendril slicing just above him. Where it passed, the droplets shattered into fine ice shards that rained down on his back.
Zack rolled to his feet, slipping slightly on the icy ground. The monster moved its claw in a downward motion and the frozen droplets launched toward Zack. He got struck with one shard and swatted another with his weakened Armor of Flame.
The Voidstalker kept up the attack, spinning its tendrils like a deadly cyclone. Zack braced himself and darted to the side, but the slick ground and hanging droplets slowed his movements. A tendril whipped against his shoulder, the force sending him staggering into the icy wall of frozen rain.
“Damn it!” he hissed, righting himself just in time to dodge another strike.
The Voidstalker’s tendrils spun faster, carving paths through the air. Zack swung a counterpunch, without the blades this time, as he aimed for the creature’s translucent torso. The strike connected, and for a moment, the Voidstalker recoiled, its body rippling under the impact. But just as Zack’s fire flared against the creature’s surface, it sputtered and died, extinguished by the oppressive cold.
He glanced down at his extinguished flames, realizing they were almost completely snuffed out now, weakened by touching the Voidstalker. His fire wasn’t going to be enough.
The creature came at him again, quick as a shadow. Zack ducked and rolled, his hands instinctively striking out as it passed. This time, his punch landed squarely on the Voidstalker’s face. Without the fire, his bare knuckles collided with its translucent flesh—and to his shock, the creature let out an unearthly screech, stumbling back.
Zack froze for a moment, blinking at his own fist. “No magic...” he muttered, piecing it together. “You’re only strong against magic, aren’t you?”
The Voidstalker raised its claws, twisting them in the air. The frozen droplets responded instantly, swirling into a dense mist that obscured Zack’s vision. Tendrils lashed out from the fog, forcing him to dodge blindly. One struck his leg, throwing him off balance, and another grazed his side, tearing through his jacket.
Zack hit the ground hard. He scrambled to his feet, frustration mounting. The Voidstalker had the upper hand, using the frozen rain to control the battlefield and keep him on the defensive.
“Think, Zack, think,” he muttered, glancing around. His eyes settled on an area where the Voidstalker’s earlier attacks had shattered the droplets entirely. The air there was clear, the frozen rain reduced to glittering fragments on the ground.
An idea formed.
Zack ran toward the shattered area, leaving the swarm of droplets behind. The Voidstalker attacked in response, its tendrils snapping forward. Zack ducked under the first and rolled away from the second, leading the creature closer to the cleared zone. His movements were deliberate now, each dodge drawing the Voidstalker further away from its advantage.
The creature hesitated as it approached the shattered area, its tendrils slowing. Zack smirked.
“Not so confident without your toys, huh?”
Zack rushed in, pivoting to deliver a hard kick to its torso. The impact sent a ripple through the creature’s body, and it screeched, stumbling into the barren space.
Zack pressed his advantage. He darted forward, weaving around the Voidstalker’s sluggish strikes. His fists connected again and again with its translucent form, each blow landing harder than the last. The monster’s tendrils flailed wildly, but without the frozen droplets to augment its attacks, it couldn’t regain the upper hand.
It screeched again, its glowing eyes flaring as it raised its claws in desperation. The remaining droplets around them shot toward Zack, forming jagged spikes.
“Nope, not this time!” Zack shouted, diving low and rushing the Voidstalker.
He drove his shoulder into the creature’s midsection, tackling it further into the cleared zone. The spikes dissipated as the Voidstalker lost control, its claws raking the air. Zack spun on his heel, delivering a powerful roundhouse kick to its side. The impact sent it crashing to the ground.
The Voidstalker writhed, its body flickering like a glitching image. Its tendrils lashed out weakly, but Zack didn’t let up. He raised his fists, focusing on every strike as he pummeled the creature’s torso. Each blow sent ripples through its form, its screeches growing weaker with every hit.
Finally, the Voidstalker’s glow dimmed, its body unraveling into translucent threads of energy. It let out one last, hollow screech before collapsing into mist, which evaporated into the frigid air.
Zack stumbled back, panting heavily. His fists were raw, his arms trembling from the exertion. The shattered droplets crunched beneath his boots as he steadied himself, his breath visible in the freezing air.
The frozen rain around him hung motionless once more, the eerie silence returning. Zack wiped sweat from his brow, the cold stinging his exposed skin.
“That was tiring,” he muttered, flexing his sore fingers. The training with Aiko had made a difference—every movement, every strike had been repeated countless times before.
Zack stood up and pushed forward into the frozen landscape. Somewhere ahead, the Essence Crystal awaited—and likely, more challenges with it.
