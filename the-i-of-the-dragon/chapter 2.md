Chapter 2

Zack woke to the sterile scent of antiseptic and the distant beep of medical monitors. He groaned, his head pounding, and blinked against the harsh fluorescent light. The room came into focus—white walls, a curtain drawn around his bed, and a window that offered a glimpse of Tokyo's skyline.

"You're awake," a soft voice said from beside him. He turned his head, wincing at the ache in his neck, and saw the woman from before. She was seated in a chair, her posture relaxed.

Her long, silver hair fell in soft waves down her back, framing her face perfectly. Her blue eyes, bright and clear, were filled with concern and relief. In that moment, Zack thought she looked almost like an angel, her calm and reassuring presence bringing him a sense of peace.

The first thing he noticed was the necklace with a large blue gemstone; it was only after that, that he noticed the rest of her outfit. The airy fabric flowing gently around her, and its thin, delicate shoulder straps that lead to a simple white dress that had an only slightly revealing neckline.

The soft light from the window behind her gave her a gentle glow, making her look even more angelic. The contrast between the way she'd looked in battle and the way she did now was striking.

“Be you angel?” Zack managed to croak, his throat dry.

The woman laughed. "No, I'm no angel, but thank you."

"Who... what happened?" Zack asked, his mind still clouded.

The woman was still smiling, a hint of amusement in her eyes. "My name is Aiko. You had a rough encounter with a Voidstalker."

"A Voidstalker?" Zack echoed, trying to make sense of the unfamiliar term.

Aiko nodded. "They are creatures from another realm, drawn to places where the boundary between worlds is thin. Tokyo is currently experiencing an infestation."

Zack's mind reeled. "And you... you fight them?"

"I'm part of an organization called the Dragon Guard," Aiko explained. "We protect humanity from them and similar threats.”

Zack frowned, trying to piece together the fragments of his memory. The monster, the fire... "You mean that was real? The flames, the power... it was all real?"

"Very real," Aiko confirmed. She winked at him and looked around conspiratorially. Then she held out her hand and an ice shard appeared above it. She made it disappear the next second. She turned serious again. "And now that your power is awakened, the Voidstalkers will be drawn to you. They'll come for you, whether you want to or not."

Zack's jaw tightened. "So, what? I'm supposed to join your organization and fight monsters now? I didn't ask for this."

Aiko let out a sigh. "I understand this is a lot to take in. But you have a choice: either learn to control your power and fight, or be a target for the Voidstalkers. I can help you train, help you understand your abilities."

Zack sighed, rubbing his temples. The idea of battling monsters was ludicrous, yet the memory of the power he had wielded was tempting. And if what Aiko said was true, his life was in danger regardless.

"I need to know more," he muttered, his head already aching from it all. "About the Dragon Guard, the Voidstalkers, everything."."

Aiko's smile returned, this time warmer. "I’ll tell you as much as I can. I don’t know everything."

“How did no one else see what was really happening?” Zack asked, confusion and frustration taking over. “How come everyone thinks it was just a car accident? I mean, we fought a monster in the middle of Shibuya! There were people everywhere. Shouldn’t they have seen it?”

Aiko's expression grew serious again, and she leaned in closer, lowering her voice as if she was afraid someone might overhear. “That’s part of the magic of the wWorld and how it protects reality itself. You see, from the viewpoint of our realm the Voidwalkers are an anomaly. So the realm tries to ‘correct’ them.

It is a form of reality manipulation that shields our battles from the perception of ordinary people. In essence, what they see is tailored to keep them calm and ignorant of the real dangers lurking in the shadows.”

Zack’s frown deepened. “You mean the planet does a cover-up?”

“In a way, yes, but also way more than that. When something supernatural happens, like our battle yesterday, reality gets literally rewritten. If your fire left any scorch marks that didn’t match the car accident story they would get ‘erased. The reality would change as if they never existed in the first place.

“ Her voice took a cadence that Zack associated with academia, someone taught her this and she was repeating it. “The regular people’s perception also gets distorted. To them, it looked like a car accident. Both what they currently see and their memories are altered to fit new reality.”

Zack’s eyes widened. “So, it's not just that they think it was a car accident? It actually was one for them? ”

“Exactly, the World ensures that what they perceive is mundane, something they can rationalize and forget quickly. It’s not perfect, but it’s effective in keeping the peace. And if nothing mundane fits then it erases the changes.” She shrugged., ”Sometimes it's hard to tell what the World will do with the given damage, but it usually turns out okay. You learn not to think about it too much”

Zack leaned back against the pillows. He had a dozen more questions—about the World, about how deep this went—but his brain was already at capacity.

By this point Zack just felt numb, his whole idea of how the world worked crumbling around him. Wonder what my christian parents would think about it. But thinking about it, it actually kinda fits. He returned his attention to the present. “How long has this been going on?”

“For centuries,” Aiko said softly. “The Dragon Guard has been using that effect and protecting humanity from the shadows. It’s a thankless job, but it’s necessary.”

He stared at her, seeing the weight of responsibility in her eyes. The more she spoke, the more he realized how deep this rabbit hole went. “And now I’m part of it, whether I like it or not.” He absolutely didn't want to join this shadow organization. He just got his life together and was always a bit of a loner, who chafed at authority.

“Yes, but you’re not alone.” She smiled at him, ”I’ll help you, I feel responsible because I've awakened your powers.”

Zack rubbed his face, still trying to wrap his head around it. “How do these Voidstalkers even find me? Why are they after me?”

“The first one was an accident, actually, it was after me.” She had the good grace to look embarrassed. “Voidstalkers are drawn to spirit power. With your power awakened, it is like a beacon to them. They are able to sense it and will come looking for you. The more you use your powers, the more they’ll be attracted to you. That’s why it’s crucial you learn to control and conceal your abilities.”

Zack exhaled sharply. “So, training isn’t just about fighting. It’s about hiding too.”

“Precisely,” Aiko agreed. “I’ll teach you how to mask your energy, how to protect yourself and those around you.”

Zack shifted slightly in the hospital bed, wincing as a jolt of pain shot through his head. Despite the discomfort, he turned his gaze back to Aiko, curiosity burning in his hazel eyes. There was a hole in her story. “How did they notice me in the first place? The voidstalker ignored the other people there.”

Aiko leaned forward slightly, a hint of admiration in her voice. "I sensed a tremendous amount of unrefined spirit power within you, Zack. It's rare to find someone with such potential. When you caught me, I could feel it.”

Aiko blushed and continued. “It was like standing next to a dormant volcano, powerful and untapped. I believe that the voidstalker felt your power even in its unawakened state."

Zack let out a slow breath. "So, you're saying I had this power all along, and you just... unlocked it?"

"Precisely," Aiko said. "Everyone has some level of spirit power, but most people never realize it, let alone tap into it. I wish I didn't have to do that but the situation forced my hand."

Zack connected the dots in his head. “You said you were sorry. Why did you say that?"

Aiko's cheeks flushed a delicate pink as she looked down at her hands, nervously clasped in her lap. She fidgeted for a moment before finally meeting Zack's gaze, her blue eyes not quite meeting his. "I said I was sorry because I didn't want to pull you into this world," she admitted, her voice a bit shaky.

"Awakening your powers... it's not something to be taken lightly. It changes everything. But at that moment, I saw no other choice. It was the only way for both of us to survive."

Zack’s brow furrowed as he absorbed her words. "Don’t be sorry, you had no other choice, right?"

"Yes," Aiko replied quietly. "The Voidstalker we faced was powerful, and I got hurt when I protected you. If I hadn’t awakened your latent abilities, we wouldn’t be having this conversation right now. We’d both be dead."

Zack nodded slowly, digesting Aiko's words. “Thank you then, you saved my life more than once, Ii owe you”

Aiko's cheeks flushed slightly, and she gave him a playful smile. "Well, if you insist on repaying me, maybe you can start by training hard. That way, I won't have to rescue you so often."

Zack chuckled. "I'll do my best. But you make it sound like you're expecting me to be a handful. But seriously, I’m grateful. It’s a lot to take in, and having someone like you around makes it a bit easier."

Aiko leaned back in her chair. "You’re pretty easy to talk to, Zack. I think you’ll fit in with the Dragon Guard just fine. And who knows? You might even surpass me one day."

"Surpass you?" Zack raised an eyebrow, intrigued. "Is that a challenge?"

She laughed. "Maybe it is. I wouldn't mind having someone around to keep me on my toes."

"Well, I do like a good challenge," Zack said, leaning forward slightly. "But don’t blame me if I take you up on that and actually win."

Aiko's eyes gleamed, her smile widening. "I look forward to seeing you try. Just remember, I won’t go easy on you."

"Wouldn’t expect anything less," Zack replied, feeling a strange rapport between them, as if Aiko was an old friend and not someone he just met.

Zack replayed the events in his mind again, and another question formed. "But there's something I don't get. When I used my power, it felt incredibly strong, almost overwhelming. It seemed like I had more power than you, and you've been trained. How is that possible?"

"That's a part of the awakening process. When someone's powers are first awakened, it often results in a massive burst of energy. It's a surge that gives you access to incredible strength, but it's short-lived and unstable. It almost always ends with the person fainting from the exertion. Next time you activate your powers they could feel a bit… underwhelming" Aiko explained, almost apologetically

Zack leaned back, thinking it through. "So, that burst of power I felt... it won’t be like that again?"

"Oh it can," Aiko said. "But that requires training, and a lot of it. With time most Dragon Guards exceed the power of their awakening."

Zack sighed, rubbing the back of his neck. "That’s good, I guess. But it’s still a lot to take in."

Aiko’s expression was gentle. "I know. It’s a big change, and it’s completely fine to feel overwhelmed. But you’re not alone in this. I’m here to help you every step of the way."

Zack managed a small smile in return. "Thanks, Aiko. I appreciate that. It’s just... a lot."

"It is," she agreed, her tone gentle. "But you’re handling it well. Better than most, actually. And once you start training, you’ll find your footing. It’ll become more manageable."

Zack nodded, feeling a bit more confident. "Good. I’m ready to at least give it a shot."

Aiko grinned. "That’s the spirit."

Zack leaned back against the pillows and stared at the ceiling. Monsters, magic, secret organizations. A week ago his biggest worry had been a dwindling bank account. He almost laughed.
