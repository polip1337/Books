




Chapter 12
The door to the inn burst open, letting in a gust of cold night air as Zack, Aiko, and Kaito stumbled inside. Zack carried Rei in his arms. Her blue hair was damp from the cold outside, her face pale. Kaito, panting heavily, adjusted his glasses and glanced nervously around the room.
“W-what are we going t-to tell her father?” Kaito stammered, his voice breaking with panic. “Zack, this—this doesn’t look good!”
“We’re here now,” Zack said, his voice strained as he set Rei gently on one of the empty tables near the hearth. His eyes darted to Aiko. “You’re sure about this, right?”
Before Aiko could answer, a tall man with a weathered face and salt-and-pepper hair emerged from behind the bar. His brow furrowed as his gaze fell on Rei, and his expression twisted with both fear and anger.
“What happened to her?” Rei’s father demanded, his voice booming. He rushed to the table, brushing past Zack and Kaito to kneel beside his daughter. He pressed two fingers to her neck, his breath hitching in relief when he felt a pulse. “She needs a hospital—now!”
“That’s not going to help her,” Aiko said, stepping forward. Her silver hair glinted in the firelight as she stood with an air of calm authority.
Rei’s father shot her a glare, his voice rising. “Not going to help? She’s unconscious! Do not talk nonsense, girl!”
“It’s not nonsense,” Aiko replied, her voice level. “Rei isn’t suffering from anything a hospital can treat. This is beyond normal medicine. Her condition is magical in nature, and taking her to a hospital would only waste time.”
“I don’t have time for this!” Rei’s father barked, his hands shaking as he hovered over his daughter. “I’m not going to sit here and watch her die because of some absurd—”
Before he could finish, Aiko raised her hand, and a small shard of ice materialized in her palm. With a flick of her wrist, it grew into a crystalline dagger, shimmering with light. She thrust it into the table beside Rei, the wood splintering under the force.
Rei’s father recoiled, his eyes wide, his voice caught in his throat.
“This isn’t a fairy tale,” Aiko said, her tone sharp. “What you just saw is real. And so is what’s happening to Rei. She’s in a magical coma—a state caused by a complete depletion of her spirit energy. If you take her to a hospital, the doctors won’t find anything wrong, and you’ll lose precious time.”
Rei’s father stared at the ice dagger, his jaw tightening. “You expect me to believe that magic is real because of... that?”
Aiko took a step closer, lowering her voice. “I understand it’s hard to believe, but I swear to you, this is the truth. I’ve fought monsters with her, creatures no hospital could ever explain. And now, she’s fighting to survive. If you love your daughter, you need to trust me.”
The room fell silent except for the crackling of the hearth. Rei’s father looked at the dagger, then at his daughter, and finally back at Aiko. His hands clenched into fists, his expression torn between anger and desperation.
“Fine,” he said at last, his voice rough. “I don’t understand any of this, but if there’s even a chance you’re right…” He exhaled heavily. “Take her upstairs. Do what you need to do.”
Aiko nodded, relief flickering across her face. She gestured to Zack, who scooped Rei back into his arms and carried her up the creaking stairs. Kaito followed nervously, casting glances over his shoulder as if he expected Rei’s father to change his mind.
Once Rei was settled in a guest room, Aiko closed the door softly behind them. “I’ll explain everything,” she said to Rei’s father. “Just give me a moment.” 
The explanation was quick and to the point. Though Rei’s father listened, his skeptical expression didn’t waver. But the ice dagger, summoned from nothing, seemed to weigh heavily on his mind. For now, it was enough to keep him from demanding more.
As Aiko rejoined the others, Zack leaned against the wall, exhaustion written across his face. “Well?” he asked.
“He’s listening,” Aiko replied quietly. “That’s all we can ask for right now.”
Zack slumped into a chair in the corner of the guest room, his head still swimming. Rei lay motionless on the bed, her chest rising and falling in shallow breaths. Kaito hovered nervously by the doorway, fidgeting with the hem of his hoodie, his wide eyes darting between Aiko and Zack.
“What do we do now?,” Zack said, breaking the heavy silence. “Rei’s not getting better, and none of us knows how to help her. We need someone who does.”
Aiko, standing stiffly by the bedside, didn’t respond right away. She pressed a hand to her temple, her silver hair falling like a curtain over her face. “There’s one option,” she said reluctantly. Her voice was quiet.
Kaito perked up, his voice shaky. “W-what is it?”
“The Dragon Guard,” Aiko said. She glanced at Zack, then at Kaito. “They have resources and knowledge to fix this. If anyone can pull Rei out of this, it’s them.”
Zack straightened in his chair, hope flickering in his hazel eyes. “Then let’s call them. What’s the problem?”
“The problem,” Aiko snapped, turning to face him, “is that involving the Dragon Guard comes with consequences. They’ve spent centuries keeping the existence of Voidstalkers secret from the general public. If they think any of you know too much, they might not let you walk away.” Her gaze softened as it landed on Rei’s unconscious form. “And even if they do, the cost could be... high.”
Kaito paled, his hands shaking. “W-what do you mean ‘high’? L-like memory erasure? Or, uh… worse?”
“The Guard doesn’t take chances.” Aiko admitted, her voice tight. “ And memory erasure is often difficult. Anyone who knows too much about Voidstalkers is either brought into the fold or... dealt with.”
Zack leaned forward, his voice firm. “We’ll deal with that if it happens. Right now, Rei’s life is the priority.”
Aiko turned to Rei’s father “Sir, the best way to protect you and your daughter is for you to leave the matter to us.”

The man bristled, his hands clenching into fists. “You’re asking me to stand by while my daughter’s life hangs in the balance—and trust some secretive organization that you admit might erase me from the equation? Do you hear how insane that sounds?”
“It is insane,” Zack replied, standing to meet the older man’s glare. “But it’s also the only option we’ve got. If they can save Rei, isn’t it worth the risk?”
“Is it?” Rei’s father shot back. “ Who’s to say they won’t just decide we’re all too much of a problem and wipe us out?”
“They won’t do that,” Aiko interrupted, her voice sharp. “The Dragon Guard isn’t cruel. They’re secretive, yes, but their goal is to protect people. If we’re careful, there’s a good chance we’ll all come out of this intact. We just want to be on the safer side.”
Rei’s father’s jaw tightened, his gaze dropping to his daughter’s pale, unmoving form. His hands trembled as he reached out to touch her face, brushing her hair back gently. “This isn’t fair,” he muttered, his voice breaking. “She doesn’t deserve this.”
“I know,” Aiko said quietly. She stepped closer, her voice softening. “But we have to act, or she won’t get another chance. I’ll do everything I can. But you have to trust us on this.”
Rei’s father was silent for a long moment before he finally straightened, his expression a mask of resolve. “Fine. But if anything happens to her—don’t expect forgiveness.”
“Understood,” Aiko said simply. She turned to Zack and Kaito, her silver hair swaying as she gestured for them to step back. “When the Dragon Guard arrives, things will move quickly. Sir”—she glanced at Rei’s father—“please go now.”
Rei’s father opened his mouth to protest but stopped himself. He stared at his daughter one last time, then gave a reluctant nod. “I’ll be downstairs,” he muttered, his voice rough. He turned and walked out, the door creaking shut behind him.
Once he was gone, Aiko pulled her phone from her pocket. She hesitated for a moment, her expression conflicted, before dialing a number. “This is Aiko,” she said curtly when the line connected. “It’s an emergency. One of ours is down—magic depletion coma. I need immediate assistance. Sending coordinates now.”
She ended the call and slipped the phone away, her shoulders stiff with tension. “They’re coming,” she said, her voice tight.
“W-what do we do when they get here?” Kaito asked, his voice quivering. “Do we just... stand around and hope they don’t decide to, uh, ‘fix’ us too?”
“We stay calm and cooperate,” Aiko replied. “I’ll handle the explanations. Just don’t say anything unnecessary.”
The air grew heavy with anticipation as they waited. Then, a ripple of energy pulsed through the room, making the air hum and crackle. A glowing circle of intricate runes materialized in the center of the floor, and in a flash of blue light, a figure emerged.
The man who appeared was striking, almost theatrical in appearance. His long coat swirled dramatically as he stepped forward, its silver embroidery catching the light. A katana hung at his side, the hilt wrapped in deep blue leather. Mismatched eyes—one gold, the other a dark, gray—surveyed the room.
“Well, well,” the man said, his voice rich with amusement. “Looks like I’ve got my work cut out for me. Name’s Shiro. I hear someone here needs saving.”
Zack stared at him, both impressed and wary. “You’re with the Dragon Guard?”
Shiro grinned, showing sharp teeth. “The one and only. Let’s get to work, shall we?” He turned his attention to Rei, his expression shifting from lighthearted to serious in an instant. “This,” he murmured, crouching beside her, “is going to be interesting.”
Shiro examined Rei carefully, his mismatched eyes narrowing as he placed a gloved hand lightly over her chest. A faint ripple of energy emanated from his palm, spreading across her form. After a moment, he leaned back, his mouth set in a thin line.
“She’s in deep,” Shiro said. “There is an additional spell besides the spirit depletion. Whatever she was facing, it wanted her asleep for a long time” His gaze flicked to Aiko. “You said she’s one of ours?”
Aiko nodded, keeping her expression neutral. The lie came smoothly. “Yes. She’s been fighting Voidstalkers alongside me for some time. The last one we encountered—well, it overwhelmed her.”
“Hmm,” Shiro murmured, his tone noncommittal as he turned his attention briefly to Zack and Kaito. His sharp gaze lingered on them just long enough to make Zack shift uncomfortably and Kaito tug at his hoodie nervously.
Shiro’s grin returned, as if he didn’t just stare at them for a couple of seconds. “And these two? Don’t look like members of the Guard to me.”
“They’re...” Aiko hesitated, searching for the right words. “Trusted allies. They helped me get Rei to safety.”
“Trusted allies,” Shiro repeated slowly. He turned to Zack, his golden eye glinting. “What exactly do you know about the Dragon Guard, hmm?”
Zack met his gaze steadily, though his gut tightened. “Enough to know you’re our best shot at helping Rei. That’s all that matters to me right now.”
Shiro chuckled softly, his mismatched eyes glinting. “Fair enough, for now.” He stood, brushing his coat off. “Let’s focus on Rei before we get into the messy details.”
Aiko exhaled quietly in relief, though her shoulders remained tense.
Shiro worked swiftly, his movements precise as he poured shimmering powder into the air above Rei’s chest, murmuring incantations under his breath. The powder glowed faintly, settling over her like a thin veil. Her breathing, which had been shallow and uneven, began to steady, though her expression remained unchanged.
“That’s the best I can do for now,” Shiro said, rising to his feet. He dusted off his hands, his sharp eyes scanning the room. “She’s stable, but only barely. If we don’t get her an essence crystal, she’ll slip beyond even the Guard’s ability to save her. And those aren’t exactly lying around in the nearest apothecary.”
Aiko frowned. “Doesn't’ he Guard keep a stockpile of those for emergencies?”
“Not right now,” Shiro said, uncorking the vial and letting a drop of the liquid fall onto Rei’s forehead. It sizzled faintly. “Our supplies have been stretched thin after the last wave of Voidstalker attacks.”
Shiro’s expression turned serious as he capped the vial and rose to his full height. “Guess we’ll need to find another solution. And as luck would have it, I might have a way to solve two problems at once.”
Aiko stiffened, her silver hair shimmering in the dim light. “What are you talking about, Shiro?”
“Does anyone else know anything about this situation?” His gaze was focused on Aiko.

“N-no, just us in this room.” The girl responded. “What do you propose?”
Shiro put a hand on his katana. “Simple. The girl nor those two”—he gestured to Zack and Kaito with his other hand “are not members of the Guard. And you and I both know the Keepers won’t let that slide.”
“That's not …” Aiko began.
Shiro ignored her, leaning in towards Zack, his grin widening and turning predatory. “So, how about you just disappear?”
