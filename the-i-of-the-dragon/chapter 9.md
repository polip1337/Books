Chapter 9

Rei had everything planned, so they took a bus on Friday afternoon. The group settled into their seats, the atmosphere light and relaxed. Zack sat next to Rei, who was busy pointing out interesting landmarks through the window, her enthusiasm contagious.

Kaito, sitting across the aisle, fidgeted nervously as he tried to make small talk with Aiko. Despite his best efforts, his words kept coming out awkward, causing Aiko to stifle her laughter more than once. Zack caught Aiko’s eye, but she blushed and looked away, things were still a bit awkward between them.

The drive up to Rei ’s father’s inn had been scenic, the winding roads offering breathtaking views as they ascended. The late afternoon sun bathed the landscape in a warm, golden hue, and by the time they arrived at the inn, the sky had begun to transition into the deep orange and pinks of dusk.

The inn itself, nestled snugly against the mountainside, was the dictionary definition of rustic charm. The building itself was made of dark, weathered wood, with lanterns hanging from the eaves, casting a soft glow that only added to the atmosphere. The air was crisp, with the faint scent of pine and the distant sound of a brook.

“Welcome to the Sato Inn!” Rei  announced grandly as they stepped out of the bus, stretching her arms wide. “The perfect place to kick back, relax, and forget about everything else.”

Zack smiled as he looked around. The inn had a cozy, inviting feel to it, and after everything that had happened recently, he was more than ready to take Rei ’s advice and unwind. Kaito, standing beside him, looked equally impressed, his eyes wide as he took in the surroundings.

“This place is amazing,” Kaito said, his usual nervousness melting away. “I didn’t know your dad owned something like this, Rei.”

Rei grinned, clearly proud. “Yup! It's been in the family for generations. And the cooking is the best! You guys are in for a real treat tonight!”

Aiko, who had been rather quiet during the drive up, finally spoke up. “It’s beautiful here, Rei . I can see why you enjoy coming back.”

Rei gave Aiko a playful nudge. “You know it! And trust me, we’re going to have a blast tonight. Just wait until you try the hot springs—we’ll soak all our stress away.”

As they made their way inside, Rei ’s father greeted them with a warm smile and a strong handshake. He was a tall, broad-shouldered man with a kind face, his hair starting to gray at the temples. After a brief round of introductions, he showed them to their rooms, one for the girls and one for the boys. He promised a home-cooked meal that would “put all their troubles to rest.”

Once they’d settled in and freshened up, the group met again in the dining area, where a long, low table had been set up with an array of dishes that made Zack’s stomach growl. There were steaming bowls of miso soup, platters of freshly grilled fish, an assortment of pickled vegetables, and, to top it all off, a huge pot of Rei ’s father’s signature hotpot, filled with slices of meat, tofu, and a variety of mushrooms.

“Alright, everyone, dig in!” Rei  declared, taking a seat at the head of the table. “Trust me, you’re going to want to try everything.”

Zack didn’t need any more encouragement. He grabbed his chopsticks and went straight for the hotpot, piling his bowl high with as much food as it could hold. Aiko, sitting beside him, was more reserved in her approach, but even she couldn’t resist the delicious aroma wafting from the pot.

“This is amazing,” Zack said between mouthfuls, his eyes wide with delight. “Rei, your dad’s a genius.”

“I told you,” Rei  replied with a grin, already halfway through her own bowl. “There’s nothing like a home-cooked meal in the mountains.”

Kaito had already gone back for seconds. “I could eat this every day,” he mumbled through a mouthful of fish. “Why didn’t we do this sooner?”

As they ate, the conversation flowed easily, with Rei telling them stories of her childhood spent at the inn. She told them about the time she tried to run the kitchen by herself and almost set the whole place on fire, and another story about the local legends surrounding the mountain, including one about a mischievous tanuki spirit that was said to live nearby.

“You know, they say if you don’t leave food out for the tanuki, it’ll play tricks on you,” Rei  said with a grin.

At this, her father, who had been quietly enjoying his meal, looked up with a more serious expression. "The tanuki legend isn't just about tricks," he began, his voice calm but with an edge that made the others pause. "In the old days, people treated that stuff really seriously. Most of the time, it was harmless fun—a missing shoe here, a strange noise there—but recently, there have been more troubling incidents."

Rei’s father sighed deeply before continuing, “In the past year, several people have gone missing in these mountains, more than usual. The locals whisper that the tanuki have become more dangerous, or maybe some wild animals moved closer to town. We advise all our guests to stay close to the buildings after dark and avoid wandering alone, just in case.”

The mood around the table shifted slightly. Zack and Aiko glanced at each other, both had Voidwalkers on their mind. Rei tried to lighten the atmosphere by saying, “Dad loves his stories. But seriously, don’t worry—just stay inside at night, and you’ll be fine.”

Kaito, who had a new appreciation for monsters, looked genuinely concerned. “Wait, are you serious? We’re not going to get attacked by a tanuki, are we?”

Zack comforted his friend. “Relax, Kaito. I’m pretty sure Rei ’s just messing with you.”

Rei winked. “Or am I?”

Kaito’s went rigid, glancing nervously around the room as if expecting to see a pair of glowing eyes staring back at him from the shadows.

“Don’t worry, Kaito,” Aiko said reassuringly. “I’m sure any tanuki spirits around here are friendly.”

Kaito didn’t look entirely convinced, but he managed a weak smile. “If you say so…”

After they had all eaten their fill, Rei ’s father appeared with a bottle of sake and a tray of tiny cups. “A little something, before the hot springs,” he said with a wink, pouring each of them a small cup.

Zack took his cup, the warmth of the sake spreading through him as he sipped it. He glanced over at Aiko, who was sipping hers with a thoughtful expression.

“Are you sure you don’t want to skip the hot springs?” Zack teased. “Aren’t you afraid of the tanuki?”

Snapping out of her thoughts Aiko gave him a playful glare. “I think I could handle it, Zack.”

With their spirits lifted by good food, the group made their way to the inn’s outdoor hot springs. They split up by gender and went into the changing rooms. They quickly changed.

With towels wrapped around their waists Zack and Kaito entered the hot springs proper. The natural pools were surrounded by smooth stones, with steam rising gently from the water. The night sky above was clear, dotted with impossibly bright stars.

They slipped into the water, the heat immediately relaxing Zack’s muscles.

“This is the life,” Kaito said, closing his eyes. ”I take back everything bad I ever said about Rei.”

“And what would that be Kaito?” Rei said. They must have entered while the boys were getting in the water and snuck up on them.

Both of the girls were wearing only the white towels.

Kaito’s eyes snapped open, and his face turned a deep shade of red. “W-What are you doing here?” he stammered, desperately trying to maintain eye contact with the girls and failing miserably.

Rei smirked, her brown eyes sparkling mischievously. “What does it look like? We’re here to enjoy the hot springs too. It’s not like you own the place, Kaito.”

Aiko giggled, her voice soft and melodic, as she gracefully lowered herself into the water. Her long, silver hair cascaded over her shoulders, the steam from the hot springs creating a glow around her. “Don’t worry. We’ll behave,” she teased, with a grin.

Zack watched as Kaito swallowed hard. “Y-Yeah, well, I’m not used to sharing my bath with… with girls, i didn’t know it was mixed gender” he muttered, sinking lower into the water in a futile attempt to hide his embarrassment.

Rei chuckled, wading over to Zack and lightly flicking water at him. “Oh, come on! It’s not like we’re naked.” She gave him a wink that only deepened the crimson hue of his face.

Kaito let out a loud yelp. The sight of them in nothing but towels and the idea of them naked was too much for him. His nose started bleeding profusely, and he tried to stop it with his hand.

Rei burst out laughing. “Oh no, Kaito’s down! I guess we broke him. Quick, somebody get a medic!”

Aiko giggled again, covering her mouth with her hand as Kaito stumbled out of the hot springs, blood still trickling from his nose. “I-I’ll be back!” he managed to blurt out, rushing towards the changing room, the wet towel clutched tightly around his waist.

Rei watched him go, her laughter dying down into a soft chuckle. “Poor guy. He’s too innocent for his own good.”

Zack shook his head, trying to not laugh. “Well, don’t tease him too much,” he said.

“Okay okay, i promise,” Rei turned her attention back to Zack, “So, Zack, how do you like it here?”

Zack relaxed a bit more ”It’s amazing. I didn’t think I’d like it this much, but there’s something really magical about this place. I can see why you love it so much, Rei.”

Rei smiled, her expression softening. “I’m glad you think so. My dad says the hot springs are special— It’s supposed to be good for the soul as well as the body.”

Aiko nodded in agreement. “I feel like I’m in another world here”

Zack closed his eyes, leaning back against the rocks that bordered the spring. “Yeah… peaceful.” He let out a contented sigh.

Rei splashed him. “Don’t get too comfortable! We still have to check out the other springs. My dad says each one has a different feel to it. Plus, I want to show you the one where the tanuki plays the most tricks.”

Zack opened one eye, giving her a skeptical look. “You’re not going to let the tanuki thing go, are you?”

Rei grinned wickedly. “Nope! Besides, it’s fun to see you get all freaked out. You should have seen your face earlier when my dad was telling the story. You were like a scared little kid!”

Aiko giggled softly. “It was kind of cute, though.”

“You know I wasn't scared, right? You just came up with it.” But seeing the look on Rei’s face Zack groaned. “But you’re not going to let it go anyway?”

“Not a chance,” Rei and Aiko said in unison, both of them laughing.

They spent the next hour or so in the hot springs, chatting and joking around, with Rei occasionally teasing Zack about the tanuki. The water was soothing, and the atmosphere was light.

Aiko seemed more relaxed than Zack had ever seen her. The warmth had brought a soft flush to her cheeks, and she had loosened enough for her to join in on the teasing. She had a wit that played well with Rei’s more forward personality.

Eventually, they decided to explore some of the other hot springs. They wrapped themselves in their towels again, with Zack making sure to keep a respectful distance behind the girls as they moved through the misty paths connecting the various pools.

“So, where’s this tanuki spring you keep talking about?” Zack asked.

Rei led them down a narrow path, lined with smooth stones and lit by small, glowing lanterns that gave off a warm, amber light. The steam gave the place a mystical quality.

“Right over here,” she said, pointing to a small, secluded spring nestled between two large rocks. “This is the spot where the tanuki are supposed to hang out. My dad says people used to leave the offerings here.”

Zack peered around the spring “And… has anyone ever actually seen one?” he asked..

Rei shook her head, a teasing smile on her lips. “Not really, but there have been a lot of weird stories.”

Aiko, who had been quietly observing the spring, spoke up. “There’s something about this place, though. It does feel… different.”

Zack cast a sharp glance at Aiko, was she really sensing something or was she just teasing?

Rei grinned, clearly enjoying Zack’s discomfort. “Maybe there is. You never know with tanuki.”

Zack rolled his eyes, but before he could respond, they heard a faint rustling noise coming from the nearby trees. He froze, his eyes darting towards the sound.

“Did you hear that?” he whispered, his voice barely audible.

Aiko and Rei exchanged glances before turning their attention to the trees. The rustling grew louder, and then, as if on cue, a small, round object rolled out from behind the rocks and stopped at Zack’s feet.

“What the—” Zack started, staring down at the object. It was an old, weathered coin, covered in dirt and moss.

Rei’s raised her eyebrows and bent down to pick it up. “That’s weird,” she said, examining the coin.

Aiko frowned, her brow furrowed in thought. “Do you think it’s one of the offerings people used to leave for the tanuki?”

Before anyone could answer, there was another rustling sound, this time from the opposite side of the spring. Zack turned quickly, his heart pounding in his chest. Another coin rolled out, this time Zack picked it up. “Okay, this is starting to get creepy,” he muttered.

Rei’s playful demeanor faltered for a moment, and she took a step closer to Zack and Aiko. “Maybe we should head back. It’s getting late, and my dad will freak out if we stay out too long.”

Zack felt the air get a lot colder suddenly, Aiko must have used her aura, preparing for a fight. Zack reached into his core and his own aura, to cover the change in temperature before Rei.

Just as they were about to turn and leave, the rustling sound intensified, and a shadowy figure darted out from behind one of the rocks. It was small and quick, too fast for any of them to get a good look at.

“Did you see that?” Rei yelped, taking an involuntary step back.

Aiko, usually the calmest of the group, looked genuinely unnerved. “Let’s just go,” she said quietly, glancing around as if expecting more shadows to emerge from the trees.

They hurried back up the path, their earlier laughter and teasing replaced with silence. The warm glow of the inn’s lanterns was a welcome sight as they emerged from the trees and made their way back to the safety of the building.

Zack breathed out slowly, the tightness in his shoulders unwinding. “That was… weird,” he said.

Rei nodded, her usual bravado tempered. “Yeah, weird. But, you know, maybe it was just an animal. There are lots of them around here. Anyway, we should get changed.”

Aiko said nothing, simply nodding in agreement, she let Rei go ahead into the changing room.

“We need to talk, it's dangerous here.” Aiko said with a serious tone.
