Chapter 8

The next couple of weeks passed in a blur. Rei joined their little group of friends and they hung out every day at the university. Zack had no trouble with his course work even when he spent the evenings training with Aiko in the woods.

And that training was paying off. Turns out that if you do something diligently for hours every day progress is quite quick. Especially if you have a teacher like Aiko. She was relentless, but in the best way possible. She pushed him, correcting his form, training his speed and stamina. The difference in Zack's abilities was becoming more apparent with each passing day.

Armor of Flame has reached level 4.
Flame Talon has reached level 2.
Flame Talon has reached level 3.
Flame Talon has reached level 4.
General skill Martial Arts has reached level 2.

“Again,” she instructed, her voice calm but commanding.

Zack nodded, he summoned the Flame Talon with ease now, the blade of fire flaring to life in his hand almost instantly. There was no longer any hesitation, no instability.

Aiko didn’t give him time to admire his progress. She lunged at him, her movements a blur. Zack met her attack head-on, the fiery dagger slicing through the air as he parried the strike of her staff. The impact sent a shockwave through his arm, and his heels dug a little deeper into the ground but he held.

“Good!” Aiko called out, impressed. She pressed the attack, her staff moving in a flurry of rapid strikes. Zack moved with her, blocking and countering with his own attacks, the fire in his hand clashing with the thin layer of ice on her staff.

They continued to spar, the sound of their movements echoing through the woods. Zack’s Scorching Aura was always on during his training mainly for the strengthening of his body. Aiko could counter the heat with her own spirit energy.

“Not bad, Zack,” she said with a small grin as they broke apart, only Zack breathing heavily. “You’ve gotten a lot stronger. You’re even able to keep your skills active during a fight without losing focus. That’s a big step forward.”

Zack let the flames dissipate, wiping the sweat from his brow. “Thanks. I’ve had a pretty good teacher,” he replied with a wink.

Aiko shook her head. “Flattery won’t get you out of more training, you know.”

Zack chuckled, rolling his shoulders to work out the tension. “I wouldn’t expect anything less.”

Zack could feel the fatigue in his muscles, but it was the good kind—the kind that came from knowing he’d pushed himself to his limits and grown stronger because of it.

But his thoughts were already drifting elsewhere. “Aiko… there’s something I’ve been thinking about.”

Aiko raised an eyebrow, sensing the shift in his tone. “What is it?”

Zack hesitated for a moment, gathering his thoughts. “It’s about Rei. She’s already so involved in our lives, and I hate keeping things from her. Kaito’s in on this now because he can see Voidstalkers, but Rei… she’s still in the dark. I’m starting to think maybe we should tell her the truth.”

Aiko’s expression immediately darkened, the glint in her eyes replaced with something more serious. She crossed her arms, shaking her head. “Zack, that’s a bad idea.”

“Why?” Zack pushed back, his tone earnest.

Aiko sighed, her gaze dropping to the ground for a moment before meeting Zack’s eyes again. “We’ve been over it with Kaito. Telling Rei about the Voidstalkers would only put her in more danger. The more she knows, the bigger a target she becomes.”

“She’s already a target just by being around us,” Zack argued, his voice rising slightly with frustration. “If something happens, she’ll be completely blindsided.”

Aiko took a step closer, her voice gentle but resolute. “If we tell Rei everything, she’ll feel obligated to help.” Aiko left the consequences of that unsaid.

Zack clenched his fists, the frustration evident on his face. He knew Aiko was right in some ways, but it didn’t make the decision any easier. “So what do we do? Just keep lying to her?”

“For now, yes,” Aiko said, her tone leaving no room for argument. “We focus on dealing with the Voidstalkers and protecting everyone. If the time comes when we have no choice but to tell Rei, we’ll deal with it then. But not before.”

Zack exhaled sharply, leaning back against the tree with a heavy sigh. “I hate this.”

“I know,” Aiko said softly. “But it’s the best way to keep her safe.”

Zack finally nodded, though the tension hadn’t completely left his shoulders. “Alright. We’ll keep her out of it… for now.”

“So,” Zack began, sitting down, he didnt feel like training any more. “how are you liking life at the university? It’s a lot different from the Dragon Guard, I bet.”

A soft, almost wistful expression crossing Aiko’s face. “It’s… nice. Peaceful, even. I never realized how much I needed this kind of calm until I got here.”

Zack raised an eyebrow. “Really? I figured you’d be bored out of your mind after all the action with the Guard.”

Aiko chuckled softly. “I thought I would be too. But growing up with the Dragon Guard, life was always about training, missions, and being ready for anything. It was intense, all the time. Don’t get me wrong—I loved it, and it made me who I am today. But here… I don’t have to be on high alert every second of the day. I can just… be.”

Zack nodded, understanding more than he thought he would. “I can see how that’d be a nice change of pace.”

Zack paused, picking at a blade of grass. “Hey, something’s been bugging me. Back at the hospital you said the World rewrites reality. Does it... choose to do that? Like, does it have a will?”

Aiko was quiet for a moment. “We believe it does. That there is at least some powerful entity guarding our realm. There is no hard evidence to confirm or deny that, but it fits with what happens with the battle damage. And some other stuff…”

“Other stuff?” Zack pressed.

Aiko hesitated. “There are other realms. Getting there is both hard and dangerous, but the fact that barriers between them exist at all suggests something is maintaining them.”

Zack let that sink in. Other realms. A sentient World. He filed it away next to dragons and monster-fighting secret organizations in his rapidly expanding folder of impossible things.

Aiko glanced at him. “Yeah. It’s been good for me. I get to experience things I never really had time for before—like making friends, studying different subjects, and just enjoying myself. Plus, I get to boss you around during training.”

Zack laughed, shaking his head. “And you like that a bit too much, don’t you?”

“Absolutely,” Aiko teased, “But in all seriousness being here with you, Kaito, and Rei… I never had friends. Comrades yes, but not friends… Im glad we’ve met”

Something about that hit Zack harder than he expected. “I’m really glad you’re here too, Aiko. We’re lucky to have you around.”

Aiko gave him a playful nudge. “Don’t go getting all sappy on me now.”

Zack grinned, nudging her back. “Too late.”

They both laughed,

After finishing up their training they got back on Aiko’s bike and Zack’s mind drifted to the dragon egg he’d been carrying around in his backpack. The egg had been with him for a while now, tucked safely inside of his bag. But lately, it had started to change.

At first, it had been subtle—a slight warmth emanating from the egg, a soft, almost imperceptible vibration when he held it close. But over the past few days, the signs had become more noticeable.

The red cracks that covered the otherwise smooth black surface were also getting bigger. He’d noticed these changes one morning after training with Aiko, when he’d taken the egg out to check on it. Holding it in his hands, Zack had felt the warmth radiate through his palms, stronger than before. It was as if the egg was responding to him, growing in strength alongside his own powers.

As they reached his appartment, Zack adjusted his backpack, the egg shifting inside like a warm stone. It was definitely heavier than it had been a week ago, and he wondered how much longer he’d be able to carry it around like this before someone noticed.

Aiko glanced at him, her expression curious. “You okay? You’ve been quiet for a while.”

Zack forced a smile. “Yeah, just thinking about the egg.” He then explained to her the changes he observed.

Aiko's eyes brightened "I can't believe it's already showing signs of hatching. This is amazing, Zack! I mean, it usually takes a lot longer!"

"Yeah, it's pretty wild, isn't it? How many people get to say they're about to hatch a dragon?" Zack chuckled, though there was a nervous edge to his laughter. “Come inside i will show it to you”

They entered his apartment, and Zack placed his backpack on the table and carefully unzipped it, revealing the dragon egg nestled inside. The cracks on its surface had deepened, glowing faintly as if fire simmered just beneath the shell. Aiko moved closer, her gaze fixated on the egg with awe.

"It’s beautiful," she whispered, reaching out to touch the shell.

"Yeah, and it feels like it’s growing stronger every day. I can’t help but wonder what kind of dragon it’s going to be."

Aiko grinned, a playful glint in her eyes. "A strong one, obviously. Just like its owner."

Zack rolled his eyes, but he couldn’t hide a smile. "You think so, huh?"

"Absolutely," Aiko said, her tone serious again. "You’ve been chosen for this, Zack. There’s a reason you have this. The World doesn't give dragons to just  anyone—they choose carefully. And it chose you."

Zack looked at the egg. Whatever was growing inside would depend on him—and that thought landed heavier than any Voidstalker hit.

Aiko put a hand on his shoulder. "You’ve got this, Zack. And when the time comes, you’ll know exactly what to do."

Zack nodded slowly. "Thanks, Aiko. I guess I just needed to hear that. I’m really lucky to have you."

As Zack turned back to the kitchen counter, something shifted in the atmosphere. The comfortable silence they usually shared felt different, and he caught a glimpse of Aiko out of the corner of his eye. She seemed to be standing there a bit awkwardly, and when he turned to face her, he noticed a faint blush on her cheeks.

It struck him then—Aiko looked... flustered. The girl who'd thrown him across a clearing during sparring was fidgeting with her sleeve.

"Uh, Zack," Aiko said, her voice sounding a little higher than usual, "I just realized I... um... should probably get going. I mean, it’s getting late, and I’ve got, uh, some stuff to take care of."

Zack blinked, caught off guard. "Oh, yeah, sure, no problem."

He watched as she forced a smile, her usual ease replaced with a kind of sweet awkwardness. "Yeah, you know, stuff to do and all that."

"Anytime, Aiko. I’ll see you tomorrow?" Zack asked, trying to match her smile with his own.

"Yeah, definitely," she replied quickly, her movements a bit stiff as she backed toward the door. "Take care of the egg, okay?"

Zack nodded, feeling a bit of the tension ease. "I will. And thanks again, Aiko. For everything."

She paused, glancing back at him. Aiko fumbled with the door for a moment, and Zack found himself watching her intently, for the first time in quite a while noticing her bare shoulders and how cute she looked.

"Goodnight, Zack," she said softly, her voice carrying a note of something he couldn’t quite place.

"Goodnight, Aiko," Zack replied, keeping his tone casual.

As the door closed behind her, Zack stared at it for a moment, replaying the last few minutes in his head. He leaned back against the kitchen counter, what just happened? They’d talked hundred times before, but tonight had felt... different.

He couldn’t shake the image of her flustered expression or the way she’d hurried out. It was almost like she’d been nervous. That idea surprised him—Aiko, nervous? It didn’t seem possible. And yet, the way she had blushed, the way she’d struggled to find the right words—it all pointed to something he hadn’t considered before.

Zack leaned against the counter, frowning at the door.

Shaking his head, Zack pushed off from the counter and headed back to the table where the egg sat, warm and pulsing with life. Dragons, monsters, a beautiful guardian who apparently forgot how doors worked around him—if this were a game, the difficulty setting had just jumped from Normal to What The Hell.

He tapped the egg gently with one finger. "Don't you start being complicated too."

After another long day at the university Zack was more than ready to head home. But as he, Aiko and Kaito were packing up their things,Rei sauntered over, wearing a vintage band t-shirt tucked into high-waisted jeans, with a leather jacket slung over her shoulder,

“Hey, you three! Got any plans for the weekend?” she asked, casually leaning against Zack’s desk.

Zack shrugged, exchanging a glance with Kaito and Aiko. “Not really. Why?”

Rei ’s grin widened. “Great! Then we’re all going to my dad’s inn. He’s been bugging me to bring some friends over, and I figured you guys could use a break. Besides, it will be plain awesome.”

Zack hesitated, glancing at Kaito, who looked equally unsure. “I don’t know,Rei. I was thinking about just lying low this weekend.”

“Lying low?” Rei  scoffed. “Come on, Zack. You’ve been acting all weird lately, and it’s time you unwind. My dad’s inn is in the mountains—it’s quiet, relaxing, and the food’s amazing. Trust me, it’s just what you need.”

Zack sighed, knowing that Rei  wasn’t going to take no for an answer. He glanced at Aiko, who gave a small nod. “Alright, fine. We’ll go.”

Kaito spoke up “S…Sure, why not? I could use a change of scenery.”

Rei  clapped her hands together, her grin widening. “Awesome! We’ll leave on friday afternoon. Pack light, but don’t forget your swims there is a hot spring there.”
