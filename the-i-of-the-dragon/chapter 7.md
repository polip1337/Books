Chapter 7

Zack stood panting in the courtyard, the last wisps of smoke from the charred remains of the Voidstalker dissipating into the night air. His Armor of Flame was still burning, casting a reddish hue on the stone path beneath his feet. He disabled his skills. He defeated the raptor.

But the real challenge was standing right in front of him.

“Kaito, seriously, you didn’t see anything,” Zack said, wiping the sweat from his brow with the back of his hand.

Kaito, wide-eyed and pale, was trembling like a leaf. He was gesticulating wildly, as if trying to physically piece together what he’d just witnessed. “D…Didn’t see anything? Zack, you just fought a m…monster! A freaking monster! With claws! And teeth! And…and fire!”

“Shh! Keep it down, will you?” Zack hissed, looking around. “It wasn’t a monster. It was, uh…” Zack scrambled for an explanation “It was a…really big dog.”

“A dog?” Kaito’s voice cracked with disbelief. “A dog, Zack? You’re telling me that thing was a dog? That thing was taller than I am! And it had scales! And it tried to eat you!”

“Yeah, but you know how some people have really exotic pets,” Zack countered, now fully committed to the lie. “It’s probably one of those rare, um… scaley breeds. You know, like a…dragonhound.”

“A d…dragonhound? You’re not even trying!” Kaito’s hands flailed, his glasses slipping down his nose. “I know what I saw, Z…Zack. That thing was like a raptor from Jurassic Park! It had claws that could slice through metal and you were—” Kaito mimicked throwing punches with exaggerated movements.

Zack groaned inwardly. He needed to change tactics before Kaito started doing a play-by-play for the entire campus. “Okay, okay, I get it. It was weird, alright? But maybe your brain just, you know, exaggerated things a little. Ever heard of mass hysteria?”

“M…Mass hysteria?” Kaito looked at Zack like he’d just suggested the moon was made of cheese. “Zack, I’m the only one here! Who else is hysterical?”

Zack opened his mouth to reply, but before he could, the heavy metal doors to the courtyard swung open, and Rei  strolled out, earbuds in and swagger in her step. She looked at Zack and Kaito with mild curiosity, one eyebrow quirked up.

“What’s with all the yelling?”Rei asked, popping out an earbud.

“The fuse box blew up,” one of the students nearby said. It didn't seem like he overheard the exchange between Zack and Kaito. “Hana is hurt.”

The trio turned together, and saw that students were gathered in a loose cluster, around a girl apparently Hana sitting on the ground.

It was the girl that got attacked by the raptor before. She was clutching her shoulder, and where Zack had expected to see the gashes left by the monster’s claws, there was instead an ugly, red burn. The flesh around it was blistered, with the charred edges that came from a severe electric shock.

Kaito was staring, his mouth slightly open. “That…that wasn’t there before,” he muttered, his voice barely audible over the murmurs of the other students. “There’s no way.”

“I will explain later” Zack took the opportunity “Let’s just…make sure she’s okay first.”

Together withRei they quickly made their way through the crowd. “What the hell happened?” she asked, glancing between Zack and Kaito.

Zack forced a smile, trying to sound calm. “Looks like the fuse box blew.”

He pointed at the box. The metal casing was warped and blackened, with melted wires hanging limply. It looked like it had taken a direct hit from a lightning bolt.

A voice from the crowd called out, “Someone get the nurse! She needs help!”

Rei  jumped to her feet, immediately taking charge. “I’ll go!” she said, sprinting off toward the main building where the nurse’s office was located.

Kaito, still standing behind Zack, seemed to be struggling with the events. “Z…Zack, this is… this is too weird. That w…wound—it shouldn’t look like that. I saw the claws—how does it turn into a burn?”

“Shh man, i will explain everything, lets just go somewhere quiet”

Zack woke up the next morning with bleary eyes. After the previous day’s insanity, all he wanted was a quiet, uneventful day. He had managed to explain everything to Kaito, as promised. Though his friend had taken the news surprisingly well, it had taken most of the night so he didn’t have much time to sleep before his classes.

Kaito had barely said a word during the explanation, he only offered an occasional nod or a “hmm” as Zack recounted his encounters with the Voidstalkers, his powers, and Aiko’s role as his guardian. By the end of it, Kaito had simply stared at Zack, wide-eyed, and said, “So…you’re like a real-life anime protagonist.”

“Something like that,” Zack had replied with a weary smile. The night had ended with an awkward silence, Kaito’s brain clearly working overtime to process the new reality.

Now, as Zack got dressed he remembered he got some notifications after the fight with the raptor, at the time dealing with Kaito came first so he didn’t really check them out. He focused on his core and read the notifications.

Devouring Fire has reached level 5.
Scorching Aura has reached level 3.
You have reached level 6.
You have unlocked a new skill Flame Talon at level 1

Zack immediately checked the description:

Flame Talon level 1

Channel your spirit energy into his hand, summoning a blade of pure flame that extends from your fist. With each strike, the dagger burns hotter, dealing 5.5% increased piercing and fire damage up to 55% at 10 consecutive strikes. Getting hit resets this bonus.

He activated the skill. The energy inside him surged, rushing from his core to his arm. Flames erupted around his hand, spiraling and twisting like two intertwining snakes. As the flames coalesced, they began to take shape—elongating and sharpening, the blaze narrowing into a fine, dagger-like form.

Zack marveled at it for a moment, it reminded him of something he saw in a Diablo game, a katar. It was a push dagger with a wide blade extending over his knuckles for about 6 inches. He tried it with a quick slash through the air, and the flames followed the motion in a blazing arc, the heat distorting the space around it.

He grinned, another fucking awesome addition to his arsenal. If he had these when he fought the raptor that fight would be over way sooner.

He dismissed the blade and got ready to go to the university, he was expecting to be a regular student for once. His hopes were dashed as soon as he entered the lecture hall.

There, sitting near the back, was Aiko.

Zack froze in the doorway, blinking in disbelief. After seeing her in battle, she looked out of place in a classroom setting. The other students were giving her curious glances, clearly wondering who the new girl was.

Aiko caught Zack’s eye and gave him a subtle nod, her expression neutral but her eyes conveying a clear message: We need to talk.

Zack dropped into an empty seat near the front, ignoring the curious looks from his classmates. He glanced to his side, where Kaito was trying, and failing not to keep staring at Aiko. His eyes were locked on Aiko like she was the final boss and he had zero HP left.

The class dragged on, with Zack’s mind wandering constantly back to Aiko’s presence. Why was she here? Did it have something to do with yesterday?

When the bell finally rang, signaling the start of a short break, Zack practically bolted out of his seat. He waited by the door for Aiko to join him, casting an anxious glance at Kaito, who was still sitting at his desk, looking like he was trying to figure out how to breathe.

“Zack,” Aiko said as she approached him, her voice calm and composed as ever. “We need to talk. Now.”

Zack nodded, and the two of them found a quiet corner of the courtyard where they could talk without being overheard. Aiko wasted no time, turning to Zack with a worried expression.

“You fought a Voidstalker again yesterday?” She asked.

Zack looked at Aiko, his brow furrowing. “Is that why you’re here?”

Aiko sighed, glancing around to make sure no one was within earshot. “Yes. My detector showed a Voidstalker appeared, but it disappeared before I got here. It wasn't hard to connect the dots from there.”

“You were worried for me” Zack teased with a smile.

“Zack! This is serious” She said with a stern expression, she did blush however. ”Until you learn to conceal your energy, I need to stay close to you. That’s why I’ve enrolled in your classes. I’ll be here until I’m sure you can handle things on your own.”

Zack blinked. “You’re sticking around just to watch over me?”

Aiko gave him a small smile, looking genuinely entertained. “It’s my duty as your guardian. I wouldn’t be doing my job if I let you get yourself killed, now would I?”

Zack chuckled, scratching the back of his head. “Guess not. Thanks, Aiko. I appreciate it.”

They stood in silence for a moment before Zack continued, scratching the back of his head.

“I need to tell you something else…”

Aiko’s brows furrowed “What is it?”

“I told Kaito about everything,” Zack admitted.

“Why did you tell him?” she asked, her voice low but sharp. “You know how dangerous it is to involve others in this.”

Zack sighed.“I didn’t have a choice. Kaito saw the Voidstalker, Aiko. He saw everything. There was no way I could convince him it wasn’t real. And believe me I tried”. It was Zack’s turn to blush when he remembered the dragonhound.

Aiko’s gaze softened slightly. “I see. If he saw the Voidstalker, then you had no other choice.” She turned back to Zack. “But that doesn’t change the fact that this complicates things.”

“I know,” Zack replied, his shoulders slumping. “I didn’t want to drag Kaito into this, but he was persistent and he would have made more trouble if i didn’t tell him anything.”

Aiko nodded, pressing her lips together. “I understand. If Kaito has seen one, it means he has enough spiritual power to be at risk as well.”

“Good that you’re here then, isn’t it?” Zack grinned at the girl.

“Yeah I'm glad too,” Aiko let out a small laugh.

“Guess it's time to introduce you to Kaito, officially then” Zack grabbed her hand and led her to his other friend.

As they approached Kaito’s eyes widened slightly, and he was visibly starting to panic.

“This is Aiko, the friend I told you about.” Zack introduced the girl.

Kaito simply stood there, his mouth opening and closing as if he were trying to find the right words but failing miserably.

“H-hi,” Kaito finally managed to stammer, his voice cracking slightly. His eyes were wide with awe as he stared at Aiko, who, to her credit, maintained her composure despite the awkwardness of the situation. “I’m, uh, Kaito. Nice to meet you.”

Aiko offered him a warm smile, extending her hand. “It’s nice to meet you too, Kaito. Zack’s told me a lot about you.”

Kaito stared at her hand for a while before he remembered he was supposed to extend his own. “Y-yeah? I mean, uh, really? What did he say?”

“He said that you’re really smart,” Aiko replied, her voice easy and warm.

Aiko’s smile remained steady as she gently shook Kaito’s hand, though Zack could sense a hint of amusement in her eyes. Meanwhile, Kaito looked like he was trying to come up with something to say, but the gears in his brain seemed to be stuck. Zack was pretty sure his friend’s mind had short-circuited the moment he touched Aiko’s hand.

“Smart? I—I guess…” Kaito muttered, clearly flustered by the compliment. He glanced at Aiko, his gaze flicking nervously between her and the ground. “Uh, so, Aiko… uh, where did you, um… how did you… I mean, what do you, uh…”

Aiko’s smile broadened, and Zack could see she was trying her best not to laugh. “You can ask me anything, Kaito. Don’t be shy.”

Kaito took a deep breath, visibly trying to compose himself. “Right! Okay, uh… I just… um… what classes are you, uh, taking?”

“Well, the same ones as Zack,” Aiko said, with a slight tilt of her head. “And you I guess. But im mostly here to make sure Zack doesn’t get into more trouble.”

“Trouble?” Kaito echoed, his voice rising an octave. He shot a panicked look at Zack. “You’re in trouble?”

Zack quickly raised his hands in a calming gesture. “No, no, nothing like that! Aiko’s just keeping an eye on me, you know, to make sure I don’t accidentally blow up the campus or something.”

Kaito blinked, then turned back to Aiko. “Blow up… the campus?”

“It’s just a joke,” Aiko said quickly, though her tone was light and teasing. “Zack’s not actually going to blow anything up. I’m just here to help him, that’s all. Zack told me you know about the …”

“Y… yeah, the, um, stuff … Zack explained it to me yesterday. He mentioned you have some sort of locator.” Kaito seemed to have regained some confidence when technology got mentioned. “Think I could take a look at it?”

Aiko thought for a second. “As long as you promise you won’t break it, I'd hate to have to requisition for a new one.”

Kaito’s eyes lit up at the mention of Aiko’s locator device, clearly interested by the technology. “Oh, I wouldn’t break it,” he assured her, his words coming out in a rush. “I’m just really curious about how it works. Is it some kind of advanced tracking system? Does it use electromagnetic waves or something more… mystical?”

Aiko chuckled, clearly amused by Kaito’s sudden burst of enthusiasm. “It’s a bit of both, actually. But I can show you later if you’d like.”

“Maybe you could tell us about it over lunch?” Zack interrupted, his tone light. He could see Kaito was about to launch into a lengthy scientific explanation, and as much as he enjoyed his friend’s enthusiasm, he was pretty sure they’d lose Aiko if they didn’t rein it in a bit.

“Lunch sounds good,” Aiko agreed, giving Zack a grateful smile.

With that, Zack led the way to the cafeteria, grateful for the distraction. He could sense that Aiko was doing her best to put Kaito at ease, and while his friend was clearly still smitten, at least he was talking now instead of just staring.
