Chapter 25: The Guardian
They spent time they didn't have preparing.
Zack pulled back to the intact room two streets away and went through everything he'd seen in the chamber, piece by piece, until he had a picture of it that felt reliable. The secondary entrance. The large Voidstalker and its function. The drifters and their orbits. The two still ones near the platform. The crystal's altered pulse.
Nyx listened and occasionally corrected him when his memory of a detail didn't match hers. They were better at this together than either of them was alone, which was something he'd noticed gradually and now simply accepted as fact.
The plan they arrived at wasn't elegant. There wasn't an elegant version. The chamber was what it was, and what it was didn't leave room for elegant.
He'd go in through the secondary entrance this time — smaller, less likely to be watched from the inside. Nyx would handle the drifters that couldn't be avoided, her shields intercepting rather than her fire, keeping the heat signature low. He'd use Smothered Flame for anything requiring contact. They'd work toward the platform incrementally, not in a straight line, letting the drifters' orbits carry them out of the path before moving again.
The large Voidstalker near the crystal was the variable they couldn't fully plan for. They both knew it. They agreed not to approach it directly unless it initiated contact, and to deal with it if it did.
It was a plan with gaps in it. Zack had stopped expecting otherwise.

The secondary entrance was a narrow split in the chamber wall, low enough that he had to angle his shoulders to get through. Nyx slipped in above him, soundless, and was already oriented by the time he straightened up on the other side.
The chamber from this angle was different. He could see the platform more directly, the crystal's rotation visible from a new perspective — the double-pyramid form catching its own light on the downward turn, the corruption moving through it in the way he'd memorized. The original frequency underneath it, steady and patient.
The large Voidstalker was still there. Still beneath the crystal. Still doing whatever it was doing.
He moved along the wall.
The first drifter crossed his path eight meters in and Nyx's shield materialized between them — small, precisely placed, deflecting the tendril that had begun to extend without the creature registering what it had touched. The drifter swung away on its arc. Zack held still for the count he'd established and then kept moving.
The second required Smothered Flame. A quick, tight strike, the creature folding before it could vocalize. He killed the flame and pressed on.
The third he avoided entirely, reading its orbit correctly for once and timing his movement through the gap before it closed.
The platform was five meters away.
He stopped.
The large Voidstalker hadn't moved from its position directly below the crystal. Its back was to him — if back was the right word for something that didn't have obvious facing — its tendrils extended upward, the dense corruption lines in its body cycling brighter and dimmer in rhythm with the crystal's altered pulse. Up close, the size of it was more apparent. Half again as large as any Voidstalker he'd fought in the lower city. The lines of corruption through it weren't the diffuse glow of something recently affected. They were structural, load-bearing, the corruption as much a part of its form as the translucent body it ran through.
This one hadn't been changed by the crystal. It had been made by it. Or made for it. The distinction felt important and he didn't have time to work out why.
He looked at the crystal directly above it.
At this distance the original frequency was visible without trying. A deep, clear pulse running through the heart of the crystal, blue-white and steady, the color of something that had been made with intention. The corruption moved around it in layers, the violet-black churning at the surface, thinning toward the center where the original light still held. The soldier's words. Frost on glass.
He took one more step toward the platform.
The large Voidstalker turned.
Not slowly, not with the gradual reorientation of the drifters. It turned the way something turns when it has been aware of you for longer than you realized and has decided it is done pretending otherwise. Its orb eyes, larger than any he'd seen, caught the crystal's light and held it, the violet-black running through them in the same churning currents as the crystal above.
Then it moved toward him.
He stood his ground.
Not because he'd decided to. Because something in the way it moved told him that retreating would be the wrong choice — that this creature had been stationed here, had been waiting here, and that running would resolve its uncertainty about him in the direction he didn't want.
It stopped two meters away.
The pressure of it was immense up close — not physical, though that was part of it, the sheer mass of the thing. Something else. The crystal's pulse hammered through the air between them, faster now, responding to the proximity. He felt it in his teeth, behind his eyes, at the base of his skull.
The large Voidstalker made a sound.
Not the keening of the jellyfish-types he'd fought. Not the resonant hum of their coordinated movement. This was layered — multiple frequencies running simultaneously, some too low to hear and some too high, the audible range between them resolving into something that landed in Zack's mind as almost-language.
Not the crystal-chime of the Icemen. Something that had learned from it secondhand, imperfectly, the way a copy of a copy loses resolution.
Nyx went very still on his shoulder.
Zack, she said.
"I hear it."
It's not attacking.
"Not yet."
The layered sound continued, rising and falling. He couldn't understand it the way he'd understood the Icemen — whatever mechanism allowed that wasn't operating here, the connection too degraded, too far from the original. But the shape of it was communicative. The creature was doing something with sound that wasn't a threat display and wasn't a call for backup.
He didn't know what it was doing.
Then the drifters in the chamber changed direction.
All of them simultaneously, their orbits collapsing inward, the five remaining Voidstalkers converging toward the platform in a tightening spiral. Not attacking — the same directionless drift, just redirected, flowing toward the crystal the way water flows toward a drain.
The large one hadn't signaled them. Or it had, and Zack hadn't seen it.
He looked at the crystal. The corruption was moving faster now, the churning intensified, the violet-black currents accelerating. The original frequency underneath was harder to see — not gone, but pressured, the clean pulse compressed beneath the weight of everything on top of it.
It's feeding them, Nyx said. Her voice was tight. The crystal is feeding them. That's what it does now — it generates them. They aren't coming from outside.
The dying priest. It call them. Not attracting. Generating. Manufacturing.
The large Voidstalker raised its tendrils.
Zack lit both fists.
Not Smothered Flame — the real thing, Armor of Flame at full output, the blades extending from his knuckles, the heat washing outward in a wave that pushed back against the chamber's oppressive cold. The drifters reacted instantly, their spiral collapsing into something more agitated, their tendrils snapping toward the heat source.
He ignored them and charged the large one.
It was faster than he'd expected. The tendrils came down in a wide arc, and he slid under them using Inferno Step, the burst of speed carrying him inside its reach, and drove both blades into the dense corruption lines running up its torso.
The creature's layered sound became something else entirely.
He felt the impact travel up his arms, the corruption resisting his fire in a way the other Voidstalkers hadn't — not dampening it exactly, more like absorbing it, taking the heat and cycling it back through the lines in its body. His flames bit in and held, but the damage was slower. He was going to have to work for this.
He pulled back before the counterswing landed, the tendril passing close enough to stir the air beside his head. Inferno Step again, repositioning to the creature's left, and struck at the same lines from a different angle. The corruption brightened where his fire touched it, flaring violet-black before settling.
The drifters, Nyx called. She was moving, intercepting with shields, buying him fractions of seconds. Three coming from your right.
He didn't look. He trusted her and kept his attention on the large one.
The chamber's geometry was the only thing working in his favor. The platform was elevated, the space around it tight, and the large Voidstalker's size became a liability in close quarters — it couldn't bring its full reach to bear without the risk of striking the platform itself. He used that, keeping himself in the angles it couldn't cover, making it turn to track him, forcing it to work against its own bulk.
The corruption lines in its body were dimming where he'd struck. Slowly, but dimming.
He hit the same spots again.
Nyx's shield took a tendril strike that would have caught him across the back. He heard the barrier crack. That one's done, she said, and he could hear the strain in it. I have two more.
He filed that away and kept moving.
The large Voidstalker changed tactics. Instead of striking at him directly it pressed forward, using its mass to crowd him back toward the chamber wall, the drifters closing from either side in a pincer that was too coordinated to be coincidence. The crystal's pulse hammered the air, three beats gone now, just a continuous accelerating rhythm that pressed against his skull.
His back touched the wall.
He looked up.
The crystal was directly above the platform, three meters off the ground, rotating in its slow contaminated arc. The corruption churning through it, the original frequency compressed underneath. The large Voidstalker advancing, the drifters flanking.
He had about two seconds.
He used one of them to look at the chamber's ceiling, tracing the angle, doing the geometry fast and badly. Then he used Inferno Step not toward the creature but upward — angled off the wall behind him, pushing off the ice with both feet, the burst of speed carrying him up and over the large Voidstalker's reach in a trajectory that was either going to work or wasn't.
It worked.
He came down on the far side, stumbled, caught himself, and turned.
The large Voidstalker had followed his movement with its body but not its feet — the turn had been too fast, and for one moment it was facing the wrong direction, its tendrils extended toward where he'd been. The drifters were still converging from the flanking position, now behind it.
He hit the damaged corruption lines in its back with everything he had left.
The fire went in deep this time. The creature's layered sound collapsed into a single frequency, high and sustained, and the corruption lines in its body flickered — brightened wildly, then went dark. It swayed. He struck again, the same spot, and the lines went dark for longer this time, the body's coherence losing something essential.
The large Voidstalker fell.
Not loudly. Not with the dramatic final motion of something that had been enormous and powerful. It simply lost the organizing principle that had been holding it together and came apart, the corruption dissipating first, then the translucent body beneath, dissolving in the same gradual way the smaller ones did when their cores were disrupted.
The drifters stopped.
Their orbits collapsed all at once, the five of them hanging motionless for a moment in the chamber's glow. Then, one by one, they began to disperse — not toward Zack, not toward each other, just outward, losing direction, the organizing signal from the large one gone. Within thirty seconds they had drifted through the chamber walls and were gone.
Zack stood in the empty chamber and breathed.
His arms ached. His legs ached. The Armor of Flame dimmed and went out, and he let it, standing in the crystal's light without any fire of his own to compete with it.
The crystal rotated above the platform. The corruption still churned. The original frequency still pulsed underneath, patient, unchanged by everything that had just happened. The chamber was quiet for the first time since he'd entered the tower.
Nyx landed on the platform's edge and looked up at the crystal for a long moment.
Still wrong, she said. But the ones it was feeding through are gone. It might be quieter now. Easier to hear underneath.
Zack climbed onto the platform and stood below the crystal. This close, the pulse was almost overwhelming — the compression of the original frequency beneath the corruption felt physical, a weight pressing down from above, a light pressing up from below.
He reached toward it.
Not yet, Nyx said.
He stopped. His hand hovered a foot below the crystal's lowest point, the corrupted light washing over his skin in shifting violet-black patterns.
"I know," he said. He didn't pull his hand back immediately. He held it there, close enough to feel the resonance against his palm, the original frequency distinct now that the room was quiet and the large Voidstalker's interference was gone. Deep and clean and still recognizably itself despite everything that had been done to it.
He pulled his hand back.
"We're not taking it," he said. "Not like this."
Nyx watched him from the platform's edge. She didn't say anything. She didn't need to.
He stood in the chamber with the crystal rotating above him, its corruption churning, its original light holding, the silence of the room filling in around them like water filling a space. Outside, somewhere, Rei was running out of time. The tower's foundation carried the fracture lines outward into the levels below. The Ice Men still fought their endless losing battle in the streets beneath his feet, unaware that the thing they were protecting was the source of what was killing them.
He looked at Nyx.
"Tell me what you hear," he said. "Everything. From the beginning."
She held his gaze for a moment, then looked back up at the crystal. And she began to talk.
