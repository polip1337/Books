
Chapter 18
Zack’s boots crunched against the icy street, his breath fogging in the frigid air as he and Nyx headed for the hut where they rested before. The hunt had been relentless. Zack lost count of how many he killed. 
You have reached level 10.
“Getting good at this,” Nyx had said, perched on a broken pillar after the last kill, her tail swishing smugly. “Almost didn’t need me that time.”
“Almost,” Zack replied, wiping ichor from his knuckles. “Don’t get too comfortable up there.”
They’d pushed on, the blue glow of the ice formations guiding them through streets. Hours blurred together, the cold seeping into his bones, until exhaustion forced a break. Once they backtracked to the hut, Zack slumped onto the table, the faint heat soothing his sore limbs, while Nyx perched by the window, amber eyes scanning the dark.
“Rest up, hero,” she muttered. “You’re no use to me dead on your feet.”
“They way you say ‘hero’ it does not make me think you’re saying ‘hero’”

“I’m not saying anything”

“Fine, thinking then”
“Someone has to do that here.”
He managed a tired grin, closing his eyes. “Wake me if anything moves.”
Sleep came fast, dreamless this time, the weight of the Icemen’s past lingering but distant. When he woke, Nyx’s tail flicked against his cheek, a sharp prod. “Up. My turn now.”
Zack rubbed his face, sitting up. He glanced at her, perched there with her sleek black scales catching the faint light, her wings folded neatly, their red streaks glowing softly. It hit him how fast they’d clicked. From the first crack of her egg in that damp cavern, dripping with sarcasm as she shook off shell fragments, they’d fallen into step like they’d known each other forever. 
Zack kept guard as he thought about their relationship. They’d gone from strangers to a team in days, her sass and courage pulling him through the Void’s horrors. He trusted her implicitly, and that trust had grown so naturally it felt like she’d hatched knowing him, her presence as vital as the flames he wielded. Shaking his head with a faint smile, he pushed off the table—she’d probably call him a sap if he said any of this out loud.

He never felt like petting a lizard, but he had to suppress the urge now. Nyx would kill him with words for that. Her tiny curved horns and whip-like tail—never still, never calm—were just so…unfairly cute.

A rustle of wings broke his thoughts. Nyx stretched, her scales rippling like liquid shadow. “Stop staring,” she muttered, one amber eye cracking open.

Zack coughed, jerking his gaze away. “Wasn’t staring. Just…plotting our next move.”

“Plotting. Right.” She launched herself onto his shoulder, tail flicking his ear. “Your ‘plots’ usually involve charging headfirst into walls. Let’s try subtlety today. The tower’s upper levels won’t raid themselves.”

He snorted, but his hand rose instinctively to steady her as she balanced. “Subtlety? You’ve been flaring your wings for dramatic effect every time we fight.”

“Drama wards off existential dread,” she said airily, claws kneading his collarbone. “I’m doing you a favor. We need to head to the next floor. The staircase—it’ll have to be tucked along the outer walls. Because—”

“—we saw nothing going up when we hunted before,” Zack finished, grinning. 

She gave him a toothy smile. “See? Not entirely hopeless.”

After Nyx’s beauty sleep, they left the hut. The dragon fluttered ahead, her wings cutting through the air as Zack followed, until she paused mid-flight, circling over a wide street. 

“There,” she called down. “Ice is worn from footsteps. Let's try following this road.”

It led them towards one of the walls. After a couple of minutes Zack heard sounds of fighting. They were getting louder. 
Zack crouched behind a wall of ice. Judging from the noise there was indeed a small battle going on ahead. He quickly cast a glance over the wall. There was an entrance carved into the tower’s side, glowing with pale blue light. It was their way to the next level, a step closer to the Essence Crystal. But the path wasn’t clear. A group of Icemen clashed with Jellyfish Voidstalkers in a melee, blocking the route entirely. Around ten on each side. Ice spears flew, shattering against writhing tendrils. 
Nyx perched on his shoulder, her tail flicking against his neck. “Well, this is a mess. What’s the genius plan, oh fearless leader?”
Zack peered around the edge again, keeping low. The Icemen fought with precision, their fissures pulsing as they hurled shards. The Voidstalkers countered with erratic lunges, tendrils snapping like whips. Neither side seemed to notice him yet. “We let them kill each other,” he whispered, pulling back. “Wait it out, then slip through.”
Nyx tilted her head, amber eyes gleaming. “Truly, Sun Tzu would be proud.”
He smirked despite the tension. “It’s called tactics, smartass. They’re distracted. We sneak by while they’re busy.”
She snorted, a puff of smoke curling from her nostrils. Zack scanned the scene again. The fight was chaotic but contained, a tight knot of violence centered fifty feet from the entrance. If he stuck to the shadows and moved fast, he could skirt the edge without drawing attention. The risk was high, but charging in wasn’t an option—there were too many enemies.
Nyx fluttered her wings “I’m just going to fly above.”
Zack nodded and took a steadying breath. He crept forward, boots pressing lightly against the icy ground. The faint crunch beneath his soles sounded deafening in his ears, but the roars and crashes ahead drowned it out.
The street sloped upward, littered with chunks of ice and dark ichor. Zack moved along the left wall, hugging the shadows cast by a row of ruined buildings. Their walls leaned precariously, offering cover but threatening collapse. He kept his eyes on the fight. An Iceman drove a spear through a Voidstalker’s core, only for another to lash out, tendrils wrapping around its arm and yanking it off balance. The chaos was perfect—so long as it stayed focused on itself.
Nyx said in his head as she circled above. “You’re doing okay so far,” she muttered. “Circle that next building on the left. The right is blocked by rubble.”
“Noted,” Zack nodded, knowing Nyx would see the gesture. He eased past a fallen slab of ice. The entrance was closer—sixty feet now. He could see the faint outline of a tunnel beyond, its walls shimmering with the same light  he’d seen before. His heart thudded, adrenaline and hope pushing him forward.
Thirty feet. The fight shifted slightly, an Iceman stumbling back as a Voidstalker’s tendrils slashed its chest. Zack froze, flattening against the wall. The creature’s glowing fissures dimmed, but it retaliated with a barrage of shards that forced the Voidstalker to retreat. They were still blind to him.
“Almost there,” he thought. He edged along, stepping over a puddle of ichor that glistened in the dim light. The tunnel loomed ahead, its mouth wide and inviting.
Zack nodded, his focus narrowing. Five feet. He was parallel to the fight now, the clash of ice and flesh a constant roar in his ears. He kept his pace slow, deliberate, every muscle taut. The tunnel’s edge was within reach—just a few more steps.
Then the ground shifted.
A faint tremor ran through the ice, subtle but enough to make Zack pause. He glanced down, expecting a crack or loose stone. Nothing. The fight continued, unabated. He shook it off and took another step.
The ambush came fast.
An Iceman erupted from a hollow in the wall, its arm swinging down with a spear of ice. It had been still, blending seamlessly with the ruins, waiting. Zack twisted aside as the spear grazed his shoulder, shattering against the ground in a spray of shards. Pain flared, sharp and cold, but he bit back a yell.
“Dammit!” he hissed, stumbling back. The Iceman stepped forward, towering over him, its glowing fissures pulsing with corrupted light. Its featureless face turned toward him, shards forming on its forearm – a cry for help, perhaps?”
“Move!”
Zack dodged as the Iceman hurled the shards, the air hissing with their passage. He rolled, coming up in a crouch. The creature advanced, its movements slow but deliberate, each step cracking the ice beneath it. He couldn’t outrun it—not without Inferno Step, and that would be like a signal flare. He weaved between strikes, each shard a near miss. One clipped his leg, drawing a thin line of blood that froze instantly in the cold.
“What now?” he grunted, ducking another throw.
“Hit it till it dies, genius!” Nyx shot back. “Or stand there —your call!”
The Iceman raised both arms, shards swirling into a deadly cloud. Zack saw a gap—a heartbeat where it paused to aim. He lunged, aiming for its chest, but it swung its arm, forcing him back. The cloud launched, and he dove aside, the shards embedding into the wall behind him. Zack slipped on frozen ichor and fell down. The Iceman stepped closer, its fissures getting brighter.
“Zack!” Nyx’s voice sharpened, panic breaking through. She dove, glowing faintly. A shield flashed in front of him, small but firm, intercepting the next spear mid-flight. The barrier cracked but held, giving him a second to scramble up.
“Thanks,” he rasped, getting up. He summoned Armor of Flame, fire surging into daggers around his fists. The heat steadied him as he ducked a strike and drove a blade into its chest fissure. Ice sprayed, sizzling against the flames, and the creature groaned, its form shuddering.
He yanked the blade free as the Iceman swung. It wasn’t done—shards flew faster, desperate. “Finish it!” Nyx barked, hovering above. Zack surged forward, Inferno Step igniting in a burst of speed. He landed behind it, slamming both fists into its back. Flames roared, shattering its crystalline form, and it collapsed with a final groan.
Zack staggered back, chest heaving, fire flickering out. The tunnel was steps away—he could still make it. Nyx landed on his shoulder, panting. “Saved your bacon again,” she said, smirking. “You owe me a castle now. At least a hoard.”
“Yeah, sure,” he muttered, catching his breath. “Let’s—”

A low sound cut through the silence. Zack froze, eyes snapping to the fight. The Icemen and Voidstalkers noticed him, their gazes—fissures and orbs—turning toward him. 
“Oh, great,” Nyx whispered, wings twitching.
The combatants shifted, breaking formation. An Iceman raised an arm, ice forming a spear. Zack stood exposed, the tunnel teasingly close.
“Run?” Nyx asked, half-teasing, half-urgent.
“Run,” Zack agreed, bolting for the tunnel as the first spear flew.
