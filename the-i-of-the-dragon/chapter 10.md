Chapter 10

"There's something I need to tell you," she began, her voice concerned. "About the Voidstalkers."

"Voidstalkers aren't always mindless beasts," Aiko continued, her blue eyes locking onto Zack's. "Most of the time, yes, they're driven by hunger and the primal urge to destroy. But sometimes, if they survive long enough, they start to develop intelligence. It's rare, but it happens. They become cunning, capable of planning, and worst of all, they learn how to disguise themselves."

Zack's eyes widened in disbelief. "You're saying the 'tanuki' we saw earlier was a Voidstalker?"

Aiko nodded slowly. "I think so. When we were there I felt something. At first, I thought it was just the usual residual energy from a place like that—after all, people have been leaving offerings there for years, and those kinds of rituals have an actual effect. But then I realized it was different. It tried to scare us with the coins but when it sensed my aura, it ran."

Zack frowned, trying to process this new information. "So, what does that mean for us? If it's a Voidstalker, shouldn't we be able to sense it? And if it's intelligent, why would it be playing tricks instead of attacking?"

Aiko sighed, crossing her arms as she leaned against the wall. "Voidstalkers with intelligence are unpredictable. They don't always attack outright, especially if they're trying to blend in. They might be testing us, trying to figure out what we know, or they could be trying to lure us into a trap. The fact that it ran away when it sensed my aura means it knows who we are and what we can do."

Zack frowned "So, what do we do? Do we go after it?"

Aiko shook her head. "Not yet. We don't have enough information, and it's too dangerous to go hunting for something we can't even detect properly. For now, we should focus on trying to learn more."

Zack nodded in agreement, though a part of him felt uneasy about leaving something so dangerous unchecked. "Fine. But if that thing shows up again, we're taking it down."

With that, they made their way to their rooms. Zack entered the one he was sharing with Kaito to find his friend already fast asleep, sprawled out on the futon. Kaito's soft snores filled the room. He never got back to the hot springs, Zack would have to fill him in on the tanuki in the morning.

He quietly changed into his sleepwear, trying not to disturb Kaito. As he lay down on his own futon, Zack couldn't fall asleep. His mind kept going back to the tanuki, or rather, the Voidstalker that Aiko suspected it to be. The idea of an intelligent Voidstalker lurking nearby was unsettling, to say the least. He rolled the old coin in his fingers, maybe it was a clue?

However before he figured anything out, exhaustion won out, and Zack drifted into a fitful sleep, his dreams haunted by foxes and rustling leaves.

The next morning, Zack was awakened by the sound of birds chirping outside the window and the smell of breakfast. He groggily opened his eyes to find Kaito already up and dressed.

"M…Morning, Zack!" Kaito greeted him with a wide grin. "You slept like a log. I t..tried to wake you up earlier, but you were dead to the world."

Zack rubbed his eyes, trying to shake off the sleepiness. "Yeah, I guess I was more tired than I thought." He glanced around the room, noticing the neatly folded futon and the sunlight streaming through the window. "What time is it?"

"Almost eight," Kaito replied. "Rei and Aiko are already downstairs. They're having breakfast with Rei's dad."

Zack nodded, getting up and quickly dressing. He felt a pang of guilt for not being more vigilant last night, but he pushed it aside. "Alright, i need to talk to you Kaito."

Zack filled his friend in on the events of last night and Aiko's suspicions. Kaito's cheerfulness was crushed so badly Zack felt a bit guilty.

"D..Damn, monsters everywhere" Kaito said, dejected "I thought this would be a nice weekend"

"I get it, man," Zack said, "I feel the same way"

The two of them made their way down to the dining area, where they found Rei, Aiko, and Rei's father sitting around a low table, enjoying a traditional Japanese breakfast.

"Good morning!" Rei called out, waving them over. "Come join us! My dad made extra."

Zack smiled, grateful for the warm welcome. He and Kaito sat down at the table, and they all dug into the meal. It felt different than dinner last night, they were all subdued, everyone's mind on the strange encounter.

Aiko was quiet, her focus shifting between her food and the window that looked out onto the forested mountainside. Zack suspected she was keeping an eye out for anything unusual.

When breakfast was finished, they all thanked Rei's father and helped clear the table. Rei gave them a wink and announced that she wanted to take them on a hike to a nearby waterfall that was supposed to be beautiful this time of year. Aiko was immediately on board, so Zack and Kaito followed their lead.

As they gathered their things, Aiko pulled Zack aside. "This is a good opportunity to take a look around the mountain," she said quietly. "I still don't know exactly what we're dealing with, and we might learn something."

Zack nodded. "Got it. I'll keep my eyes open."

Aiko gave him a small smile, but there was worry in her eyes. "Thanks, Zack."

Even though it was an excuse the hike to the waterfall was as beautiful as Rei had promised. The path wound through the dense forest, the trees towering overhead and the air filled with the scent of pine and earth. Sunlight filtered through the leaves, casting shadows on the ground.

Rei led the way, she seemed to know the forest like the back of her hand, and her joy at being back in her element was evident in every step she took. It was as if she put the tanuki out of her mind completely.

Aiko stayed close to Rei, she was tense, her gaze occasionally flicking to the trees around them, searching for any signs of danger.

Zack, though enjoying the hike, remained vigilant. He kept his senses on high alert, trying to detect anything out of the ordinary. But the forest was peaceful, the only sounds being the rustling of leaves and the distant calls of birds.

After about an hour of hiking, they reached the waterfall. It was a stunning sight—water cascaded down from a rocky cliff, crashing into a clear pool below. The sunlight caught the mist rising from the water, creating a rainbow that arched over the falls.

"This place is amazing," Kaito said, awestruck as he took in the view. The Voidstalkers momentarily forgotten.

Rei beamed with pride. "I knew you'd like it. My dad used to bring me here all the time when I was a kid. We'd have picnics by the water and play in the pool. It's one of my favorite places in the world."

Aiko smiled, though her eyes were still scanning the area. "It's beautiful. I kind of envy you for your childhood spent here."

They spent some time exploring the area around the waterfall, with Rei showing them a small cave hidden behind the falls. The cave was shallow, as Rei told it, it was a perfect hiding spot for children to play in.

Eventually, Rei suggested they head back to the inn for lunch. They agreed, and began the trek back down the trail. Zack couldn't decide if he was relieved or disappointed they didn't find any clues about the Voidstalker. But as they walked, Zack noticed that Rei was becoming increasingly distracted. She kept glancing over her shoulder, her brow furrowed in thought.

"Rei, are you okay?" Zack asked, concerned.

Rei hesitated before nodding. "Yeah, I'm fine. I just… I thought I heard something back there. Probably just my imagination."

Aiko exchanged a worried glance with Zack, but said nothing.

As they continued down the trail, Rei's behavior became more erratic. She started to lag behind, looking in the direction of the waterfall. Zack and Aiko tried to keep her engaged in conversation, but it was clear that something was bothering her.

Finally, when they were about halfway back to the inn, Rei stopped in her tracks, her eyes wide with alarm.

"Did you hear that?" she whispered.

The others froze, listening intently. For a moment, there was nothing but the sound of the wind rustling through the trees. But then, faintly, they heard it—a low, laughing sound coming from deep within the forest.

Zack's pulse spiked as he scanned the surrounding trees, he instinctively reached for his core but did not enable his skills yet. "Everyone stay close," he said, his voice tense.

But before they could react, Rei suddenly turned and bolted into the forest, running towards the waterfall.

"Rei, wait!" Zack shouted, but she was already gone, with a speed that was clearly beyond a human.

Without thinking, Zack and Aiko took off after her, with Kaito following behind. The forest seemed to close in around them as they ran. The laughter grew louder, more menacing, as they got closer to the waterfal.

"Rei! Stop!" Aiko yelled, her voice tinged with panic. But Rei didn't stop, didn't even seem to hear her. She was moving with so much  speed that even Aiko couldn't catch up to her.

Zack and Kaito followed Aiko, their breaths coming in ragged gasps as they sprinted after Rei.

They burst into a small clearing with the waterfall just in time to see Rei jumping through the water into the small cave behind it. The laughter cut off suddenly.

"NO!" Zack yelled, lunging forward, made it to the shallow cave but it was empty.

Aiko and Kaito reached his side, their faces pale. Aiko checked her locator, but the device emitted only a faint, confused beep.

"Nothing," Aiko muttered, her voice shaking. "I can't detect anything. No Voidstalkers, no energy signatures… nothing."

Kaito, his face contorted with fear, said "What just happened? And what did she mean when she asked if we hear it?"

Zack clenched his fists. "Didn't you hear the laughter?"

"What laughter" Aiko asked. "I didn't hear anything"

"There was a creepy laugh that got louder the closer we got to this cave. Then it cut off when she jumped through the water." Zack looked to Aiko for an explanation

"I never even heard about anything similar." Aiko looked deeply troubled. She shook the device in her hand. "But if my locator isn't picking up anything, it means one of two things: either that thing is masking its presence somehow, or it's already a long distance from here."

"Then we need to figure out another way," Zack said, his voice rough. "We're not leaving this forest without her."

Aiko looked at the two of them, her eyes filled with worry. "We need to be careful about this. This Voidstalker is clearly intelligent, it's going to try to outsmart us."

Zack nodded. "Then let's get to work. We need to find any clues that might tell us where it took her. We start here."

The three of them spread out, carefully examining the area where Rei had disappeared. Zack's was trying to piece together the events of the last few minutes. The laughter, Rei's sudden behavior change.

After a few minutes of searching, Zack felt something vibrate in his pocket. He took out the coin and examined it again. It had a slight but detectable vibration to it. Zack slowly walked around the small cave and the intensity of the vibration changed, using it as a dowsing rod he scanned the ground.

Then he called out "I found something!"

Kaito and Aiko rushed over to where Zack was kneeling. There, partially hidden under a pile of leaves, was a small stone, about the size of a playing card. Its underside was intricately carved. It was covered in strange symbols that matched the ones on the coin. There was no way they could have found it without the help.

"What is that?" Kaito asked, crouching down beside Zack.

Aiko drew a sharp breath as she examined the stone. "It's a warding stone. These are used to protect areas from evil spirits or entities. But this one… it looks like it's been tampered with. The symbols are wrong, like they've been altered to do the opposite."

Zack's stomach dropped. "As in luring something here?"

Aiko shook her head. "I don't think so, but it's hard to tell. It could be what's masking the Voidstalkers presence."

Zack's mind raced as he tried to connect the dots. "If that's true, then did the Voidstalker kidnap Rei?"

Aiko clenched her fists, "We need to find her fast."

Kaito, still crouched beside Zack, looked up at Aiko, his face pale with worry. "So what do we do? How do we find her?"

Aiko bit her lip, her brow furrowed in thought. "If this stone is indeed interfering with my locator, then it's possible that destroying it could help. But it could also be some sort of trap."

Zack activated all his skills, raised his fist and brought it down with all his strength against the carved stone. The impact sent a shockwave through his arm, and for a split second, he feared he triggered the trap. But then, with a sharp crack, the stone shattered into pieces, the symbols crumbling into nothingness. A wave of heat rushed through the cave.

Nothing moved. Zack's breath came in ragged gasps as he stared at the broken fragments, half-expecting something terrible to happen.

For a moment, nothing did.

Then, Aiko's locator emitted a sharp, insistent beep. The sudden sound made them all jump, and Aiko quickly fumbled to check the device. Her eyes widened as she saw the screen. "It's picking up something—a Voidstalker, nearby!"

Zack leapt to his feet "Where?"

The locator's beeping grew louder, more frantic, as they ran deeper into the forest. The trees here were thicker, their branches intertwining to form a natural canopy that blocked out most of the sunlight. The air was thick with the scent of damp earth and the faint, sickly-sweet smell of rot.

As they burst into a clearing, they saw her.

Rei lay unconscious on the ground, her body motionless, surrounded by a circle of small, polished stones. Her face was pale, her breathing shallow.

But that wasn't the worst of it.

Standing just beyond the circle, its body half-hidden in the shadows of the trees, was a creature unlike anything Zack had ever seen. It stood on two legs, it was wearing a simple black and white kimono. What could be seen of its body was covered in sleek, white fur that seemed to shimmer in the dim light. Its eyes were far too large, gleaming with a malevolent intelligence.

It had two small demon-like horns and a black dot in the middle of its forehead. A wide, sinister grin stretched across its snout, exposing rows of sharp teeth.

The creature's eyes locked onto them, its grin widening as it took a step closer to Rei, as if daring them to make a move.

"Rei!" Zack shouted, rushing forward, but Aiko grabbed his arm, stopping him in his tracks.

"Wait! Don't cross the circle!" she warned, her voice urgent. "It's a trap."

Zack stood frozen, staring at Rei's unconscious form, just out of reach. The Demon watched them with those cold, calculating eyes, its grin never faltering. It was toying with them.

Zack's desperation was reaching a breaking point. He couldn't lose Rei—not like this. He had to do something, anything, to save her.

But before he could act, the Voidstalker let out a low, rumbling chuckle that sent a chill down Zack's spine. It raised one clawed hand, and with a slow, deliberate motion, it traced a symbol in the air.

"You're late" its voice like nails on a chalkboard.

The symbol glowed with a dark light, and the ground beneath Rei began to tremble. The stones in the circle started to pulse with energy, the light growing brighter as the ritual intensified.

"No!" Zack shouted, his voice filled with panic. He struggled against Aiko's grip, desperate to reach Rei, but Aiko held him back, her own fear evident in her eyes.

Aiko's mind was racing, but the complexity of the ritual left her stumped. She knew she had to act fast, but without understanding the exact nature of the magic at play, any interference could be catastrophic. The wrong move could kill Rei.

The fox's laughter grew louder, its amusement evident as it watched them struggle. It was savoring the moment, relishing in their helplessness.

And then, the circle of stones flared with a blinding light. The ground shook violently, and a deafening roar filled the clearing, drowning out all other sounds.

Zack, Kaito, and Aiko were thrown backward by the gust of wind that followed.

When the light finally faded and the dust settled, Zack struggled to his feet, his ears ringing from the blast. He blinked, trying to clear his vision, and his heart nearly stopped.

Rei was gone.

The circle of stones lay shattered. Voidstalker stood in the center of the devastation, its grin wider than ever, its eyes gleaming with triumph.

And then, with a final, mocking laugh, the creature turned and vanished into the shadows, leaving nothing but silence in its wake.

Zack stared at the spot where Rei had been, his mind struggling to process what had just happened. She was gone, taken by the Voidstalker, and they had been powerless to stop it.
