Chapter 6

The following morning, Zack awoke with a sense of purpose. The previous week clung to him—powers, monsters, a world hidden beneath the ordinary. According to Aiko, those powers could attract even more danger. He had to learn more, and he already set up his next meeting with Aiko.

The girl also urged him to keep his day to day routine. It would help him build what she called a facade. It was all part of keeping the whole Voidstalker business secret. Not that Zack cared about that but he had no intention of sabotaging his own education.

He dressed quickly, throwing on his favorite hoodie and jeans. As he looked at himself in the mirror, the face staring back didn’t quite match the person he’d become. He shook his head, grabbed his backpack, and headed out the door.

The crisp morning air greeted him as he stepped onto the campus of Tokyo University. The sun was just beginning to climb above the rooftops, casting a golden hue over the buildings. Zack felt good.

Because of all the business with his hospital stay he excused his absence during the first week of his third semester quite easily. He checked the schedule on his phone and headed towards the lecture hall.

When he arrived the place had the usual background of chatter as students filtered in, finding their seats. Zack slipped into his usual spot near the middle, where he had a good view of the front but wasn’t too conspicuous. Today’s class was a continuation of the Japanese mythology course he had been eagerly following, but his mind kept wandering back to Voidstalkers and the new knowledge that the world was not as ordinary as it seemed.

Just as he was about to lose himself in thought, the door to the lecture hall swung open with a loud creak. Heads turned as a figure shuffled in—a boy who seemed too thin, with pale skin and dark circles under his eyes as if he hadn’t slept in days. He wore thick glasses that magnified his large, intense eyes, giving him an owlish appearance.

The boy moved awkwardly, as though he was unsure of where to step or where to look. Zack could hear some students snicker as the boy stumbled slightly, clutching a stack of books close to his chest.

“He’s so weird,,” someone whispered from behind Zack. He didn’t pay much attention, though. He didn’t recognize the boy which meant he must have joined the class recently.

The boy found a seat near the back, far from anyone else. He placed his books on the desk with care, and then slouched into his seat, attempting to become invisible. The boy didn’t fit in, that was clear, but there was something more—something beneath the surface that Zack couldn’t quite put his finger on.

The lecture began, but Zack kept glancing back at the boy. He couldn't shake off the feeling that something was different about that guy. During a break, when the students were allowed to stretch their legs and chat, Zack noticed a group approaching the boy’s desk. He recognized them, they were the rich assholes that coasted through the university thanks to their parents' money. They just wanted a diploma and a cushy job at some company.

They surrounded the boy, blocking him from the view of others.

“Hey, Akatsuki?” one of them sneered, leaning over the boy in a way that was anything but friendly. “You got my essay on the yōkai?”

The boy nodded timidly, pushing his glasses up nervously. “I… I finished the work, yes.”

“Great. Hand it over then” the apparent leader said, snatching the papers from Kaito’s desk. “Write the one we were assigned for philosophy class, I'll pick it up tomorrow.

One of the other members of the pose noticed Zack looking at them. He had a stylish hairstyle and his hair was dyed blonde.

“What are you looking at, you filthy Gaijin? Fuck off and go back home.” He made a shooing motion with his hand.

Zack’s temper flared. He hadn’t planned to interfere, it was not his business. But now that asshole made it his business.

“Or what?” Zack said as he approached the group. The whole group turned to look at him, surprised by the sudden interruption. There were four of them.

“Or i’m going to make you regret ever coming to our country” the blonde guy responded, taking half a step forward.

The leader put his hand on the most aggressive bully’s arm and pulled him back. “No need for all that nasty stuff. Who might you be, friend?” He said with an insincere smile.

“I’m someone who thinks you should pick on someone your own size,” Zack replied, his voice steady. His eyes locked onto theirs, and for a moment, something flickered in the air—a tension that made the leader hesitate.

“We’re not picking on anyone, just had a friendly chat” He turned to the still sitting boy “right, Kaito-kun?”

Kaito nodded and mumbled “Th-that’s correct, Itsuki-san”

“As you can see everything is fine” Itsuki patted Zack’s shoulder. “Unfortunately we have to go now. Kaito-kun we will talk again. And it was a pleasure meeting you personally Zack”

Zack knew the guy skipped on any honorific on purpose, however he wasn’t versed enough in the culture to know whether it was worse than the way Itsuki used -kun to signal Kaito’s lower position.

“Nice to meet you too Itsuki” he decided to respond with the same.

The blonde guy growled but stopped at a shake of Itsuki's head.

“Such fun this is. I look forward to our future conversations” Itsuki turned and left with the essay on yokai still in his hand. The other bullies followed with the blonde shoulder checking Zack.

Zack hid a smirk when the japanese man bounced off of his shoulder.

“Y-you know what's going to happen next?” Kaito stammered, glaring up at Zack even as his hands trembled in his lap.

“What?” Zack said, surprised by the reaction.

“They’re going to come down h-harder on me now. One does not annoy Itsuki Takagi and get off scot free.” the boy explained.

“Then I guess I should stick around till they leave you alone. Im Zack by the way” he introduced himself, holding out a hand.

“Kaito Akatsuki,” the boy replied with a sigh, shaking Zack’s hand tentatively. His grip was surprisingly firm for someone who looked so frail. Kaito’s knee bounced under the desk, his fingers drumming the cover of his textbook—but there was something else about him, something that reminded Zack of Aiko.

“You joined the class recently, right?” Zack asked, though he already knew the answer. He wanted to keep the conversation going, to make Kaito feel at ease.

Kaito nodded. “Yes. I… I’ve transferred from a different university, but…I haven’t seen you here before.”

“Well, I kinda got in a traffic accident and missed the first week of classes” Zack said and scratched the back of his head in embarrassment. “Maybe you could help me catch up?”

Kaito’s eyes brightened slightly. “And we’d have an explanation if you stuck around. Thank you, Zack. I… I appreciate it. ”

The rest of the lecture passed uneventfully, but Zack noticed Kaito glancing over at him every now and then, as if he couldn’t quite believe someone had stood up for him. Zack made a mental note to keep an eye on him. There was no telling when the bullies would return.

As the day went on, Zack ended up spending more time with Kaito. They shared a few more classes, and during the breaks, Zack would strike up conversations, asking Kaito about his interests. He quickly learned that Kaito was considered a genius, particularly when it came to science and technology. His knowledge seemed limitless, but his social skills were severely lacking.

Kaito struggled to maintain eye contact and often mumbled his words, but when he spoke about his passions, his eyes would light up, and he’d become more animated.

“Have you ever heard of the theory that reality might just be a simulation?” Kaito asked Zack during one of their breaks. They were sitting outside on a bench, enjoying the mild afternoon weather.

Zack chuckled. “You mean like in The Matrix? Yeah, I’ve heard of it. Why?”

Kaito’s eyes gleamed with excitement. “Well, there’s this new paper I’ve been reading that argues we might actually be living in a simulated universe. The evidence is mostly theoretical, of course, but it’s fascinating! Imagine if everything we perceive is just data being processed by some higher intelligence.”

Zack smiled, that would explain the rewriting of reality somewhat. “That’s pretty wild. Do you think it’s true?”

Kaito shrugged, fidgeting with nervous energy. “I don’t know. It’s a possibility, though. The universe is full of mysteries we can’t even begin to comprehend. But the idea that there might be something more—something beyond our understanding—is what drives me to keep learning.”

“If you’re so much into tech why are you taking so many humanities classes?” Zack asked.

“Uh… you see… my parents told me to.” The boy answered clearly not agreeing “I need to ‘round up’ my education apparently. These classes are mostly stupid.”

After two seconds Kaito realized what he had said “Oh god, Zack i didn't mean to say that what you’re interested in is stupid or useless. I just meant..”

“It's fine, to each their own” Zack interrupted with a small laugh before Kaito dug an even deeper hole for himself and died of embarrassment.

As they talked, Zack noticed a figure approaching them. It was Rei, Zack’s friend from university. She was the kind of girl who turns heads without trying, though it’s more out of curiosity than admiration. Rei’s athletic build and short-cropped blue hair gave her a no-nonsense vibe that contrasted sharply with how warm and quick to smile she actually was. She spotted Zack and waved, her smile brightening as she reached them.

“Hey, Zack!” Rei called out as she approached. “Who’s your friend?”

“This is Kaito,” Zack replied, introducing them. “He’s in a few of my classes.”

“Nice to meet you, Kaito,” Rei said, offering a hand. Kaito hesitated for a moment before shaking it. He seemed to be intimidated by the confident girl.

“H-hello,” Kaito managed, the word almost swallowed by his own throat.

“So, what were you guys talking about?” Rei asked as she sat down on the bench next to Zack.

“Kaito was just explaining a theory about how we might be living in a simulation,” Zack explained with a grin. “It’s pretty mind-blowing stuff.”

Rei’s leaned forward, genuinely impressed. “Whoa, that sounds intense! You must be really smart, Kaito.”

Kaito blushed again, looking down at his shoes. “I just… like to read a lot,” he mumbled.

Rei laughed, a light and carefree sound. “Well, that’s awesome! I’m more of a hands-on person myself, but it’s cool to meet someone who’s into all that brainy stuff.”

The three of them spent the rest of the break chatting, with Kaito slowly opening up more as Rei and Zack engaged him in conversation. Her lighthearted nature seemed to put him at ease, his stutter fading slightly as he became more comfortable.

Zack realized that he was genuinely enjoying Kaito’s company. The boy might be socially awkward, but he was intelligent, and had a wealth of knowledge that made every conversation with him fascinating.

Later, as they were leaving their last class, Kaito’s expression changed. His eyes darted around searching for something.

“What’s wrong, Kaito?” Zack asked, concerned.

Kaito hesitated, his brow furrowing. “I… I just feel like something’s not right. Like there’s… something watching us.”

Zack went still. He remembered Aiko’s warning from the previous day—how those with strong spirit power could attract the attention of Voidstalkers. Could it be possible that it was happening so quickly?

A familiar presence washed over him—a cold, suffocating sensation that made his skin crawl. He looked around, trying to pinpoint the source, but there was nothing out of the ordinary. The air itself seemed to grow heavier, and he was sure he was not imagining it.

“Zack…” Kaito whispered, his voice trembling. “Do you… do you see that?”

Zack turned to follow Kaito’s gaze, and his breath caught in his throat. At the edge of the courtyard, partially obscured by the shadows of the trees, was a monster. With the signature Voidstalker gray eyes and the extra teeth.

It was lurking just beyond the reach of the light, its gaze fixed on them. The other students walked past, oblivious to the presence of the creature. But Zack could see it, and apparently, so could Kaito.

Again, it seemed unique. This one was shaped like a humanoid raptor, its body lanky and twisted, with long, sinewy limbs that ended in razor-sharp claws. Its skin was a mottled black, slick with an oily sheen that absorbed the light.

Zack rushed the Voidstalker, expecting the monster to do the same. However the malevolent creature picked a different course of action. The monster tore its gaze from Zack and lunged towards the closest student. Its claws slashing toward her with unnatural speed.

The student fell to the ground grasping her arm which was torn open. Just as the Voidstalker raised its claws to deal the final strike Zack delivered a jumping punch to its side. Flames erupted from his hands, searing through the Voidstalker’s flesh with a hiss. The creature screeched in pain, stumbling back as smoke rose from the charred wound.

Zack didn’t let up. He pressed the attack, striking with a series of rapid punches, each one burning the monster just a bit more.

But the Voidstalker wasn’t so easily defeated. Even as Zack’s flames tore through its body, he noticed something disturbing—the wounds were healing almost as quickly as he inflicted them. The charred flesh knitted itself back together, the blackened skin regenerating.

The Voidstalker snarled, its eyes blazing with fury as it launched itself at Zack. This time, it didn’t just slash with its claws—it bit down, its jaws snapping shut where Zack’s head had been moments before. Zack ducked, rolling out of the way with a swift move, and sprang back to his feet, his eyes locked on the creature.

He knew that he needed more power, but fighting and keeping up his skills was too much to handle at the same time. Zack focused, letting the energy inside him flow into his hands, the flames growing hotter, more intense. He could feel the heat surrounding him, his Scorching Aura calming his mind.

Zack clenched his fists, the flames flaring with a brilliant orange-red light. “Let’s see how fast you can heal from this,” he said, his voice firm with determination.

The creature roared and charged again, its claws slashing at Zack with blinding speed. But this time, Zack didn’t dodge. He met the attack head-on, his gauntlets colliding with the Voidstalker’s claws. The impact sent a shockwave, but it was Zack’s Scorching Aura that made the difference. The heat radiating from his body intensified, the flames surging around him in a fiery inferno.

The Voidstalker howled as Zack’s aura enveloped it, the flames consuming its flesh faster than it could regenerate. Its body convulsed, the oily skin bubbling and melting under the heat. Zack didn’t give it a chance to recover. He drove his fist into the creature’s chest, the flames exploding outward in a burst of energy.

Zack stood there, breathing heavily, the flames on his gauntlets slowly dying down. He watched as the remnants of the Voidstalker crumbled to ash, the last of its dark energy dissipating. He flexed his fingers, feeling the warmth of the fire still lingering in his hands. Aiko’s training had paid off—he had not only survived but had defeated a creature that would have torn him apart just a week ago.

“What. The. Fuck.” Kaito said. Not stuttering for once.
