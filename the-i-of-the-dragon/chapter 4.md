Chapter 4

Zack’s eyes opened wide. “I’m going to have a dragon?” He asked, incredulous.

“Have is a bit strong of a word, it will be its own creature. But if you take care of it it will be your most trusted ally. The familiar bond is virtually unbreakable.”

“This. is. So. Fucking. Awesome” Zack couldn’t hold his happiness any longer. He grinned from ear to ear, still looking at the egg.

Aiko watched him with a broad smile, clearly amused. "I'm glad you're excited, Zack."

Zack turned to her, his face glowing with enthusiasm. "I can't believe this is happening. I mean, a dragon! I've always thought they were just myths, and now I'm going to have one as a familiar. It's incredible!"

Aiko nodded. "It is. But remember, it's also a responsibility your familiar will look to you for guidance and strength."

Zack nodded eagerly. A dragon. His own dragon. "I'm ready. I'll do whatever it takes."

Aiko tilted her head, studying him for a moment. "I know you will. Now, let's take the next step. I'll show you how to display your status and skills. This will help you understand your current abilities and track your progress."

"Focus on your core again," she instructed, "but this time, focus on the word ‘status’"

“Gimme a second.” Zack put his egg in his backpack, he didn’t really have a better idea of what to do with it right now. Once it was secure he closed his eyes and concentrated, feeling the familiar warmth. He visualized a glowing screen materializing before him, and when he opened his eyes, there it was—a transparent, holographic interface floating in the air.

"Whoa," he breathed, staring at the display in awe.

"Good," Aiko said, stepping beside him to examine the screen. "This is your status screen. It shows your current level, attributes, and skills. As you train and grow stronger, you'll see these numbers change."

Zack's eyes scanned the information:

Zack scoffed at his Dancing skill level. He thought he was a better dancer.

"These are your base stats," Aiko explained. "They reflect your physical and mental attributes. Your skills show the abilities you've unlocked. You have five active and five passive skill slots. You will unlock new ones as you level"

Zack nodded, absorbing the information. "This is incredible. It's like a real-life RPG."

Aiko chuckled. "In a way, yes. But remember, this is real. Your actions have real consequences."

"I will," Zack promised. He glanced down at the egg, pressing his palm against its warm shell. "I won't let you down."

“Feel free to focus on specific skills you should be able to see more info” Aiko said with a smile, amused at his eagerness.

Armor of Flame: Level 1

Conjure a physical barrier of flames that surrounds you, absorbing damage at the cost of mana. The armor’s coverage and damage to mana conversion rate get better with level and INT.

Scorching Aura: Level 1

Passive Abilities:

Establish a deep, symbiotic bond with your familiar. This link enhances both your abilities and your familiar's, allowing you to share strength and communicate telepathically. You gain 11% of your familiar's stats and the familiar gains 11% of yours. The bond becomes stronger and more intuitive as you grow together.

Devouring Fire: Level 1

Enhance the destructive power of your flames. Fire conjured by you burns hotter and spreads more aggressively, consuming fuel more efficiently. This passive ability increases the potency of all your fire-based skills by 11%

Incandescent Form: Level 1

Your body was changed by magic. Your body is immune to your own fire based skills. Increase resistance to other fire damage sources. Increases your physical skills by 11%

Zack stared at the status screen. Each skill read like something out of a fantasy novel, yet here they were, real and at his fingertips. He flexed his hands, half-expecting sparks to fly.

He turned the Scorching Aura for a second just to feel the power and heat coursing through him.

A DRAGON. He still couldn't believe it. Summoner's Bond would tie them together, sharing stats and strength. He was going to have a dragon companion and he could not stop grinning about it.

Zack's grin faded into something harder. He was no longer just an ordinary person; he didn’t know what he was yet but he would sure figure it out.

“How do the stats work? If I get higher intelligence do I actually get smarter?”

“Well it's not that simple…” Aiko’s explanation was cut short by a beeping sound from her back pocket. She pulled out something looking like a small tablet, but not of any brand that Zack recognized.

“What is it?” He asked.

“There is another Voidstalker in the city, it's in Shibuya again” Aiko looked troubled, like there was something wrong. Well something more than a monster in a city full of people. “ Wait here. Don’t follow me.”

Without another word, Aiko stepped back and raised her hands. Frost formed around her limbs, quickly expanding into a sleek, shimmering ice bodysuit that covered her arms and legs. The ice glinted in the light, casting a bluish hue that made her look dangerous and beautiful. She then summoned a slab of ice beneath her feet, which lifted her off the ground. She soared into the air, swiftly flying towards the city.

Zack watched her go, awestruck by the display of her power. Her silver hair flowed behind her like a banner, and the ice slab glided smoothly through the air, leaving a trail of sparkling frost in its wake. He idly wondered if the public would see her as a helicopter or a cloud.

As the distance between them grew, Zack's initial admiration turned to worry. Something gnawed at him, a sense that something was wrong. He replayed the scene in his mind, noticing the details he had initially overlooked. Aiko hadn't summoned her entire armor, only the parts that covered her arms and legs. And the speed at which she moved and flew was not as fast as the blistering pace she had shown back in Shibuya.

Realization hit him like a punch to the gut—she was still hurt. The injuries from their last battle were slowing her down, she hadn't fully recovered. Despite her brave front, she was vulnerable. That's why she appeared so worried.

"I can't let her face this alone," Zack muttered to himself, clutching the egg tighter. She needed him, and he wasn't going to let her down.

Summoning all the courage he had, Zack grabbed his bag and dashed out of the clearing. He remembered the bike Aiko had originally used to bring them here. He found it parked outside, he hopped on, the egg still safely in his backpack. He turned the key, and the engine roared to life.

The streets blurred around him as he sped towards Shibuya. The cityscape passed in a rush of colors and sounds, but Zack had one thought: find Aiko. He weaved through traffic, the bike’s agility allowing him to navigate the crowded streets faster than ever before..

As he neared Shibuya, Zack's anxiety grew. He rode through the neighborhood, his eyes scanning for any sign of Aiko or the Voidstalker.

He turned corner after corner, frustration mounting as he found no trace of the girl. Just as he was about to shout her name, he heard it—the sounds of battle: clashing, growls, and the crackling of ice against the pavement.

Zack sped towards the noise, he rounded another corner and there she was. Aiko stood in the middle of a deserted street, her ice armor fractured and smeared with blood. She was locked in combat with a Voidstalker.

Zack spent a moment taking in the monster. The first thing that came to his mind was a bull, if bulls had a mouth full of sharp teeth and spikes growing from their backs. It has the similar look of malice in its glowing gray eye’s as the previous Voidstalker. However it looked .. younger? Its skin was not as gnarly.

The demon bull lowered its head and charged Aiko. It became clear that Zack was right, her movements were slower, her strikes less precise. She managed to dodge but her breathing was labored. Blood trickled down her face, and she was barely holding her own. Each charge attack from the Voidstalker was threatening to finish the fight..

"Aiko!" Zack shouted.

Aiko glanced at him, her eyes widening in surprise. "Zack, no! Stay back!"

But Zack couldn't obey. He couldn't stand by. He sat his backpack with the egg gently behind a car, ensuring it was safe, and stepped forward.

The Voidstalker huffed, its attention momentarily shifting to Zack. Aiko took the opportunity to strike, throwing an summoned icicle, but the creature barely staggered. The attack was nowhere near what Zack knew the girl to be capable of.

"Get out of here!" Aiko yelled, backing up with a limp. “It's not like your Awakening!”

"No," Zack replied, noticing the large wound on her leg. He stepped closer. "I'm not leaving you."

He focused on his core, feeling the familiar warmth ignite within him. His hands began to glow with fiery energy, and he summoned his Armor of Flame, currently in the shape of gloves covering his hands. The flames hummed with power, dancing eagerly. He also triggered Scorching Aura, he felt he needed every advantage in this fight.

The Voidstalker charged again, this time at Zack. He sidestepped at the last moment and punched the monster's side as it was rushing by him. Skin met fire and the creature huffed in pain as its flesh got seared. Unlike the last time he fought, the attack seemed to do little else. The burns quickly healed as the monster prepared another charge.

“No! Zack! You don’t understand, you’ve got no…” Aiko's warning was cut off as the monster opened its mouth. Zack didn't know what he expected, but it was not a translucent wave that rushed toward him. He had no idea what it was, but dodging seemed prudent.

He jumped to the side, but the wave expanded, catching him just before he escaped its reach. Just before it touched Zack he felt a slight pull forward instantly followed by a hammer of force that threw him back.

He landed hard, scrambling to his feet as quickly as he could. He glanced at Aiko, who seemed unharmed, but her mouth was moving without sound. The only thing Zack could hear was a loud ringing. He felt blood trickling from his ears.

You have been deafened.

That made sense, the wave had been a sonic attack. Zack shook his head, refocusing on the monster. It seemed momentarily disoriented by its own attack, giving Zack a crucial moment to recover.

Zack rushed the monster. If the thing had ranged attacks he needed to keep the distance short where he could at least respond. Staying far away he could just get hit over and over.

He got close in and delivered a solid punch to the side of its head, igniting its skin. The flames quickly extinguished, causing minimal damage.

Shit, I'd have to hit it a hundred times to bring it down.

Zack backed off slightly, thinking fast. The bull shook its massive head, recovering from the punch, its gray bottomless eyes locking onto Zack with renewed fury. The brief disorientation from its own sonic attack was gone, and the creature seemed even more enraged now.

Zack needed to stall for time, to figure out a plan. He couldn’t just keep hammering away at the beast—he needed a strategy. The bull scraped its hooves against the ground, preparing to charge yet again.

He darted to the side just as the bull lunged forward, narrowly avoiding its deadly horns. Zack felt the wind rush past him as the beast barreled through the space he’d just vacated, its hooves thundering against the ground. The bull's speed and power were terrifying, but they could be used against it.

Zack circled around, forcing the bull to turn to keep him in its sights. Each time the monster adjusted, Zack made sure never to stand in front of it, moving in quick, unpredictable patterns. The bull charged again, but Zack was already moving, dodging to the side with a burst of speed.

Zack glanced around the street, looking for anything he could use to his advantage. His eyes settled on a large van. If he could time it right, maybe he could use the bull’s own momentum against it.

The bull turned, snorting angrily, and Zack taunted it with a quick wave of his hand. “Come on, big guy, let’s see what you’ve got!”

The bull snorted again, and predictably rushed Zack who sprinted towards the van. At the last possible moment, he darted to the side, and the bull couldn’t slow down in time. Its massive body collided with the vehicle, the impact shaking the ground.

The Van flew into the building behind it with a crash that Zack could feel in his bones. The bull didn’t seem fazed in the slightest.

Don’t get hit. Noted.

Zack backed off again, breathing hard. He could see cracks forming in the facade of the building from the force of the van’s impact. The creature had both power and defense but maybe if he could…

Zack circled around again, playing for time. He dodged, always staying just ahead of the enraged beast, using the terrain to his advantage. The bull crashed into another car, breaking it in half.

But Zack knew he couldn't keep this up forever. He hoped his plan would work out or he’d run out of energy before the bull did. He could stall for now, but he needed a way to turn the tables—and soon.

He pushed himself to his absolute limit trying to occupy the monster just for a couple more seconds. And a couple more after that. And once he got so far what was another dodge?

He was breathing heavily, barely able to hold up his arms. His muscles were burning and he could only think about collapsing to the ground. The monster charged at him again and Zack more fell down out of the way than dodged. Then all his efforts paid off.

A two yard-long spike of ice rose from the ground in front of the bull like a spear with one end lodged against the ground. The bull couldn’t fight its own momentum and impaled itself on the weapon. The end of the icicle exited through its back, then it fell to the side, already dead.

Finally Zack thought. He knew it was Aiko who got back into the fight. He noticed that earlier she was saving her spells. She must have been out of mana, whether it was from the flight here or her previous wound he didn’t know.

He managed to roll over onto his back staring at the sky. Aiko rushed into his field of vision. She leaned over him and her silver hair brushed against his face, they came back red. She was checking him over for wounds and pulled a vial with a golden liquid, she forced his mouth open.

You are no longer Deafened.

You are Regenerating.

The sound came back with a vengeance, Zack could hear the sirens of the emergency vehicles that rushed to the spot of the battle. He looked at Aiko?

“Another car crash?” He asked with a half grin.

“You idiot! I was so worried about you!” She hugged him on the ground. She smelled of blueberries and blood. “What were you thinking rushing that Voidstalker you couldn’t even damage it. You're not ready”

“Kinda realized that after the fact” he said with a weak smile “but i figured out you needed mana and time, so i kind of distracted it.”

Aiko’s blue eyes grew even wider. “ You’re the only person I know who would fight in that situation. I was certain you would die before I could help you.”

“It turned out okay,” Zack said, trying to get up.

She pushed him back down and with a shake of her head said. “It turned out okay he says. You're either very brave or very stupid.”

“I hope it's the former,” Zack groaned.

“Yeah me too.” She said under her voice, and then louder “I'll go get your egg, in the meantime you might want to check your status.

Zack nodded and focused on the information.

You have reached level 2.
You have reached level 3.

You have reached level 5.

There were a lot more notifications.
