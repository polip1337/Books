Chapter 21: The Rot at the Heart
The first thing Zack noticed was the quiet.
Not peaceful quiet. The level below had been a battlefield — Ice Men and Voidstalkers grinding against each other in the dark, the constant percussion of it rising through the walls. Up here, none of that reached him. The silence sat differently, the kind that comes after something is finished.
He moved slowly, keeping to the outer wall. The residential district — what was left of it — stretched ahead in a wide arc, the ceiling lost somewhere in the dark above. The columns that supported it were massive, the same pale ice as everything else in the tower, but here they had fractured differently. Not from battle. The cracks in them radiated outward in patterns that had nothing to do with impact, more like pressure from within, like something had pressed against the tower's bones from the inside and not quite broken them.
The floor was wrong too.
He'd noticed it in the first seconds after climbing out of the shaft — the fracture lines he'd seen on the floor below, radiating from a point deeper in. Down below, the Ice Men's city had shown damage that made sense. Gates forced inward. Walls smashed through. The evidence of a siege. Up here the damage pointed the other way, as if whatever had happened had originated from the center of this level and moved out. He kept that in the back of his mind and kept walking.
You're doing that thing, Nyx said. Where you go quiet and start thinking too hard.
"I'm allowed to think."
You're allowed to think while also telling me what you're thinking so I can tell you you're wrong.
He almost smiled. "The damage up here. It's different from below."
Different how.
"Below, the Voidstalkers broke in. The blast patterns, the gate damage — it all pointed inward. Up here it points outward. From somewhere ahead." He paused at a corner, checking the street before moving. Empty. "Whatever corrupted the crystal, it didn't come through the walls."
Nyx was quiet for a moment, her claws adjusting on his shoulder. So it came from inside.
"Or it already was inside."
The structures around him had the shape of homes without the contents. He'd seen the craftsman's hut on the level below — its tools, its furs, the small figurines lined on the shelf. He'd understood what it was because of what was in it. Up here the walls stood in more places than they didn't, but the insides were empty. Not emptied — empty, as if the level had been built but never properly inhabited, or as if whatever had been here had dissolved along with the people who owned it.
He thought about the ice dust. Every Iceman he'd seen fall — in the dream, in the temple — had become that same fine powder. How long had this level been sitting in silence. He didn't let himself finish the thought.
There, Nyx said.
He stopped.
The glow he'd seen from the shaft — the violet-black pulse that had lit the underside of the ceiling like a bruise — was stronger now. It came from ahead and to the left, through the remains of what might have been a wider street or a plaza. The pulse moved in and out on a rhythm that had nothing to do with regular intervals. Three beats close together. A long pause. Two beats. Another pause. It was biological without being alive, the rhythm of something that had forgotten what it was supposed to be doing but kept doing it anyway.
He moved toward it carefully, staying in the narrower alleys, using the standing walls for cover.
The Jellyfish Voidstalkers appeared before the chamber did.
Three of them drifted through the open space ahead, their translucent bodies catching the crystal's glow and bending it, the violet light moving through them like light through clouded glass. They weren't patrolling. They weren't hunting. They were drifting, their tendrils trailing below them, their movements slow and directionless.
He'd fought dozens of their kind on the levels below. He knew their heat signature, knew the way they moved when they were about to strike. These ones weren't doing any of that. They moved the way things move when there's nowhere they need to be.
They came from in there, Nyx said, her voice quieter than usual. Look at the floor near the entrance.
He looked. The fracture lines on the floor were densest around a collapsed section of wall ahead — the outer wall of a larger structure, caved in from the inside. The Voidstalkers drifted in and out through the gap without touching the edges. The floor around the opening was darker than the rest, stained with something that wasn't ichor.
Zack. Nyx's voice had a quality he hadn't heard from her before. Not fear exactly. Wariness. I can hear it.
"The crystal?"
It's not... it doesn't sound right. I don't know how else to say it. When I was in the egg, before I hatched, I could feel things. Warmth. Resonance. Like the echo of something larger. A pause. This sounds like that. But it's been playing the wrong notes for a long time and it doesn't know it anymore.
He processed that. She'd never mentioned sensing anything like this before — it made a strange kind of sense, that a creature bonded to him at the most fundamental level might be sensitive to something the Icemen had built their entire civilization around. He didn't push for more details. She'd given him what she had.
"How bad?"
I don't know. I can hear the crystal underneath it. The original thing is still in there. But what's on top of it is...  She trailed off.  We can't just take it. If we try to take it in this state I don't know what happens.
He stood at the edge of the alley, watching the Voidstalkers drift. The chamber was maybe forty meters ahead. Through the gap in the collapsed wall, the glow pulsed again — three quick beats, the long pause, two beats. He could see the underside of a vaulted ceiling lit from below, shadows moving across it in patterns that didn't match anything drifting in the room.
One of the Jellyfish turned, its orb eyes catching the light. Zack went still. It drifted for another few seconds, then turned away, continuing its directionless path.
He catalogued what he knew.
The corruption hadn't come from outside. The Voidstalkers in that room weren't acting like the ones he'd fought — they weren't hunting, weren't defending. They were moving like they'd been generated there. The priest in the temple below had said the crystal was calling them. Not attracting them from a distance. Calling.
And underneath all of it, according to Nyx, the original thing was still there. Still the crystal the Ice Men had built a tower to protect. Still, somewhere in all that violet-black wrongness, what Rei needed.
He pulled back from the alley's edge and turned to Nyx, keeping his voice low. "We're not going in yet."
She looked at him.
"We don't know enough. If we go in there and set off the wrong thing —" He stopped, aware he was thinking aloud. "We need a way to approach it that doesn't announce us to everything in that room."
Nyx's tail flicked. So we scout.
"We scout."
Finally, she said. A plan that doesn't start with charging directly at the dangerous thing. I'm going to mark this day.
"Don't."
Too late. It's marked.
He almost told her to shut up, but the truth was her voice in the back of his head was the only thing keeping the weight of this place from settling too fully on his shoulders. He let it go.
They pulled back further into the ruined streets, putting distance between themselves and the chamber. The glow still reached them here, the pulse still visible as it lit the distant ceiling. Three beats. Silence. Two beats.
Zack kept his back to a standing wall and thought about what they'd learned.
The Ice Men hadn't lost because the Voidstalkers were stronger. His dream had shown him that much. They'd lost because the Voidstalkers kept coming. Endless, adaptive, relentless. And the thing driving them — the thing the dying priest had gestured toward with the word corruption — had been here the whole time, nested inside the thing the Ice Men were trying to protect.
He looked at his hands. The aura was still there, the ember of it waiting at his core. Still functional, still his. Whatever was in that chamber, it hadn't reached this far.
You're doing it again, Nyx said.
"I'm still thinking."
I know. I can feel it. She shifted on his shoulder, wings settling. So can I, for what it's worth.
He looked at her.
Feel the wrongness. From here, it's faint. But it's there. Her amber eyes were steady. Whatever that thing is doing to this place, it's not done. It's still spreading.
He'd seen the fracture lines on the floor. He'd seen the way the damage radiated outward. He thought about the tower's foundation, the levels below, the Ice Men still locked in their endless fight down there without understanding that the thing they were protecting might be the source of what was killing them.
"Then we can't wait long," he said.
No, Nyx agreed. We can't.
The crystal pulsed again in the distance, three quick beats, the long terrible pause, two more. The glow reached the edge of the alley and lit the ground at their feet in violet-black, then faded.
Zack studied the approach, measuring distances, counting the Voidstalkers he'd seen, thinking about the collapsed wall and what angles it offered. Nyx watched with him, silent now, her presence grounding him in the specific and the real.
The chamber could wait a few more minutes. They were going to need every advantage they could find when they went in.
