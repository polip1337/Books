
Chapter 16
Zack’s boots echoed against the icy floor as he staggered forward, the cold inside the tower not helping his frozen limbs. Nyx perched on his shoulder, her breathing heavy. The blue light from the walls gave the space an otherworldly glow, but the scene ahead quickly reminded Zack they were far from safe.
Ruined buildings sprawled out before them, their jagged, opaque ice walls shimmering faintly. The structures looked like they had been crafted by hand, molded into sturdy, utilitarian designs—houses, workshops, even a tower-like structure that reached to the ceiling two dozen meters above them. But now, most were in ruins, their walls cracked and sagging.
Zack froze at the sound of heavy, rhythmic footsteps echoing down a distant street. Nyx’s claws tightened slightly on his shoulder. "Patrol," she whispered into his mind. 
They ducked into the nearest alley, Zack pressing his back against the icy wall as the vibrations in the ground grew stronger. Through a crack in the wall, he saw them—a trio of Ice Men, their glowing fissures pulsing like heartbeats. Their heads turned slowly, scanning the area.
Zack held his breath as they passed, the cold radiating off them making his teeth ache even from a distance. Nyx’s tail curled tightly around his neck as she perched closer, her body tense and still.
When the patrol was out of sight, Nyx let out a slow breath. "We can’t fight them.”
Zack nodded, his body still pressed against the wall. "I know. We’ll have to sneak through."
The two moved deeper into the city, avoiding open streets and sticking to the shadows of the ruined buildings. Zack found himself hyperaware of every sound—the crunch of frost beneath his boots, the faint hum of magic in the air, the distant roars coming from the distance. Each time they heard the footsteps of another patrol, they ducked out of sight, holding their breaths until the danger passed.
Zack approached the nearest building cautiously. The doorway was wide open, and within, he saw the remains of a battle. The corpse of an Ice Man lay sprawled in the center of the room, its crystalline body shattered. Nearby the remains of a couple, hard to say exactly how many, Voidstalkers were strewn about.
“They’ve been at each other’s throats,” Zack muttered, stepping back into the street. His breath fogged in the cold air “But why? What’s even here to fight over?”
Nyx fluttered off his shoulder, hovering a few feet in the air. “I don’t think the ‘why’ of it matters much to us… only that we’re in the middle of it.”
The further they went, the fewer Jellyfish corpses they encountered. At first, there were clusters—entire groups of the creatures, their bodies crushed or riddled with holes—but gradually, their presence thinned. Same was true for Ice Men corpses
“The fighting’s been heavier near the entrance,” Zack observed, pausing at an intersection. A single Ice Man lay scattered on the ground. He still flickered faintly, its glow pulsing weakly as if trying to hold onto the last remnants of its life force.
Nyx landed beside him, her gaze lingering on the dying creature. “He looks… wrong, corrupted.” 
“Yeah the glow seems like a disease” Zack pressed forward, his steps careful. The buildings around them began to change and they were less damaged. These were clearly adapted for living: doors carved into the ice, furniture molded seamlessly from the same material, and faint carvings on the walls that looked like an abstract script.
“This… is their town,” Zack said quietly, running his hand along the wall of one of the intact buildings. 
Nyx perched on his shoulder again, her tail curling lightly around his neck. “Makes sense. These things weren’t just mindless brutes. Look around—everything’s too orderly, too deliberate. This was a society.”
Zack’s gut twisted as he spotted another Ice Man corpse, this one sprawled across the steps of a larger building. Its massive form blocked the entrance, and its glowing cracks were dim, lifeless. The position of its arms suggested it had been shielding the doorway from something.
“And they’re losing,” Zack murmured.
As they slipped through the streets, Zack felt something click in his head. His movements become just a bit more fluid, his steps more silent. When they avoided another patrol by slipping through a narrow alley, a notification appeared in his mind, startling him.
You’ve learned a new general skill: Sneaking Level 1.
The growth rate is weird, it's very staggered. He didn't develop the skill like a regular human would, it grew in leaps.
The sound of distant crashes interrupted his thoughts. Both Zack and Nyx froze, their eyes snapping toward the source. The noise echoed through the streets, followed by faint, guttural roars and the high-pitched keening Zack now associated with the Jellyfish.
“The fighting’s still going,” Nyx said, her voice tense. She turned her sharp gaze toward Zack. “We shouldn’t be anywhere near it, not now.”
Finally, Zack’s eyes fell on a small hut tucked to the side of a wide, open street. Its structure was plain compared to the buildings around it, but its walls were intact, and the doorway was narrow enough to offer some semblance of safety.
“There,” Zack said, pointing to the hut. “Looks like a good spot to rest.”
Nyx fluttered from his shoulder, landing softly on the wall near the entrance. She scampered along, scanned the doorway and peered inside. After a moment, she gave a small nod. "Clear."
With a cautious step, Zack followed Nyx inside. The space had a cold, sterile feeling. Carvings on the walls depicted abstract patterns—circles and jagged lines that pulsed faintly with light, almost like runes. The floor was smooth ice, untouched by dust.
One object stood out—a large, rounded stone with a mirror-like surface. Unlike everything else, it radiated faint warmth. Zack hesitated before touching it. This wasn’t ice. It was something foreign, something important, but he didn’t know why.
"Beautiful," Zack murmured, brushing his fingers along the ice table in the center of the room. It was carved with swirling etchings of snowflakes and geometric patterns, the light of the runes gave it an eerie sense of movement. Four chairs, sculpted seamlessly from ice, stood beside it, massive enough to accommodate an Ice Man’s towering form. 
Zack inspected the chairs. Each had carvings of Icemen doing different things. Some of them were fighting, some stood in front of large gatherings yet others guarded a gate, similar to the one he used to get in.
Zack stood up and studied the rest of the room. Set into the far wall was an alcove, shaped to fit an Ice Man.  Above it, a mobile of frozen crystals turned slowly. 
“Hey Nyx what do you think this is? A bed? A meditation chamber?”
“Could be a privy for all we know.”Nyx said, watching through the window for any movement. 

Zack chuckled at that, and that made him realise he was really tired. He continued his exploration. Next to the alcove there was a basin of solid ice. Above it, swirling patterns carved into the wall mimicked gusting wind. A long-handled ladle hung from the wall. Zack felt an urge to try to dip the ladle into the basin but controlled himself.
He then inspected a set of tools laid on a table—chisels, knives, and sculpting instruments. Their handles were inlaid with tiny crystals, catching the dim light. This had been a craftsman’s home.
Scattered around were small ice figurines of Ice Men, some holding staffs, others bowls etched with frost patterns. Similar to the one next to the alcove. 
In a corner, thick furs were neatly folded. Unlike animal pelts, these were smooth, like woven frost. Metallic clasps lined the edges, possibly for fastening to Ice Men’s bodies.
 Whoever had lived here had left in haste. They had left behind their tools and unfinished work.
His tired mind turned back to the table. Unlike everything else in the room, it radiated a faint warmth. 
“We’ll rest here for a bit,” Zack said, glancing at Nyx. “Then figure out our next move.”
“You rest. I'll stand guard”

Zack climbed on the table. As he closed his eyes, the questions lingered: What happened here? Why did the Icemen attack him on sight even when they shared the same enemy. He was asleep before he could formulate any more.
Then, the dreams came.
He stood on an expanse of white, the sky above an unbroken sheet of silver. The ground beneath his feet was smooth ice. He looked down. His boots were gone. Bare skin touched the ice, yet no cold reached him.
Figures moved in the distance, their silhouettes sharp yet shifting, like mirages on glass. They walked with a graceful, deliberate pace, their bodies tall and angular. Their arms shimmered with embedded crystals, growing and fracturing in an endless cycle. The sounds they made were not words but the chiming of delicate glass striking together, yet Zack understood them as if they were simple speech.
A group gathered at the center of a great circular plaza, where a single spire of ice rose toward the sky. Each Iceman touched it briefly, closing their eyes. The spire responded with a deep hum, vibrations spreading outward through the ice. It was not just a structure; it was something alive, something aware.
Zack moved closer, drawn in by the rhythm of the place. He saw no chaos here, no rushing, just calm order. Each Iceman had a purpose. Some walked toward the edges of the city, where they traced their fingers along the air.. From the tips of their hands, thin trails of crystal formed, hardening into structures as they worked. Others knelt in alcoves carved into the ice, unmoving. The humming of the spire resonated through them, as if it was speaking to them.
He turned toward one such alcove, stepping cautiously inside. The walls curved gently, enclosing him in silence. An Iceman sat there, motionless, his eyes closed. No corrupted veins marred his form. 
Meditation.
Zack felt it then—the quiet, the weight of nothingness pressing upon him. This was a place where thoughts dissolved, where the self became part of the greater whole. The world outside no longer mattered. Only the hum of the ice remained.
He exhaled, and the sound of his breath was wrong. Too warm. Too human.
The Iceman in the alcove opened his eyes, pale and endless like frozen lakes. He regarded Zack with neither surprise nor curiosity, only understanding.
"You are not of us."
Zack swallowed. "No."
The Iceman reached out, the crystal on his forearms tinkling as they grew and shattered. "Then why do you hear us?"
Zack did not know the answer. He tried to speak, but his own voice felt foreign here. The words did not belong. He looked down at his hands, expecting the familiar sight of his own skin, but instead, faint lines of fire ran beneath the surface, shifting like light under ice. 
The Iceman withdrew his hand.
"You carry fire within. It does not belong here. But you need to see"
The plaza outside pulsed with light, the movements of the Icemen continuing without pause. Zack turned, feeling himself being pulled away, the ice beneath his feet growing softer, melting.
A sharp shift—
A great dark wave rising on the horizon. Jellyfish-like figures popping into existence throughout the Icemen lands. The hum of the spire turned shrill, the Icemen reacting in sudden unity. Crystals formed weapons in their hands, light pulsing in their veins.
Darkness.
The same plaza, but it felt like time had passed. The spire remained, but now it was encased in layers of defensive ice. The Icemen fought, their movements controlled but urgent. The jellyfish-men always returned, more than before, their numbers endless. The hum of the spire faltered.
Another shift—
Icemen constructing a great tower around the spire, moving the main crystal higher with each completed section. They encased it in layers of protection, fortifying against the invaders. More of them meditated in alcoves, their eyes shut tight. A sacrifice of thought, a deepening of the hum.
Flash—
The first attacks had been scattered, manageable. But they learned. The jellyfish-men adapted, attacked where the defenders were weak. The Icemen’s barriers shattered faster than they could be rebuilt. Floor after floor, lost. The jellyfish-men pushed them upward, forcing retreat after retreat. No longer did the Icemen move with grace—now they hurried, the weight of defeat pressing down upon them. The top floor was nearing.
Flash—
A desperate counterattack. The remaining Icemen poured their power into one final rush, their forms dimming as energy drained from their bodies. The air filled with the sound of breaking ice as they pressed forward dying by the hundreds. Their crystalline weapons dulled, their bodies cracking under the strain. The last effort failed.
Flash—
A final stand at the highest point of the tower. The main crystal, once the heart of the city, now isolated at the pinnacle. The remaining Icemen circled it, hands clasped together. The hum of the spire grew deafening, vibrating through Zack’s bones.
A last moment—
The jellyfish-men breached the final barrier. The hum turned into a wail. The light of the crystal pulsed one last time—
Then purple light.
Zack gasped, sitting up on the table. The dream was gone, but he still felt like he just finished fighting a war. A war that he lost.
